## Bottom line

**Does this section faithfully represent the raw data? For the numbers, yes —
emphatically.** I checked ~25 price/trial/benchmark figures digit-by-digit across 20+
raw transcripts and every one is accurate, including the four convergent analogs
(Cal AI $30/$10, Glow $40/$10, Pepai $40/$10, Pingo $99.99) that the "$7.99/$39.99,
annual-only trial" decision rests on, the full Vahe/Claim benchmark stack the funnel
kill-board rests on, and the entire Blue Throne ceiling-test spine. Quality labels
(secondhand for Waking Up, claimed-aggregate for the Parra/Vahe percentages,
self-inconsistent for Sunflower) are applied honestly and match the raws. This is a
faithful section.

**The one thing the blueprint must change: strike or re-source the "Apple pulled
Cal AI" claim (conflict row 21 → blueprint §2 "documented Cal-AI-pull class" and §10
"the Apple-pull class").** It is a fabrication relative to its cited source:

- The cited interview (thebrettway 20260302, read in full) contains **no** pull, ban,
  rejection, guideline, 3.1, divide-down, or decliner-re-prompt content — it is an
  explicit pre-sale **victory lap**.
- It is **chronologically impossible**: a March-2026 interview cannot "reveal" an
  April-2026 event.
- It is **also absent from the intermediate testimony file**, so it was injected at
  the **ledger synthesis stage** — the classic "confident, well-cited, wrong" failure
  the owner commissioned this audit to catch.

Scope of the damage is contained, and the _recommendation_ survives on other grounds:
the aggressive tactics themselves (divide-down toggle, decliner re-prompts, cancel-flow
counter-offers) **are** genuinely documented in the corpus — Vahe demonstrates all of
them (raw #23, l.106-113, 188-205) — and Apple's real 3.1.2 design-guideline exposure
is independently sourced in business-model.md. So the blueprint's "refuse the whole
family" posture is still well-founded. **The fix is narrow: replace "the documented
Cal-AI-pull class" / "one month after Zach's victory lap, Apple pulled Cal AI" with an
honest framing** — e.g. "these tactics (divide-down, decliner re-prompts) are the
standard aggressive-monetization toolkit demonstrated across the corpus (Vahe) and sit
squarely in Apple's 3.1.2 enforcement zone; a gate-everything app has no free tier to
retreat to if pulled." If the April-2026 Cal-AI pull is a real external fact the author
knows, it needs a **real external citation**, not a transcript that doesn't contain it.

**Minor cleanups (optional):** (a) Halo AI's 16.5% — soften to "each of the two
paywalls ~16.5%, combined higher" or verify the intended referent; the current "combo
converts at 16.5%" reads the ambiguous raw one way. (b) "self-admits… 'not lasting'"
was the interviewer's phrase (founder concurred) — drop "self-admits." (c) Darcy is
paid-upfront _plus optional IAP subs_, not strictly "no-subscription." None of these
change any decision.

**One value-add the fresh sweep surfaced (not a defect):** a **value-anchored pricing**
pattern recurs across the corpus — Blue Throne, Alex ("$300/yr, make back $2000"),
Clear 30 ("$55/wk on weed"), Pepai ("already spending on the drugs") all justify price
by an _"you already waste more on X"_ anchor. Crave's onboarding **already ships an
"animated money-wasted graph"** (fact sheet). The blueprint's §2 pre-wall demo and the
week-3 ceiling test would both be stronger if they explicitly tied the price to that
"you waste $Y on bad meals" anchor — it's the corpus's most consistent lever for
holding a higher price, and Crave has the asset built already.
