## Bottom line

**Does the section faithfully represent the raw data?** Overwhelmingly yes on the mechanics and
the numbers — I checked ~34 load-bearing claims and 27 are clean to the digit, with the
arthurspalanzani figures, the Blue-Throne headwind figures (80% YoY, legacy 5-8yr, 0/4-5 founders),
the Cardstock moat, the Cal-AI custom-product-page mechanic, and all three rating-ask sources
verified verbatim. The section is also commendably self-skeptical about echo risk and seller bias.

**What the blueprint must change:**

1. **Strike the fabricated Casper/Payout "Food & Drink far less contested than Finance" claim
   (MAJOR).** It is a quotation of something never said, in the raw or anywhere in the corpus.
   It appears in ledger consensus #5, the numbers table (falsely tagged "first-party opinion"),
   the Crave-transfer ("a positive secondhand signal"), and adversarial-panel Q2. The blueprint §7
   line "Food & Drink category pending the keyword pull" **survives on its own hedge** (it already
   defers to the keyword pull and says "confirm against actual current category competition") — so
   the _decision direction is unaffected_, but the category choice currently rests on a phantom
   secondhand prop that must be removed. After removal, §7's Food & Drink lean has **zero corpus
   evidence** behind it; it is pure Crave-side reasoning and should be labeled as such.

2. **Minor cleanups (no decision impact):** move the portfolio-of-$2k-apps citation from 20260203
   → 20260110; drop 20260203 from the "300 downloads / 15-30 trials" numbers row (0319-only);
   note that the never-repeat-keyword rule is single-source (Arthur), not the 2-source consensus the
   header implies; flag Payout's "#20" as hypothetical, not an actual chart placement; and note the
   "$7k/day" figure is real but from the 20241128 phase, not the $40k/day CPP era.

3. **Two gaps worth surfacing to the panel (absent, but decision-relevant):** (a) the
   trial-event pixel-attribution trap — recurs in two independent raws (Cal AI 20260302, Marcus 20260621) and bites Crave's annual-trial hard paywall on any paid instrument (§6/§8); (b) the
   only in-corpus account of the parked programmatic-SEO play (Dimitri 20260104) warns it is
   structurally decaying post-AI-Overviews — relevant if §11.8 is ever un-parked.

Everything the blueprint §7 asserts other than the Food & Drink evidentiary prop is faithfully
sourced. Fix that one fabrication and correct the minor miscitations, and the section is sound.
