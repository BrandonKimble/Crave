### The two weekly-pricing distortions (claim #23 — expanded)

- **Parra is INVERTED and mis-quoted.** Ledger Conflicts: _"Blue Throne Josh and Jonathan
  Parra both frame weekly as an AI-wrapper churn-and-burn artifact, 'declining for
  social/multiplayer apps'."_ Parra actually says the OPPOSITE for those categories:
  _"especially for social apps and apps that are kind of multiplayer… it seems like weekly
  and monthly have become **a lot more popular**"_ — it's **annual** (year-long commitment)
  that's declining there, not weekly. The word "declining" appears nowhere in Parra's raw.
  Worse, Parra is net **neutral-to-positive** on weekly: _"our users will pay more in the
  weekly plan than if they would have just paid for the annual plan"_ and weekly is _"a good
  way to see whether a user is willing to pay"_ during price testing. He attributes weekly's
  _rise_ to AI-wrapper churn-and-burn but does not condemn it. The ledger promotes a
  mildly-descriptive aside into a fabricated quote with the meaning reversed.
- **Josh is OVERSTATED.** Josh does not call weekly a "churn-and-burn artifact." He says
  weekly _works_ for short-duration categories (dating, ex-stalking apps) and is _fine_ for
  a cash-flow business — _"not everyone is chasing an exit and that's completely fine…
  weekly can give you that because you're getting a lot of your revenue very upfront."_ His
  only knock is that weekly "tends not to be as good of a **long-term/acquisition**
  strategy." That's a category-fit caveat, not a condemnation.
- **Net:** the anti-weekly "consensus" the ledger builds is weaker than presented. It does
  NOT change Crave's decision — Crave is adult / planning / no-urgency / retention-seeking /
  exit-optional, which lands on annual-primary regardless — but the blueprint should rest
  that call on _category fit_, not on a corpus that supposedly disowns weekly. Weekly is a
  legitimate cash-flow + price-testing lever both experts acknowledge.

## Bottom line

**The load-bearing spine of §10 is faithful and the kill board is trustworthy.** Every
number the blueprint §8 kill board depends on — Vahe's install→paid (4/10%), trial-start
(≥15%), trial→paid (≥30%), the 1.1%→4% case, multi-step +20-40%; Josh's 50/40/30/20
resubscribe, 50/35/20 organic, and the top-20%-engagement-curve "oracle"; Zach's 30%
industry-average renewal — checks out **digit-for-digit** against the raw, and the quality
labels (rule-of-thumb / single-operator / aggregated-agency) are honest, not laundered. The
prior agent even correctly de-garbled a caption ("81%" → "8–11%") and appropriately flagged
the Sunflower inconsistency and the Pingo-preliminary caveat.

**What the blueprint should fix (all low-to-zero decision impact, but real):**

1. **Weekly conflict (the one that actually distorts a source).** Rewrite the Josh+Parra
   weekly framing: Parra says weekly is _rising_ for social/multiplayer (the ledger has it
   backwards) and both experts consider weekly a legitimate cash-flow/price-test lever.
   Crave's rejection of weekly is a **category-fit** decision (adult, no-urgency,
   retention-seeking), not a corpus consensus that weekly is bad. §10's discard line is fine;
   the _rationale_ is over-claimed.
2. **Strike Pepai** from the ledger's "apps reporting hard-paywall revenue jumps" list — it
   reports none. (Blueprint already excludes it; only the ledger errs.)
3. **Sunflower range** should read 30–70% (not 46–70) to match the ledger's own Consensus
   line and the raw's lowest metric (+30% trials).
4. **Halo 16.5%** is each paywall individually, not the combined rate (combined is higher);
   and **Stronger** is free-tier-with-gated-pro, not a pure hard paywall — a small
   over-grouping in the §0 "five free→paid flips."
5. **Two omissions to fold in:** Phoenix/Copia is a 5th onboarding-length data point
   ("doubled our conversion"); and Pepai's age-conditional finding means "longer onboarding
   out-converts" is audience-conditional (holds for Crave's adults, breaks for under-25s).

No change is warranted to the §8 kill-board bands, the model decision (§0), or the
renewal-anchor treatment (model on 30%, verify RevenueCat's ~44% before setting kill lines) —
those are the load-bearing calls and they are faithfully sourced.
