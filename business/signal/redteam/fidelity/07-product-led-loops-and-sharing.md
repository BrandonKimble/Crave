## Bottom line

**The section faithfully represents the raw data.** Every blueprint-load-bearing number was
verified digit-for-digit and CONFIRMED: Blue Throne organic bands (§8), Jungle 30–40% / Nomad
10–15% WOM (§8), NGL scale, Hunter 150M MAU + endorsement (§5), Stronger +25% / K-factor-unmeasured,
Quittr $5 flop (§10), Nicole +50% hard-paywall A/B (§0), PushScroll / Payout / comment-farm 2.3%
(§4), Darcy / Lovely / Coconote / Jenni / Discord super-node (§5). The section's central thesis —
that Crave's dinner-table loop is an **artifact** loop that survives gate-everything, so §5's
rich-open-slug call is structurally sound — is well-evidenced and I found nothing in the six fresh
raws that undermines it.

**Two concrete defects to fix in the ledger (neither changes a blueprint call):**

1. **Citation error:** the "very dramatic screenshot" claim (Consensus #2) is cited to
   `superwall--20260623`, which **does not exist**. Correct it to **arthurspalanzani--20260623**
   (Alberto). Content is accurate; only the filename is wrong.
2. **Unsupported number:** Fitted's "~$0.25/download lifetime revenue" (Noise) is not in the raw
   (raw = "1¢ to 15¢" acquisition _cost_). Delete or correct to "1–15¢ CPI." Non-load-bearing.
   Plus two soft notes: Quittr's "pinned comment" is Cal AI's mechanism conflated in (raw = "show
   the app in every video + caption requirements"); and "$1.2M ARR in 7 days" is attributable only
   to the co-cited Methods file, not the Cluely file.

**What the blueprint must change: nothing substantive.** §5, §8, and §4 all rest on confirmed
evidence. Two _optional refinements_ surfaced by the fresh sweep, both in creator-strategy
territory (ledger 05/06, not 07) and neither touching a §5/§8 call this section feeds:

- **§4's flat "DM outreach, not email"** has a credible first-party counterexample (Connor,
  superwall--20251217): email beats DM for _larger_ creators. Consider making it size-dependent.
- **§4's "No agencies, ever" / §10's "equity for the first creator deal"** each have one
  first-party counterexample (Lots' posting agency; Connor's equity model). Both are defensible to
  keep for Crave's single-product bootstrapped shape, but the blueprint currently overstates them as
  clean consensus.
