### Detail on the one real distortion (#7 — the 27M-views figure)

Ledger numbers table: `$2,600 → ~27M views (~$1 CPM) | superwall--20251128 | Cardstock's Noise slideshow run | dashboard shown`.

The three figures are mutually inconsistent by 10×. In the raw, the host does the
arithmetic out loud (L277): **"a dollar CPM, a dollar per thousand views."** At $1 CPM,
$2,600 buys **2.6M** views, not 27M. Independently, Kyle states the Noise pricing model
is $1–2 CPM to the advertiser, ~$0.50 to the creator (L265–266). For $2,600 to buy 27M
views the CPM would be ~$0.10 — flatly contradicting both his stated model and the host's
"a dollar CPM." The garbled dashboard caption "like 27 views" (L276) reads as **"2.7
[million]"**, which is the only value consistent with "$2,600 at ~$1 CPM." So the true
number is ≈ **2.6–2.7M views**.

Where the error entered: the **testimony flagged it** — `testimony/…kyle-fowler…md` L50:
_"27M(?) views ('like 27 views' garbled — paired with the spend it reads as millions)…
≈ $1 per thousand views."_ The testimony kept the "(?)" and even noted the $1-CPM math,
but resolved the garble to 27M (itself inconsistent with the $1 CPM it wrote in the same
sentence). **The ledger then stripped the "(?)" and promoted it to a confident, "dashboard
shown"-labeled figure.** This is exactly the failure mode the audit was told to hunt: an
explicitly-hedged, auto-caption-garbled number de-hedged one stage downstream, and off by
an order of magnitude. Decision-impact is low (the blueprint §4 leans on the _concept_ —
Noise as the retargeting ground game — not this number, and the ledger elsewhere flags
Noise magnitudes as "vendor-claimed, discard magnitudes"), but the figure itself is wrong
and should read ~2.7M, or be dropped.

## Bottom line

**Section 05 faithfully represents the raw data.** 13 of 16 load-bearing claims are
confirmed close to verbatim, including _every_ figure the blueprint §4/§3 actually leans on:
the founder-first consensus (Nomad Table, Yiannis, Kyle, Quittr all check out), the 75–80%-pop
/ ≥70%-floor watch-through gate (Nomad Table, first-party), ~1 viral/month at daily cadence,
the product-as-hero / demo-as-payoff format (Kyle's faceless scan-reveal), slideshows-trending-
out, and Clear30's honest secondhand "10M→$1k" caution. The quality labels ("secondhand,"
"claimed," "first-party analytics," "dashboard shown") are mostly well-calibrated.

**One genuine distortion to fix:** the Cardstock/Noise row **"~27M views"** is ~10× too high —
it should read **≈ 2.7M** (the only value consistent with the stated $2,600 spend at ~$1 CPM),
and the testimony's explicit "(?)" uncertainty flag was stripped when the number was promoted
to a confident "dashboard shown" figure. Low decision-impact (the blueprint doesn't cite the
number) but a clear fidelity defect and precisely the class the audit targets.

**Two minor precision items:** (1) "agency half-life ~6 months" isn't in Kyle's raw and Kyle is
in fact pro-agency-after-learning — so the blueprint's absolute **"No agencies, ever"** is a hair
stronger than the cited evidence; consider softening to "no agencies _before_ founder-learning."
(2) Clear30 are founders, not "ads professionals."

**What the blueprint should change (all optional except the number):**

1. **Fix or drop the 27M figure** → ~2.7M (or omit; it carries no weight).
2. **Soften "No agencies, ever"** to reflect the learn-first-then-hire nuance (Kyle), keeping
   the real rule: no agency in the bootstrap/founder-learning phase.
3. **Two strong, missing proofs worth folding in** because they _strengthen_ existing calls:
   Yiannis's scratch-map intent-A/B (hook intent, not app-presence, drives conversion → sharpens
   the §4 controversy-lane spec) and Jay's explicit endorsement of geo-sequential city expansion
   (→ first-party support for §3's Austin-first-then-dark-bench sequencing).
   Nothing here overturns a §4 or §3 decision; the section is sound.
