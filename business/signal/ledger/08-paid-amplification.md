# 08. Paid Amplification — when and how paid enters

## Where the evidence points

The corpus's modal answer is that paid is a **liquidation layer on top of a proven organic creative machine, not a discovery channel**: build awareness and a creative archive organically, then let direct-response ads convert the warmed audience. That sequencing has the best evidence in the corpus — Cal AI is first-party at scale ($2M/mo influencer ceiling → $5.7M/mo after cracking Meta), and Pepai, Sway, Bloom, and Quittr each independently narrate the same arc with their own numbers. The ads-first dissenters are real but structurally explained: they either had organic fail for identifiable reasons (Glow's audience-product mismatch, Clear30's pain-driven ad-perfect category), sell ads services (Marcus Burke), or use paid only as a $20-per-creative _instrument_ rather than a channel (Yev, Roger Chen) — which is not actually the same position. For Crave the decided constraint (no ad budget) is mostly endorsed by the corpus, with one sharp exception worth planning for now: **Spark/partnership ads with metro geo-targeting are the only mechanism anyone offers that fixes the one-city waste problem of organic reach** — post Austin-hooked organic content, then pay small dollars to aim proven winners at the Austin DMA. That is instrument-grade spend ($20 tests, $50–100/day on winners), compatible in spirit with "no ad budget," and it should be the first paid dollar Crave ever spends. Everything else — $7k–40k/day Meta scaling, January arbitrage, sub-$1 CPMs — presupposes national TAM and a proven LTV that Crave won't have until Austin retention data exists.

## Consensus

1. **Sequence: organic first, paid only after proven creative winners exist.** Cal AI ("organic builds the goodwill, paid ads liquidate it"; paid started month ~4–5 after the influencer engine, "90% of paid ads is the creatives") [thebrettway--20241128, thebrettway--20260302]; Pepai (ads delayed to month 3 "until we had winners"; scripted-from-scratch ad UGC lost money, only organic winners worked) [superwall--20260628]; Sway (organic plateau $30–40k/mo → paid unlocked $2M ARR) [superwall--20260505]; Quittr (influencers to bootstrap, then Meta ads recycling creator videos via contractual usage rights, ~$40k/mo at 3–4x ROAS) [thebrettway--20250210]; Dan Kwan ("don't touch paid until you've cracked at least five winning formats") [superwall--20251026]; Bloom (paid CAC decayed 2023 → organic 2024 → decayed virals recycled as ads) [superwall--20250707]; Vert Ventures ("sparks them into paid") [superwall--20250717]; Halo AI (paid planned only as the post-$300k-MRR lever, seeded with proven UGC) [superwall--20260329]. **8+ sources, several first-party. Echo risk: real but bounded** — Cal AI is the ur-text everyone in this scene quotes, but Pepai/Bloom/Sway ran the arc themselves with independent numbers, including the failure of the reverse order.
2. **As paid creative, direct-response beats subtle integration.** Cal AI's single most expensive lesson: subtle influencer-style cameos capped Meta at ~$10–15k/day; whole-video-about-the-app, selling within 5 seconds, broke to ~$40k/day [thebrettway--20260302]. Corroborated first-party by Sway (hard-sell organic repurposed as ads beat the organics) [superwall--20260505], PushScroll (shameless "full ad mode" out-converted cinematic) [superwall--20251018], StudyFetch (hard URL CTA doesn't hurt when product is the star) [superwall--20260524], and Marcus Burke (app ads must _say_ they're an app or install rate dies) [superwall--20260621]. Quality: strong — multiple first-party operators, opposite of their own organic doctrine, which argues against echo.
3. **Agencies fail for early-stage paid/UGC; founder masters the channel first.** Choi (agency half-life ~6 months) [superwall--20250404]; Cal AI (agency capped at $5k/day, "never care as much") [thebrettway--20260302]; Stronger (incentives never align; agencies only to scale a proven format) [superwall--20250815]; Kyle Fowler (can't vet what you haven't done) [superwall--20251128]; Alexey (hired 4 marketers, all failed) [arthurspalanzani--20260514]; Dmitri (agencies "a terrible time") [superwall--20260104]. **6 sources, the cleanest consensus in this section**, and it cuts _against_ the ecosystem's own commercial interests.
4. **Attribution is structurally broken; workarounds are the playbook.** Custom product pages per ad + ~30% inflation for watch-then-search users [thebrettway--20260302; also superwall--20251217]; spike-correlation over link tracking [thebrettway--20241128, superwall--20250707/Symmetry]; incrementality on/off tests [thebrettway--20260302, superwall--20250623]; platform-reported ≈ half of real purchases [superwall--20250623]. AppsFlyer skepticism: "you have to use one… never really does" [thebrettway--20260302], MMPs too expensive at small scale [arthurspalanzani--20260110], Apple attribution "you can't know" [thebrettway--20250210] — vs. Pepai and Nicole using OneLink happily for creator links [superwall--20260628, superwall--20260511]. 5+ sources on the broken-ness; quality mixed but the _methods_ are first-party.
5. **Paid-as-instrument: tiny spend to test/validate creative, not to acquire.** Yev (platform-minimum behind every organic piece — $20/ad TikTok, ~$1 FB, read directional patterns) [superwall--20250623]; Roger Chen (a few thousand dollars of ads = instant validation in the pre-launch dead zone; "when you're actually scaling, no ads") [superwall--20251206]; Ryan Thorp (L1: $10–20 per video, kill at $5–10 spend) [superwall--20260117]; Hunter Isaacson (a dozen small tests, never one creative) [superwall--20250725]. 4 sources; consistent because it's cheap physics, not fashion.
6. **Spark-ads doctrine** (mostly single-source — Sideshift's Drew, a vendor): test Spark on any post with engagement rate >7.5–8% (elsewhere 10%) with $20; scale $50–100/day for a week; kill when ER decays <5%; sub-$1 CPMs on high-ER sparked content; spark priority = "what app is that?" comment sentiment > ER > views; don't spark a still-climbing organic; sparking lifts the account's later organic; GoWish hit #1 "almost entirely from Spark"; Meta partnership ads generally convert better than Spark; **Spark can target country/age/audiences** [superwall--20260601, spine/05]. Corroboration is thin (Vert Ventures secondhand, Ryan Thorp's spark-from-creator-account), and Drew sells the tooling — treat thresholds as starting grid, not law.

## Conflicts

- **Organic-first (Cal AI school) vs ads-first (Glow school).** Cal AI/Pepai/Sway/Kwan vs Arthur's Glow (ads from day one at €25–30/day; organic produced 2 canceled trials from 15 videos; best creative <$0.10 CPI) [arthurspalanzani--20260110], Clear30 (organic was "an uphill battle" 0→$1k; paid was the flywheel; "10M views and made a thousand bucks") [superwall--20250828], Massive Dan ("you should pay to get that data") [superwall--20250803], Marcus Burke (subscription apps "need Meta ads") [superwall--20260621]. **What it's really about:** (a) whether the product has an organically viral visual (Glow and Clear30 don't; Cal AI's scan does); (b) whether the founder has organic distribution skill; (c) incentives — Burke is an ads consultant, and Glow's "profit" rode $360 of one-time TikTok credits on ~$700 spend. Not a true contradiction: even the ads-first camp uses ads to _find_ what converts, then organic economics decide the ceiling.
- **Ads to validate → organic to scale (Roger Chen) vs organic to validate → ads to scale (Cal AI).** Chen argues ads get more expensive as you scale, organic compounds [superwall--20251206]; Cal AI's arc is the exact inverse. Really about product type: Chen builds K-factor social (product-led scale exists); Cal AI is single-player subscription (no loop, ads are the only scale lever). Crave is single-player — Cal AI's ordering applies — but Chen's cheap-validation use of ads is compatible and useful pre-launch.
- **"TikTok UGC is organic" (everyone) vs "it's paid, just less measurable" (Blue Throne's Josh)** [superwall--20260708]. This is an accounting fight, and the buyer is right: creator retainers, CPM bonuses, and Spark boosts are spend. It matters for Crave's books — the "no ad budget" constraint should be restated as "no cold-audience UA spend," or the creator budget becomes invisible.
- **Trial-start pixel optimization (Cal AI: optimize for trial start, back-model conversion — Apple's 24h signal window forces it) vs Marcus Burke (cheapest cost-per-trial is actively bad: young trials convert worst; bid UP on 45+/65+ despite ~100% higher cost per trial)** [thebrettway--20260302 vs superwall--20260621]. Both first-party. Resolution: optimize the event you can, but weight spend by cohort trial→paid — directly relevant to Crave's annual-with-trial SKU.
- **Apple Search Ads: validation instrument vs dead channel.** Cal AI (bid competitor keywords; profitability = proof your product is better) [thebrettway--20241128]; Arthur's Versy (discovery→exact campaigns, 70% conversion, ASA as keyword/screenshot lab) [arthurspalanzani--20260319] — vs Glow's total failure ($100 credit, wouldn't spend, 4 downloads) [arthurspalanzani--20260110]. Really about search volume in the niche: ASA needs existing query volume; low-volume niche keywords don't spend. Austin-intent queries are low-volume/high-intent — expect Versy's instrument use, not Cal AI's scale use.

## Noise (discard for Crave)

- **Sub-$1 CPM / 2¢–39¢ CPI benchmarks** (Noise $0.08–0.30 installs [superwall--20260125], PrayScreen 39–80¢ [superwall--20250928], Fitted 1–15¢ [superwall--20251121], Glow <$0.10): all national-audience impulse niches, all self-reported, several from vendors. Crave's paid delivery must be geo-constrained to one metro, which structurally raises CPM/CPI; these numbers would poison a Crave model.
- **January seasonality arbitrage** (75% of annual budget in month one, CPI halves) [superwall--20260117]: a health/fitness New-Year artifact. Food discovery has no equivalent resolution spike; also presupposes a banked creative archive and a media buyer.
- **Headway "straight paid to $200–300M/yr"** [superwall--20250803]: secondhand Sensor Tower lore used to justify paid-first; no mechanics, no costs.
- **"Subscription apps need Meta ads" as a launch premise** [superwall--20260621]: consultant selling ads services on a paywall vendor's podcast; his own honest content (age economics, targeting-is-creative) survives, the thesis doesn't.
- **Phone farms, reposter fleets, mass-account Spark of AI slop** [superwall--20251009, 20260521, 20260216]: ToS-violating, ban-prone, and trust-toxic for a credibility product whose moat is objective data.
- **Glow's unit economics as an ads-first proof**: the "profit" depended on one-time platform credits (~half of gross spend) and a garbled ~$1.64/download figure.

## Crave transfer

Concrete leaning: **keep zero cold-audience UA at launch; adopt paid-as-instrument immediately; plan the liquidation layer for later.** Three phases: (1) **Now/pre-launch** — spend Apple's free $100 ASA credit as a keyword instrument on Austin-intent and competitor terms ("best food austin," Yelp/Beli brand terms) per the Versy/Cal AI pattern; treat profitability on a competitor keyword as product validation, and non-spend as a volume fact, not failure. (2) **From first organic content** — run the Sideshift Spark grid scaled down: any Austin-hooked post (own or creator, with Spark/partnership rights contracted up front per Boost App Social's perpetual-rights rule) that clears ~8–10% ER gets a $20 Spark test **geo-targeted at the Austin metro**, $50–100/day while ER holds >5%. This is the corpus's only answer to "UGC can't target one city," and it converts Crave's structural weakness (wasted national reach) into paid-grade targeting on organic-grade creative for hundreds, not thousands, of dollars a month. (3) **Post-retention-data** — only when Austin renewal/retention pins LTV does the Cal AI ladder open: direct-response creative from the proven archive, one CBO scale + one test campaign, no interest targeting but hard geo, trial-start pixel back-modeled, custom product pages per ad with the ~30% search adjustment, incrementality tests over MMP dashboards. Derive the CPI ceiling from Vahe's install→paid band (4–10%) times first-year net — do NOT import Pepai's $5 rule. What changes if X: if TikTok cannot deliver DMA-level targeting at Austin scale, Meta partnership ads (city/radius targeting is native there, and Drew says they convert better) become the entire instrument layer; if a web/Stripe funnel ships (the ~12-point margin swing in the fact sheet), paid math improves materially and web-funnel ad formats (Marcus Burke's latent-vs-induced split) enter earlier.
