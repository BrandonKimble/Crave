# 09. ASO & App Store Surface

## Where the evidence points

This section's corpus has an unusual shape: one practitioner (arthurspalanzani, 8
videos, all selling his own ASO tool and a paid community) supplies most of the
_mechanics_, while a handful of unrelated speakers — Kyle Fowler/Cardstock, the Quittr
team, Zach Yadegari/Cal AI, Josh at Blue Throne — independently corroborate the parts
that matter. The mechanical hygiene (title>subtitle>keyword-field, no duplicate
keywords, keyword-tool validation before building, Product Page Optimization / custom
product pages, a rating ask that feeds ranking) is genuine cross-source consensus, cheap,
and worth doing regardless of category. The _strategic_ core of Arthur's playbook —
arbitraging low-difficulty **countries** via localization — is single-practitioner and
does not transfer to a single-metro product: Crave's addressable keyword pool is
Austin-intent demand, not a globally distributable lifestyle habit. The most important
non-seller voice in the section is Blue Throne's Josh, an app _buyer_ with no ASO
tooling to sell, who independently warns that organic App Store ranking is getting
structurally harder (80% more submissions YoY, legacy apps 5–8 years old own the top
keywords) and that nobody in his daily deal flow has cracked AI search — a sober
counterweight to the arthurspalanzani-ecosystem's implicit "there's always a keyword
gap to find" optimism. Crave's actual opportunity is closer to Cardstock's 2019
"baseball card scanner" gap than to a mature category fight: dish-level food-discovery
keywords ("best birria austin," "where to eat austin," "spicy vegan ramen austin") are
not squarely owned by Yelp/Beli/Google Maps, which are restaurant- not dish-indexed —
but that has to be _validated with a keyword tool before launch_, per the one rule every
credible source in this section agrees on, not assumed from this reasoning alone.

## Consensus (>=2 independent sources)

- **Keyword hierarchy (title > subtitle > keyword field) and never repeating a keyword
  across fields.** arthurspalanzani (`arthurspalanzani--20260319--bible-app-aso-playbook.md`,
  `arthurspalanzani--20260203--profitable-prayer-app-72-hours.md`,
  `arthurspalanzani--20260110--glow-affirmation-app-2k-challenge.md`) + independently,
  Kyle Fowler/Cardstock (`superwall--20251128--kyle-fowler-cardstock-aso-tiktok.md`, who
  discovered this via his own 2019–2021 Sensor Tower research, unconnected to Arthur's
  content). Evidence quality: claimed-but-demonstrated — both show rank/revenue
  screenshots. Echo risk: low for this specific claim (Fowler is a genuinely separate,
  earlier discovery), though Arthur's 3 instances are one person repeating himself
  across apps, not 3 independent confirmations.
- **Validate keywords with a tool (Astro/Sensor Tower/AppTweak) against a
  popularity/difficulty threshold BEFORE building or committing to a niche.** arthurspalanzani
  (3 files above; thresholds vary: popularity >20 & difficulty <50, elsewhere <55/<60) +
  Kyle Fowler (`superwall--20251128...`, same method via Sensor Tower/Astro, entry point
  was the same $100 ASA credit). 2 independent voices on the _method_; treat the exact
  numeric thresholds as one person's heuristic, not an industry constant.
- **A pre-use, in-onboarding rating ask measurably lifts App Store rating volume and
  feeds ranking.** Quittr (`thebrettway--20250210--quittr-hard-paywall-influencer-cpm.md`:
  "ASO = their only organic acquisition channel," claimed #1 for "quit porn" keywords) +
  Clear30 (`superwall--20250828--clear30-quit-weed-app.md`: reviews 50→~1,000, explicitly
  copied from Cal AI's onboarding) + Nicole's multi-app portfolio
  (`superwall--20260511--nicole-four-apps-ugc-playbook.md`: forced "I rated it" tap,
  later softened but "still works"). 3 apps, but Clear30 says outright it copied the
  mechanic from Cal AI — so this may be one originating tactic (Cal AI) propagating by
  imitation through the Superwall ecosystem rather than 3 independent discoveries. Real
  signal, real echo risk simultaneously.
- **Custom product pages / Product Page Optimization is underused and high-leverage.**
  arthurspalanzani (`arthurspalanzani--20260319...`: PPO A/B on icon/screenshots;
  `arthurspalanzani--20260514--teleprompter-app-17k-month-side-project.md`: "custom
  product pages — one of the big missed opportunities," self-admitted as untested) +
  Cal AI/Zach Yadegari (`thebrettway--20260302--zach-yadegari-cal-ai-sale.md`, spine
  `07-zach-yadegari-cal-ai-exit.md`: custom product pages per ad campaign are the actual
  attribution mechanism, at $1M+/mo scale, revenue-per-page shown in App Store Connect).
  2 independent sources; Cal AI's is first-party-demonstrated at real scale, Arthur's is
  a confessed gap.
- **Charting in a less-contested category/chart is a repeatable lever.** Darcy
  (`arthurspalanzani--20260523--paid-upfront-app-facebook-groups.md`: "paid apps with no
  competition can top the charts easily," #1 paid overall then #1 paid travel on app #2).
  Single-sourced in this corpus: Payout's 5-creator push → $9,700/day + Finance-chart
  placement (`superwall--20260315--creator-equity-viral-formats.md`) is real and
  demonstrates a coordinated-push charting mechanism, but the "explicit aside" that
  Food & Drink is "far less contested" than Finance was a **fabricated quotation** in an
  earlier draft — struck by red team (absent from this raw and the entire corpus). Payout
  does not corroborate the less-contested-_category_ claim, only the coordinated-push
  mechanism; treat this consensus as 1-source (Darcy) for the category-competitiveness
  point specifically.

## Conflicts

- **ASO as durable moat vs. ASO as an eroding, legacy-dominated fight.** Cardstock held
  #1 for "baseball card scanner" ~2–3 years on zero ad spend
  (`superwall--20251128--kyle-fowler-cardstock-aso-tiktok.md`) — but Blue Throne's Josh
  says 80% more apps are submitted YoY and legacy apps 5–8 years old own the top
  keywords now (`superwall--20260708--blue-throne-six-rules-exit.md`). This is really an
  **era** disagreement: Fowler's ranking was won into an empty 2019–2020 SERP; today's
  mature, English, US-market top keywords are a different, much harder fight. Crave's
  niche dish-level terms may still resemble Fowler's empty pond, but the "2-year moat"
  outcome should not be assumed going in.
- **ASO as primary growth channel vs. ASO as background hygiene only.** Cardstock ran 2
  years on ASO alone with zero ad spend, and Quittr calls it their _only_ organic channel
  — vs. arthurspalanzani's own framing that "once your ASO base is done, you can then add
  meta ads, TikTok ads or organic content" (`arthurspalanzani--20260319...`), treating ASO
  as a foundation layer, not the engine; Symmetry's founder (`arthurspalanzani--20260702--symmetry-billion-views-ugc-machine.md`)
  discusses zero ASO at all, pure UGC. This is a **category-crowding** question in
  disguise: Cardstock/Quittr won because their keyword was genuinely unserved; most
  guests in crowded categories (fitness, AI-utility) don't expect ASO to carry them.
  Whether Crave's dish-level space is "empty" or "crowded" is exactly the thing the
  keyword-validation-before-building consensus above says to go measure, not assume.
- **Country/locale arbitrage as a growth hack vs. category mismatch for a geo-anchored
  product.** Arthur's whole playbook — Brazil for a US-hard Bible-app keyword, per-country
  Nordic pricing for Glow, the "US-locale-matrix trick" (9 non-US locales feeding US
  rankings) — is not disputed by anyone in the corpus; it's simply a single-practitioner
  tactic built for globally-distributable lifestyle apps
  (`arthurspalanzani--20260319...`, `arthurspalanzani--20260110...`). For a single-metro
  product the entire arbitrage _axis_ (low-difficulty countries) doesn't exist; if an
  analogous arbitrage exists for Crave it would have to be low-difficulty adjacent
  keywords or future cities, not locales — this section's evidence says nothing
  specific about that adjacent case.
- **Forced vs. honest rating prompts.** Nicole's team shipped a button users must tap
  ("I rated it") to advance onboarding even if they hadn't — her own co-founder was
  uncomfortable, and the newest app dropped the forced tap while keeping the ask
  (`superwall--20260511--nicole-four-apps-ugc-playbook.md`). Clear30's version asks for a
  real rating post-value with no forced confirmation, and the founders themselves call it
  "the dumbest thing I've ever seen" from a consumer standpoint even though it's not
  gated (`superwall--20250828--clear30-quit-weed-app.md`). Neither source flags that
  gating _any_ action behind a review prompt (SKStoreReviewController) risks Apple
  App Review rejection — that's a compliance conflict the corpus itself is silent on and
  Crave should resolve conservatively.

## Noise (discard for Crave)

- **Country/localization arbitrage as a growth lever** (Brazil for Bible, Nordic pricing
  for Glow, the US-locale-matrix trick) — pure category mismatch for a single-city
  product; the addressable market doesn't have a "low-difficulty country" analog.
  Discard as strategy; keep only as trivia for a distant multi-country future.
- **Portfolio-of-many-small-$2k-apps preference** (arthurspalanzani's stated preference,
  `arthurspalanzani--20260203...`) — the opposite of Crave's single-city-density bet.
- **Apple Search Ads as a scaling channel.** Every source that mentions ASA
  (arthurspalanzani x3, Fowler, Darcy-adjacent) frames it as a keyword-_discovery_
  instrument, and one data point shows it can fail completely (Glow: $100 credit, 4
  downloads total, `arthurspalanzani--20260110...`). Treat ASA only as the free
  validation lab every source agrees it is, never as a launch marketing plan.
- **Darcy's "#1 paid" as an ASO benchmark** (`arthurspalanzani--20260523...`) — a
  near-zero-competition PAID chart is structurally irrelevant to a free-download,
  subscription app competing in the free charts.
- **All specific dollar/download/percentage figures from arthurspalanzani's videos** —
  he sells the exact ASO tool and community being demoed in all 8 of his videos in this
  corpus; every headline number is also the marketing hook. Keep the mechanics, discount
  the numbers.
- **Kyle Fowler's own disclaimer.** He says outright "I threw one thing at the wall and
  it stuck... I'm not the best at knowing" replicable niche discovery
  (`superwall--20251128...`) — his 2-year moat is self-flagged as non-generalizable luck,
  not a reproducible method.

## Crave transfer

**Concrete leaning:** run the keyword-validation-before-building step now, pre-launch,
against Crave's actual candidate keyword set — dish-level and Austin-intent phrases
("best tacos austin," "where to eat austin," "austin restaurants," "birria austin") —
using an Astro/Sensor Tower/AppTweak-class tool, not the generic "food discovery" or
"restaurant finder" terms that are almost certainly saturated and legacy-dominated per
Blue Throne's 80%-YoY warning. Category choice: no corpus evidence; Crave-side reasoning
pending the keyword pull [red-team correction] — the ledger previously cited a
Casper/Payout "Food & Drink charts less contested than Finance" observation
(`superwall--20260315...`) as support, but that quotation is fabricated (absent from
this raw and the entire corpus) and has been struck. The Food & Drink lean for listing
purposes rests on zero corpus evidence and must be confirmed against actual current
category competition before finalizing. Title/subtitle keyword hierarchy, no duplicate keywords,
and Product Page Optimization / custom product page A/B tests on icon and screenshots
are cheap and mechanical — build them into the App Store Connect listing now, ahead of
the Apple Developer enrollment blocker already on the fact sheet's launch checklist.
Crave's onboarding already has a rating-ask step; the finding here is about placement and
tone, not whether to have one — put it after a value-delivering moment (a personalized
"your city's top dishes" teaser, echoing Clear30's "5 minutes of personalized feedback
first"), and do not gate onboarding progress on a fake "I rated it" tap the way Nicole's
team did — both for the ethical discomfort her own team flagged and for the real Apple
App Review risk of conditioning advancement on a review action. Use the Apple Search Ads
$100 signup credit purely as the keyword-discovery lab every source describes (a broad
discovery campaign for about a week, then an exact-match campaign on winners) and budget
for the real possibility it produces nothing, as it did for Glow.

**What changes if X:** if an actual Astro/Sensor Tower pull shows Austin dish-level
search volume is too thin to matter, ASO drops to a background/hygiene layer — matching
arthurspalanzani's own "ASO is the foundation, not the whole play" framing — and the
growth burden shifts fully to UGC/creators; the panel should set a concrete threshold
(e.g., a minimum weekly App Store Connect impression count on the top 10 target
keywords) for when to stop iterating ASO and reallocate effort.

**What an adversarial panel must still resolve:**

1. Is Crave's dish-level keyword space actually a low-difficulty, real-volume gap
   (Cardstock's 2019 pattern) or a crowded, legacy-dominated fight (Blue Throne's 80%-
   YoY pattern)? This corpus gives reasoning, not a measurement — it needs an actual
   keyword-tool pull against Austin-intent terms before launch.
2. Which App Store category (Food & Drink vs. Lifestyle vs. Travel) actually gives Crave
   the best combination of chart-competitiveness and buyer intent? Category choice: no
   corpus evidence; Crave-side reasoning pending the keyword pull [red-team correction] —
   the "Food & Drink less contested than Finance" data point cited in an earlier draft was
   a fabricated quotation and has been struck; there is no direct precedent in the corpus
   for a food-discovery utility's category choice.
3. Exactly where should the rating ask sit relative to the hard paywall, and does
   asking-then-immediately-paywalling risk contaminating "rated because they saw value"
   with "rated because they were about to be asked to pay"? This is an onboarding-
   sequencing question the ASO evidence alone can't resolve.
4. Is custom-product-page infrastructure (Cal AI's model) premature for Crave, which has
   zero ad budget and one organic listing, versus Cal AI which built it to serve a
   $7k/day paid-ads motion? Building the operational overhead before there's a paid
   acquisition motion to attach it to may be over-engineering the wrong thing first.
