# 01. Positioning & Category

## Where the evidence points

The single strongest positioning frame in the corpus comes from its one non-survivor voice — the Blue Throne M&A buyer (`superwall--20260708--blue-throne-six-rules-exit.md`): **define the category narrowly enough that you are believably #1 in it, but not so narrow that nobody believes it's a category** (Flo: health → women's health, 70M+ MAU, $1B+ valuation; Runna: not #1 fitness, #1 couch-to-5K, acquired by Strava as a beginner funnel). Crave should claim the **vertical** narrow — "the only app that ranks dishes, not restaurants" / "the best-dish finder" — and treat Austin as the density proof, _not_ the category. The geo claim ("the best Austin food app") fails the believability test in the wrong direction: it reads small, has no acquirer-legible ecosystem, and every corpus datapoint on geography says algorithmic distribution can't be city-fenced anyway — positioning language has to do the geo work ("best birria in Austin" hooks), not the category definition. The dish-level claim passes Blue Throne's ecosystem test cleanly: competitors exist and are huge (Google/Yelp/Beli at restaurant level), and the dish level is structurally unoccupied — the rare case where the narrow slot is both believable and empty. The real tension is that the corpus's dominant clone school says never enter a category without a $100k+/mo incumbent proving demand; Crave's counter-evidence is its own corpus — tens of thousands of Reddit "best X in Austin" threads are latent-demand proof the clone school's chart-scan method can't see. Premium pricing is itself positioning in this corpus (the post-ChatGPT $20/mo reset; Waking Up's $150/yr), but every premium case rode a pre-existing trust persona Crave doesn't have — the gap a local-creator partnership or the evidence-receipts brand must fill.

## Consensus

1. **Narrow until you can believably win, then verticalize.** Blue Throne's rule (Flo/Runna, `superwall--20260708`) converges with the clone school's practice: PrayScreen cloned One Sec into the Christian niche and owned the "Bible focus" query ($120k ARR in ~6 months, `superwall--20250928`); Nicole shipped "UMAX for women" into an empty App Store slot (#3 overall briefly, `superwall--20260511`); Choi's verticalization math (`superwall--20250404`); Connor's niche-band sizing ($30k–few-hundred-k/mo apps, avoid Kim-K-budget broad and dog-handstand narrow, `superwall--20251217`); Caleb's adjacent-ification (ranked gym → ranked running, `superwall--20260309`). 6+ sources. **Echo risk: high** — the clone school all watch each other on the same channel and repeat "copy a winner into a niche." Blue Throne is the independent leg: a buyer's valuation lens reaching the same shape from LTV math, not content-meta. Evidence quality: Flo/Runna are public-ish facts; the clone anecdotes are self-reported survivor claims.
2. **Instantly-legible positioning language beats clever.** Massive's "Tinder for jobs" got 2–3x creator reply rates and views vs "job search on autopilot" (`superwall--20250803`, first-party); renaming Ask.fun → NGL made the product self-explaining to the demographic (`thebrettway--20250325`); Kelechi: the name is "~30% of it" early, title = Name — dash — what-it-does (`thebrettway--20250904`); Copia: a generic name ("AI journal") means you're never found post-viral (`superwall--20251022`); Driss: renaming "UGC marketplace" → "the new way to make money online" flipped growth — sell the outcome, never the mechanism (`superwall--20260301`). 5 sources, mostly independent incident reports. Quality: claimed but mechanism-consistent across all five.
3. **Positioning wording IS the audience filter.** The hook's first seconds function as targeting: "Stop using LinkedIn" (white-collar, converted) vs "Stop using Indeed" (blue-collar, zero conversion) on near-identical videos (`superwall--20250803`, first-party); identical creative with "post-breakup activity" overlay = millions of views, zero downloads, vs "procrastinating for my exams" = App Store rank jump (`superwall--20260521`); Bloom's StudyTok-borrowed narrative pulled views that didn't convert because the narrative mismatched a fintech product (`superwall--20250707`); Pingo's Russia disaster — ~half the user base unmonetizable because content, not category, chose the audience (`superwall--20260419`). 4 sources, low echo (different apps, different failures). This is the strongest transferable mechanism for a geo-locked product: the city goes in the hook.
4. **Price level is a positioning statement, with the direction set by niche psychology, not a universal rule.** Blue Throne: ChatGPT reset the ceiling to $20/mo; fewer-payers-at-higher-prices is the bootstrap shape ($20/mo → 125k payers for $30M ARR vs 500k at $4.99). Parra: niche apps with a dialed-in flow sustain $20–22/mo while commodity wrappers settle at $29.99–39.99/yr (`superwall--20260208`). Raising prices raised conversion for Nicole (3x, then +20% twice, `superwall--20260511`) and Kelechi ($6.99→$9.99/wk, `thebrettway--20250904`). Counter-cases are niche-psychological, not refutations: Sunflower's addiction demographic converted better cheaper (`superwall--20260323`); Alberto priced a save-money app low on purpose (`arthurspalanzani--20260623`); Sway priced high for private feedback while Cal AI priced low for growth — same builders, opposite postures by design (`superwall--20260505`). 7 sources. Quality: mostly claimed; the Waking Up 10x ratio is a Sensor Tower estimate.
5. **Category/audience choice caps revenue harder than product does.** Stronger: "your category defines your macro LTV limit" — a tracker competes with pen and paper, a planner competes with a personal trainer (`superwall--20250815`); Minutes AI: same app class aimed at an older audience made ~3x the money on 1/5–1/6 the downloads (`superwall--20250803`, secondhand Sensor Tower); Marcus Burke's age economics — 18–24 can't afford $70/yr, 65+ costs 2x per trial but converts far better (`superwall--20260621`). 3 sources, different lenses (product, Sensor Tower, ad-buying). For Crave this decides the _reference competitor_: position against wasted $60 dinners and tourist-trap misses, not against free Google Maps.

## Conflicts

1. **Category creation vs. proven-category entry.** Blue Throne says narrow-until-you-win even into slots without incumbents (GLP-1 trackers "possibly viable if the market grows"); Caleb Dean's hard rule is enter only where 1–3 apps already do ≥$100k/mo (`superwall--20260309`), and the whole clone school (Arthur, PrayScreen, Nicole, Connor) operationalizes demand as _visible chart incumbents_. Dish-level food discovery has no $100k/mo incumbent — by the clone test Crave is unvalidated; by the buyer test it's free category real estate with a believable ecosystem. What the disagreement is really about: **what counts as demand evidence.** The clone school can only see App Store charts; Crave's Reddit corpus (27k posts in one city subreddit, 91–97% re-mention saturation) is latent-demand proof of exactly the kind Dmitri's latent-vs-induced frame favors (`superwall--20260104`). Incentives matter too: the clone school sells speed-to-flip playbooks; the buyer prices durable category ownership.
2. **Geo-narrow vs. vertical-narrow.** Symmetry deliberately stayed Spain-first, rejected the US-first instinct, and claims "the market will not get saturated… still plenty of room in Spain," with dominance-in-one-geography as the expansion proof point (`arthurspalanzani--20260702`) — the corpus's only endorsement of Crave's depth-in-one-market shape. Against it: Jay/Nomad Table explicitly refuses location-based positioning because the algorithm can't hold a geography (`superwall--20260223`), and every UGC operator treats geo-wasted reach as the killer (Pingo's Russia, StudyFetch's >80%-US targeting heuristic). Really about: **whether distribution or positioning carries the geography.** Nobody in the corpus solved city-level targeting through the algorithm; the ones who won geo did it through creator identity (US creator → US audience, `superwall--20260524`) and hook language. Conclusion both sides support: category = vertical, geography = executed through local creators and city-named hooks.
3. **Premium-persona trust vs. earned/product trust.** Waking Up ($20/mo, ~10x revenue-to-download) and Purpose launched distribution-first on Sam Harris's and Mark Manson's pre-existing trust (`superwall--20260708`); Sunflower hired the #1 sobriety influencer as an employee for the "headliner effect" — the biggest persona makes a no-name app read as category leader (`superwall--20260323`). But the same buyer who cites Waking Up warns influencer-bound distribution is _fragile at exit_ (deals must chain the influencer to content; influencers refuse earnouts), and Sunflower's play took ~$6.5M of VC. Really about: **whether trust is rented or built** — and at what capital level. The bootstrapped scale-down both sides point to is one deep local-creator partnership (StudyFetch's zero-follower brand-account mechanic; Blue Throne's "build WITH the influencer" caveat), not a celebrity.
4. **Premium-utility positioning vs. market-share brand positioning.** Mauro (Symmetry): hard paywalls are "not thinking big enough," product-led brand wins (`arthurspalanzani--20260702`); Fitted is ripping out its hard paywall to chase share (`superwall--20251121`). Against: Cal AI, Stronger, Quittr, Cardstock, Nicole all report hard-paywall revenue steps. Really about **category economics**: every anti-paywall voice runs a social/network product where K-factor is the asset; every pro-paywall voice sells single-player utility. Positioning consequence for Crave: it must present as a premium data utility (pay for the answer), and not muddy that with social-network signaling at the wall — the corpus punishes incoherence between category posture and monetization posture (Fitted's ~$0.25/download — a **derived** revenue-per-download figure, $150k lifetime subscription revenue ÷ 600k downloads, not a quoted number, and distinct from the raw's separately-stated 1–15¢ acquisition-cost figures — is the cautionary tale).

## Noise

- **"A tenth of Cal AI is still $200k/mo" verticalization math** (`superwall--20250404`): assumes global TAM; a single-city product's niche fraction is a fraction of a fraction. Also from a community seller whose product is niche-clone optimism.
- **Paid-chart #1 positioning** (Darcy, `arthurspalanzani--20260523`): paid-upfront charts are an uncontested sandbox irrelevant to a subscription app; the "#1" is a category artifact.
- **Country-arbitrage ASO** (Brazil keywords, `arthurspalanzani--20260319`): meaningless for a geo-locked product — Brazilian trials are worthless traffic to Crave.
- **"The App Store is a pure meritocracy"** (`thebrettway--20250325`): contradicted by Blue Throne's 80%-more-apps-YoY and legacy keyword ownership; NGL's "merit" was a K-factor loop Crave doesn't have.
- **Waking Up's 10x revenue-to-download ratio as a target**: Sensor Tower estimate riding a celebrity moat; treat as unreachable ceiling, not aspiration (the spine note says exactly this).
- **"Perceived efficacy beats actual utility"** (`superwall--20260301`): Duolingo-cynicism from an 18-year-old selling views; as _strategy_ it's poison for an objectivity-branded product. Salvage only the onboarding lesson (make the Score feel authoritative instantly).
- **Category color / anti-orange branding** (Quittr purple, `thebrettway--20250210`): thin, unmeasured.

## Crave transfer

**Concrete leaning:** Claim the vertical: _"the app that ranks dishes, not restaurants"_ — with the believability supplied by the named giants it sits beside (Google/Yelp/Beli rank places; nobody ranks the birria itself). App Store title/subtitle in Kelechi's format ("Crave — best dishes near you" class), Food & Drink category (Payout charted in Finance at #70–80 off one coordinated creator day, `superwall--20260315` — Food & Drink is softer), and own the "austin food ranking / best dishes austin" query class **now**, because the PrayScreen window (sole listing → 70 clones in 2–3 months) is the realistic decay clock once the category is legible. Positioning copy mined verbatim from the Reddit corpus (Marcus Burke's language-mining is literally Crave's own pipeline pointed at marketing). Geography lives in hooks and creator selection, never in the category claim. Pricing posture stays premium-consistent — the section's evidence says raising is safer than the fact sheet's $7.99 instinct implies, but that adjudication belongs to the pricing panel; positioning's contribution is the reference frame ("one bad dinner costs more than a year of Crave" — Payout's ROI framing, the easiest sell in the corpus).

**What changes if X:** If dish-level hooks under-convert in early creator tests (the Massive LinkedIn/Indeed method applied to "ranks dishes" vs "never eat badly in Austin again"), fall back to outcome framing while keeping the category claim in store metadata. If cities #2–N seed early (~$600/city per the fact sheet), the category claim hardens from aspiration to fact ("in N cities") and the clone-school objection dissolves. If a credible dish-level competitor appears, the window logic inverts: speed to multi-city becomes the positioning defense.

**Adversarial panel must still resolve:** (1) Lead identity in creator content — dish-level category vs. Austin-insider persona — since store metadata and content hooks can diverge; (2) whether "dish-level food discovery" passes the believability test with zero direct occupants, or needs an educative comparison frame ("Beli for dishes" style) that borrows an incumbent; (3) which trust substitute carries the premium posture at launch — evidence-receipts product brand vs. one deep local-creator persona partnership; (4) whether App Store keyword positioning should target the national query class ("food ranking," contested) or the Austin-intent class (thin volume but ownable — Blue Throne says legacy apps own the big keywords anyway).
