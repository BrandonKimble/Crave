# 06. The Creator/UGC Machine

> Ledger section: creator sourcing doctrines, every deal-structure number in the corpus, management ratios, CPM benchmarks, saturation ceilings — cross-examined against Crave's Austin-first, hard-paywall reality. Citations are testimony filenames in `business/signal/testimony/` (spine notes in `spine/` where deeper).

## Where the evidence points

The corpus converges hard on the _machine shape_ and diverges on the _sourcing doctrine_ — and for Crave, the divergence resolves itself once you add the variable none of these operators faced: geography. The consensus machine is: founder personally cracks a format first (the single strongest claim in the corpus, ~10 independent tellings across all three channels), then a small roster of coached creators replicates it under a base-plus-non-stacking-view-bonus deal that nets ~$1–2 effective CPM, with the founder hands-on for the first 60–90 days and a 1:10 manager ratio afterward. The sourcing fight — StudyFetch's pay-10-20x-for-influencer-trust-transfer vs Jenni's train-charisma-from-anywhere vs Symmetry's hire-your-own-users — is really a fight about capital stage and targeting need. Crave's answer is dictated by its constraint: the For You page cannot be geo-fenced to Austin, so **locality is the binding filter and charisma is the ranking filter within the local pool**; the highest-fit single mechanic in the corpus is StudyFetch's zero-follower brand account (spine/03) scaled down to a trusted Austin food creator running a "Crave ATX"-style account at a few-hundred-to-low-four-figures monthly retainer, not $10k. Every headline view number in this section (1.2B, 500M, 400M/mo) is global-TAM survivor telling and should be discounted to near-zero for a city-fenced paywalled utility — Pingo's Russia lesson (400M views buying ~200k unmonetizable users, superwall--20260419) is the controlled experiment proving geo-mismatched virality is spend, not growth. Expect Cal AI's $2M/mo influencer-saturation ceiling (spine/07) to arrive in _months_, not years, because Austin's food-creator pool is tiny and audience-overlapped.

## Consensus

1. **Founder-led content precedes any creator spend.** Sources: superwall--20251123 (Matt made all the content himself for his own prior startup, Sideshore, pre-Jenni — not Jenni content, which he ran with a creator program from day one — misattributed as "Matt made all Jenni content pre-hire" in an earlier draft, corrected here; "you can't recruit creators without a track record"), superwall--20260524 (Kieran's first video, launch day), superwall--20260223 (Jay: first 50–100k downloads from own posts; still top creator in his 60–70-creator network), superwall--20260511 (Nicole: "I'm the first one to go viral on all four of my apps"), arthurspalanzani--20260702 (Symmetry: "you cannot manage people expecting them to go viral if you don't know how"), superwall--20260301, superwall--20251026, superwall--20260521, superwall--20260601 (Drew: founder deeply hands-on months 1–2, video reviews before posting), superwall--20251102, superwall--20260419. **10+ sources, all three channels, operationally consistent — the most robust claim in this section.** Echo risk exists (guests watch each other) but the claim survives it: it's the one thing nobody with an incentive to sell you creators would invent.
2. **Deal-structure convergence: small per-video base + non-stacking view milestones ≈ $1–2 effective CPM.** superwall--20260329 ($20/video + $60/$200/$500/$800 at 20k/100k/500k/1M, non-stacked, single-platform trigger; 29/30 creators chose it over higher-EV pure CPM), superwall--20260601 ($800/mo for 60 videos + non-stacked bonuses; pay only best of 4 cross-posts), superwall--20260419 (base + non-stacking 50k→1M tiers, "the money's in the bonuses"), superwall--20260511 ($10–30/post + decreasing CPM $4→$0.80), superwall--20250707 ($500–1,000/mo retainer + $2–4 CPM tiers), thebrettway--20250624 ($25–40/video + $500@500k/$1k@1M), superwall--20260524 ($100 per 100k views "pretty standard"). Quality: all claimed-first-party from operators; tight clustering is itself the evidence. **Echo-chamber risk: high** — this is one scene copying one meta (Drew documents the meta churning: $400+$2CPM four months earlier) — but for _price discovery_ echo is fine; these are the rates creators currently accept.
3. **CPM benchmarks: month-1 $5–8, mature sub-$1–2.** superwall--20260601 ($5–8 → sub-$3 by month 3 → $0.80–0.90 at every $100k+/mo program), superwall--20251123 (<$2 at 3 months), superwall--20260223 ($1–2 standard; the "$3 market ceiling" struck by red team — not in this raw; Jay never states a $3 ceiling, the only higher figure is the interviewer's hypothetical "$4 CPM," and it isn't attributed to any other source either), superwall--20251102 (~$2), superwall--20251022 (~$1 target), superwall--20251026 ($1–2 paid vs $6–7 RPM), thebrettway--20241128 ($2–3 CPM vs $5 assumed RPM), thebrettway--20250210 ($2–3 internal rule, never disclosed to creator). Quality: claimed, unaudited, but 8 independent operators land in one band.
4. **Views without the product on screen are worthless.** superwall--20251123 ("a viral study TikTok that didn't mention Jenni is completely useless"), superwall--20260419 ("virality that converts shows and doesn't tell"), thebrettway--20250210 (walked a $10k contract over integration placement), superwall--20260412 (40M-view video → $25k vs hypothetical $360k), superwall--20250803 (11M views → ~nothing), superwall--20260216 (VSC "convertible" test = comments asking "what app is that"), superwall--20260524 (product-as-hero doctrine), superwall--20260315, superwall--20260521 (16M-view post → rank ~170). 9+ sources, several first-party with receipts. Strongest _content_ law in the corpus.
5. **Creator rosters are power-law; cut the bottom fast; don't nickel-and-dime winners.** arthurspalanzani--20260702 (top 5 of 50 outperform the other 45), superwall--20260419 (top 20–30% of ~100; "10 goated"; cut 30–50), superwall--20260301 (top 1% ≈ 90% of views; 2,000-creator program doing 500k views/mo as the counterexample), superwall--20260601 (fire fast to keep CPMs down; capping a winner to save $50 "costs thousands"), superwall--20260329 (kept underperformers 2–3 weeks too long — named mistake). Management math: 1 manager : 10 creators, 15 max (superwall--20260601); 1 coach : 12 creators (superwall--20260412); founder can step back once blended CPM < $4 (superwall--20260601).
6. **Fresh/zero-follower accounts can go viral; follower counts don't predict output.** superwall--20250717 (1,900-follower creator; "algorithms reward quality content"), superwall--20260329 ("first video can get 10 million views"), superwall--20260524 (zero-follower influencer accounts "outperforming everything else"), superwall--20251123, arthurspalanzani--20260702, superwall--20250707, thebrettway--20240801 (pay on views never followers). Post-2022 algorithm fact, broadly attested.
7. **Niche influencer pools saturate.** spine/07 / thebrettway--20260302 (Cal AI ceiling ≈ $2M/mo — big fitness influencers share audiences; overlap invisible until paid for), superwall--20260524 (problem/solution format saturated; "UGC becoming less and less effective"), superwall--20251102 (studytok: "every third or fifth video" is disguised promo; own 400-video/week program called "a temporary hack"), superwall--20250707 (trend cycles 3–6 months). First-party from the biggest spenders — take seriously.

## Conflicts

1. **Sourcing doctrine: trust-transfer vs trained charisma vs own-users vs equity partner.** StudyFetch (superwall--20260524, spine/03): pay a 500k–1M-follower niche influencer ~$10k/mo (10–20x UGC rates) to run a zero-follower brand account — authenticity hedge because "audiences understand the videos are ads." Jenni (superwall--20251123, spine/04): ignore stats entirely, scout "camera charisma" (emotive eyes, first-frame aesthetic, yapper) in unrelated niches, train for 25+ videos, get "better deals" because you're not paying for followers. Symmetry (arthurspalanzani--20260702): hire your own app users, screen via unpaid challenge for intelligence/determination, pay revenue-indexed CPM. Connor/Casper/Fitted (superwall--20251217, superwall--20260315, superwall--20251121): give the creator equity (up to 50–70%) and build around them. Sunflower (superwall--20260323): hire the niche #1 as a salaried employee. **What it's really about: capital stage and how badly you need the creator's _specific audience_.** StudyFetch and Sunflower are post-raise; Jenni sits inside a $750k/mo company; Symmetry and the equity plays are the zero-capital shapes. If the creator's audience IS your targeting mechanism (StudyFetch's own geo rule: US creator on TikTok → 80%+ US audience), you pay for the audience. If the algorithm finds your buyers anywhere, you pay only for talent.
2. **DM vs email outreach.** Cal AI (thebrettway--20241128): DM, never email — "email reaches the manager," rates jump; 1-in-100 reply, send thousands. Quittr (thebrettway--20250210): DM "Paid promo" first from a verified account, price only on calls. Connor (superwall--20251217): email, never DM — large creators get "a million notifications"; YouTube's view-email-address CAPTCHA filters bot spam; 8-month-late replies are normal. Jenni (superwall--20251123): DMs but expect _lower_ response from non-influencers (they don't check DMs, fear scams). **Really about target class:** micro/unmanaged niche creators → DM from a credible account; larger or managed creators → email. Not a genuine contradiction once stratified.
3. **Roster size: elite few vs volume army.** Driss (superwall--20260301): <20 consistent creators did Cluely's 100M views; "much easier to make someone a better creator than to 10x creator count." Coconote (superwall--20260412): 10–12 "Navy SEALs." Versus Halo's 85 creators/300 posts/day (superwall--20260329), Sprout's 200+ (superwall--20260511), StudyFetch's hundreds (peak ~1,000). **Really about whether the format is already cracked:** armies replicate a proven format; small rosters are for finding one. Everyone agrees the _finding_ phase is small.
4. **Cap vs no-cap on winner payouts.** Drew (superwall--20260601): never view-cap — the 180M-views-in-2-weeks creator capped at ~$4k (vs ~$75k uncapped) cost the brand the creator; time-cap instead. Symmetry, Nomad Table agree (uncapped; $8k best month paid happily). Versus Bloom's hard $1k/video cap (superwall--20250707) and Halo's non-stacking tiers, which are soft caps by construction (8M cross-platform views pays one $800 bonus). **Really about relationship horizon vs budget certainty** — and note the non-stacking structure is the corpus's quiet compromise: creators accepted it 29/30 times (superwall--20260329).
5. **Is UGC decaying as a channel?** Kieran and Julian — both running _large_ programs — say yes (ads-blindness, saturation; superwall--20260524, superwall--20251102), while the 2026 cohort keeps scaling it. Era signal worth respecting: the corpus spans 2024–2026 and the sophistication bar visibly rises across it (fresh-account warm-ups, authenticity hedges, zero-follower brand accounts). The cheap-2024 versions of these tactics are already stale.

## Noise

- **Marketplace/platform vendor numbers.** Sideshift's sub-$1 CPMs and "creators earn $10–15k/mo" (superwall--20260601, superwall--20250717), Noise's $0.30/$0.08 installs (superwall--20260125), Methods' "$1.2M ARR in 7 days" (superwall--20260301): every one is a first-party sales pitch by the person selling the marketplace, with no attribution methodology. Use their _process_ claims, ignore their outcome claims.
- **Global-TAM view economics.** 1.2B views (Halo), 500M (Fitted), 400M/wk (Sprout), 2B (StudyFetch): all ride universal audiences with single-session visual wow. Behind Crave's Austin-only hard paywall, ~97–99% of a national viral hit's reach cannot convert even in principle. Pingo's Russia case (superwall--20260419) is the clean proof: half the user base from a geography that couldn't pay = "a lot of spend on views that did not monetize at all," and creator _location_ didn't control audience geography (UK creator, 90%+ Russian viewers). Discard every raw view benchmark; keep only per-Austin-view economics.
- **The "10% view→download" class of claims.** Copia's intro framing vs its own body data (13M views → 10–15k downloads ≈ 0.1%, superwall--20251022); PushScroll's 10% launch spike rode a lifetime-free giveaway (superwall--20251018). Honest corpus range is 0.05–1% by format (superwall--20260216's ladder), before any geo filter.
- **Mindshare-over-conversion (Cluely).** thebrettway--20250624: explicitly VC-subsidized, conversion-indifferent, founder-scandal-powered. A bootstrapped paywalled utility cannot spend reputation or run loss-making awareness channels. Keep only the $25–40/video rate card.
- **TikTok Shop 100k-affiliate machines** (Neurogum, superwall--20250803) and **phone-farm/reposter fleets** (superwall--20260216, superwall--20260521): commerce economics and ToS-adverse ops capacity respectively; both geography-agnostic by design, which is the opposite of Crave's need. The TikTok-Shop _hiring filter_ (affiliate-paid creators know how to sell — superwall--20260524) survives; the machines don't.
- **Fake-student/disguised-discovery scripting at scale** (superwall--20251102's own confession, superwall--20260511's fake demos): saturating, detectable, and specifically trust-poisoning for a product whose whole brand is evidence-receipted objectivity.

## Crave transfer

**The adjudication the brief demands — charisma vs locality: locality first, charisma second.** Matt's charisma-anywhere doctrine works because Jenni's buyer is everywhere; Crave's buyer is in Austin. So invert it: the candidate pool is _Austin food/lifestyle creators_ (locality = the targeting mechanism, per StudyFetch's own creator-geography rule scaled from country to metro), and within that pool apply Jenni's charisma checklist and StudyFetch's engagement-quality screen (comment density about the creator/product, not raw views). The pool is dozens, not thousands — which flips the outreach math from Cal AI's 500-DMs-for-2 spray to relationship depth per creator, and makes the equity/rev-share anchor-creator play (superwall--20251217, superwall--20260315, superwall--20251121) unusually coherent here: Austin food is exactly Blake Anderson's "tight-knit niche with a few taste leaders" case (thebrettway--20240801), and food creators don't sell a competing product (Daniel's creator-fit law, superwall--20260505).

**Concrete leaning:** (1) Brandon posts first — 60–90 days of founder-made Austin dish-ranking content, both to crack a format and to have receipts for recruiting (the consensus is unanimous and it's free). Rankings are native engagement bait: "the #1 birria in Austin is X" is Matt's 50/50-divide mechanic with the product named intrinsically (spine/04). (2) Then 3–5 local creators max on the convergent deal grid: $20–50/video base + non-stacking milestones (single-platform trigger, ~3-month trailing window per Nomad Table), min-view clauses and 20/80 payment split (thebrettway--20250210), perpetual usage rights in every contract (superwall--20260426), no hard caps on a winner. Expect month-one blended CPM at the $5–8 end and judge it on _Austin-resident_ views, not views. (3) One experiment above the grid: a zero-follower "Crave ATX" account run by one trusted local creator — the StudyFetch mechanic at Austin scale, plausibly $500–2,000/mo instead of $10k because local food creators are small. (4) Spark-ad geo-amplification of organic winners (superwall--20260601) is the one paid mechanism that could fix geography for modest dollars — contingent on verifying metro/DMA-level targeting.

**What changes if X:** if metro-level Spark targeting works, the one-city UGC objection weakens sharply and the creator machine becomes Crave's primary channel; if it doesn't, the machine's honest ceiling is the Austin creator pool's overlapped audience — the Cal AI saturation lesson compressed into months — and multi-city seeding becomes the growth lever, with UGC as per-city ignition rather than an engine. If the anchor-creator equity route is taken, cap it at one creator and structure vesting; the corpus's equity enthusiasm all comes from the equity holders.
