# 07. Product-Led Loops & Sharing

> Ledger section: what the 64-transcript corpus says about viral loops, share surfaces,
> comment-section mechanics, and community — and whether any of it survives a
> gate-everything hard paywall. Citations are testimony filenames; spine notes
> (spine/08 Hunter Isaacson) override testimony where they conflict.

## Where the evidence points

The corpus contains two mechanically different species of loop that the "viral loops" label smears together. **Reciprocal-usage loops** (NGL, Wink, Lobby, Discord) require the recipient to _use the product_ to complete the loop — and every working example is free, because a paywall decapitates the loop at the recipient. **Artifact loops** (Darcy's watermarked spending card, Cal AI's story-shareable streaks, Stronger's heatmap screenshots, Lovely's widget output) require the recipient only to _see an output_ — and these demonstrably coexist with paid products, including Darcy, the corpus's one paid-upfront app with a functioning share loop. Crave's dinner-table loop ("where should we eat" → drop a ranked list into the group text) is an artifact loop: the recipient needs the answer, not the app. That means gate-everything and a strong share loop are **not** structurally incompatible — but only if the shared slug renders the answer richly; a wall or heavy teaser at the link kills the loop at the exact moment Hunter Isaacson identifies as the whole point: "forcing users to share is an endorsement" (superwall--20250725, spine/08). Discord's super-node theory sharpens the economics: the group's organizer is the payer; the other five people at the table are audience, not lost conversions. The hard-paywall camp's own testimony (Stronger: "nobody sends a paid app to their friends," K-factor not even instrumented) is best read not as "loops are impossible behind paywalls" but as "we never built the artifact" — a choice, not a law. Verdict-leaning: rich public web view for shared lists, CTA that sells the recipient _their own_ version of the result, watermark/brand on every artifact, and instrument word-of-mouth share from day one.

## Consensus

1. **The comment section is the conversion surface for organic content; control it.** Planted and pinned comments, replying to everyone, engineering "what app is that?" — reported first-party by Cal AI (thebrettway--20241128: plant + pin + reply, "controlling the comment section" named as core strategy), Focus Tree via Choi (superwall--20250404: conversion happens in comments; video-reply to a question = free retargeting), PushScroll (superwall--20251018: ManyChat "comment APP" → 90k DMs, 33% DM CTR, ~60% store conversion; 62k comments on top video), Payout (superwall--20260315: 13k "claim" commenters → ~50% click DM link), Bloom (superwall--20250707: dedicated comment team), Quittr (thebrettway--20250210: pinned comment naming app required in contracts), and the comment-farm operators (superwall--20260111: ~300k comments → ~7k clicks; transparent "my friends built this" framing _outperformed_ covert seeding). Quality: several independent first-party implementations with numbers — the strongest cross-ecosystem consensus in this section. Echo risk: moderate (the scene teaches each other the tactic), but the convergent numbers from unrelated operators make the mechanism itself trustworthy.
2. **Share artifacts are designed into the product, not bolted on.** "Those screenshots are what make us money" (Stronger, superwall--20250815); build a "very dramatic screenshot" while building the app (arthurspalanzani--20260623 — corrected citation; no `superwall--20260623` file exists in the corpus); Strava-style watermarked share card (arthurspalanzani--20260523); watermarked group photo to Stories (Lobby, superwall--20251206); features designed for the camera (StudyFetch superwall--20260524, Jenni superwall--20251123, Sideshift superwall--20260601: "build the feature for the format"); Cal AI's name-at-top-of-UI and a separate creator build (thebrettway--20260302). Seven-plus sources, mixed first-party. This is the section's most actionable consensus.
3. **Reciprocal-usage K-factor requires a free product; hard-paywall apps knowingly forfeit it.** NGL's loop runs on free recipients (thebrettway--20250325); Discord stayed free for years (thebrettway--20241229); Jungle's declinable paywall feeds 30–40% word-of-mouth (superwall--20251102); Stronger states the forfeit explicitly and doesn't measure K-factor (superwall--20250815); Quittr's $5 refer-a-friend died (thebrettway--20250210). No source anywhere shows recipient-must-pay reciprocal virality working.
4. **Category determines whether user-to-user sharing exists at all.** Taboo/private categories: referrals ≈ zero even with incentives (Quittr; Kelechi thebrettway--20250904: "no one actually refers their friends"; Sway's share features underperformed, superwall--20260505). Public/social categories: sharing is the engine (NGL, Lovely's grocery-list tweet → 40k downloads overnight, superwall--20251221). Food is maximally public and group-decided — Crave sits on the favorable side of this line, the opposite end from Quittr.
5. **Owned real estate compounds: persistent links keep converting with zero new posts.** Hunter's link-in-bio as the loop's home + churn-with-redownload (both Hunter files); Pepai creators' bio links producing downloads after two weeks of no posts (superwall--20260628); Casper's 500-day back-catalog still driving daily installs (superwall--20260315). Word-of-mouth as measured force multiplier belongs here too: Jungle 30–40% (instrumented via onboarding attribution question), Nomad Table 10–15% (superwall--20260223), Blue Throne's organic-share benchmarks (50%+ great / 35–50 good / <20 = UA treadmill, superwall--20260708).

## Conflicts

1. **Hunter (loop IS the business, free is the fuel) vs. Stronger/Cal AI/Quittr (hard paywall, buy or earn distribution, ignore K-factor).** Not really a disagreement about loops — a disagreement about _what monetizes_: Hunter converts a microscopic slice of a 150M-MAU free base into $1–7 weekly subs; the paywall camp converts 4–15% of downloads and treats every free user as cost. Both are internally coherent; the fight is category and model. Crave cannot adopt Hunter's model, but can steal his loop _mechanics_ for artifacts.
2. **"Hard paywall means your app is bad" (Copia superwall--20251022, Symmetry arthurspalanzani--20260702, Fitted superwall--20251121) vs. hard-paywall-wins A/Bs (Nicole +50% conversion superwall--20260511; Cardstock superwall--20251128; Quittr's free-tier test killing both usage and revenue).** What it's really about: whether growth is loop-driven (free users are fuel) or conversion-driven (free users are leakage) — and evidence quality. Fitted's anti-paywall stance comes from a founder who admits no PMF and high churn; Symmetry is a network-effect social app. The paywall camp's numbers are stronger but come disproportionately from the Superwall ecosystem, which structurally selects for first-session monetizers — a real sampling bias when weighing loop value.
3. **Dan Kwan halting a working UGC engine to build K-factor loops (Shepherd, superwall--20251026: "prayer buddies," schools/churches, "like NGL and Gas") vs. the machine doctrine (Blue Throne, Sideshift): scale the repeatable creator machine, loops are unreliable.** This is a time-horizon and conviction fight — loops compound but arrive late; creator spend is immediate but rented. No resolution in-corpus; Shepherd's pivot has no reported outcome.
4. **Referral-unlock cannibalization.** Nicole: invite-3-friends-to-bypass-paywall did _not_ hurt conversion (sharers converted anyway; 3 real installs is harder than paying) and doubled as branded-keyword TikTok SEO via the template text. Wink: pay-99c-or-share produced "millions of organic downloads" (thebrettway--20250325). Quittr: $5 referral flopped. Who's right depends on what's shared (a code vs. an endorsement) and category shame level; for food (zero shame, natural endorsement), the Nicole/Wink side is more probative.

## Noise (discard for Crave)

- **NGL's detonation math** (1,000 users/day threshold → 1.5M downloads/day; 90k/hr) — friend-graph physics of a free social product where every use emits a story to hundreds of viewers. Crave's usage is invisible to non-users; no analogous phase transition exists. Keep the mechanics, discard the curve.
- **Zoom University / meme-page seeding, school-account seeding** (superwall--20250404) — teen-social tribal mechanics; category mismatch, and secondhand.
- **Phone-farm / mass-account comment and slideshow operations** (superwall--20260111, --20260216, --20260521) as a "loop" — ToS-violating, ban-prone, geo-agnostic, and trust-destroying for an integrity-branded local product. The 2.3% comment→click arithmetic is useful only as a sobriety check.
- **Fitted's "paywall hamstrings growth"** — the founder self-reports no PMF, high churn, ~$0.25/download lifetime revenue (a **derived** figure: $150k revenue ÷ 600k downloads, distinct from the raw's separately-stated 1–15¢ acquisition-cost figures), and a token launch; his freemium pivot is a symptom, not evidence (superwall--20251121).
- **Discord's Nitro/status monetization** ("pay to be selfless") — 200M-MAU VC-funded network economics; the super-node insight transfers, the business model does not (thebrettway--20241229).
- **Methods/Cluely mindshare claims** ($1.2M ARR in 7 days; "reputation is the last thing to worry about") — vendor self-promotion and VC-subsidized indifference to conversion (superwall--20260301, thebrettway--20250624).

## Crave transfer

**The dinner-table loop is Crave's NGL-link moment, and it survives the paywall because it's an artifact loop.** The group-text question "where should we eat" is answered by a shared ranked list — the recipient consumes the _answer_, not the app. Corpus mechanics transplant cleanly: share-as-endorsement (the friend who drops the list is vouching), CTA-sells-the-result (the slug's button should read "See Austin's every dish, ranked" — the recipient's own version of "get your own messages" — never "Download Crave"), owned real estate (the slug persists in the thread; group chats re-surface it the way NGL bios re-expose churned users), and watermark/branding on every rendered card (Darcy, Lobby, Cal AI).

**Recipient-experience policy options, judged by the corpus:**

- **Rich public web view (recommended lean):** render the shared list fully — dishes, ranks, scores, a taste of evidence — free, no account. This is the NGL structure: the recipient gets the _receiving side_ of the product free and complete; conversion sells them their own generative version (any craving, any neighborhood, the full city ranking, best-near-me-now). It's also the only shape with a working paid-app precedent (Darcy). The codebase already supports it (@AllowUnentitled share/public controllers per the fact sheet) — this is a product-policy decision, not a build.
- **Teaser/blur:** precedent exists only in first-session dopamine apps (Glam Up's fake blurred scan) whose mechanics Nicole herself runs alongside forced-rating dark patterns. For a brand whose entire moat is objective, evidence-receipted integrity, blurring the answer a friend explicitly endorsed reads as bait-and-switch at the moment of maximum trust. Discard.
- **Wall:** zero support anywhere in 64 transcripts. Even the hardest-paywall operators gate _usage_, never the shared artifact — Cal AI's badges and reposted creator videos are public by design.

**Super-node economics defuse the conversion worry.** Discord's ringleader insight maps exactly: the person who organizes where the group eats is the payer; "most of those features are never used by any of their members." The public slug's job is (a) advertising and (b) recruiting the _next group's_ organizer — not converting all six recipients. Measure accordingly: instrument share events, slug views, slug→store taps, and add Jungle's "how did you hear about us" onboarding question from day one (Stronger's refusal to measure K-factor is the anti-pattern).

**What changes if X:** if slug→install conversion proves negligible after 6 months, the rich view costs nothing (it's still brand + SEO surface — YourMove's latent-demand/backlink logic applies to "best birria austin"-class queries landing on list pages). If it proves strong, it argues for shortening the shared-link entry path (skip the 17-step quiz for slug arrivals — spine/08's onboarding-speed resolution). Polls as engagement surface remain corpus-unproven: they need live users (fact sheet), and the closest analog — rankings as engagement bait (Jenni's 50/50-split mechanics; "this ranking is wrong" comment wars) — works on _content_ surfaces before it works in-app. Notably, Crave's own product decisions already encode the corpus's core lesson without citing it: `product/polls.md` states polls/discussion are permanently free specifically because "they drive contribution, virality, and feed the Score, so gating them would kill the flywheel" — the Isaacson logic (a loop needs an open surface), arrived at independently. But this sits unreconciled against `business/monetization-and-gating.md` (2026-06-27), which architected a full tiered free/paid split (restaurant lists, rankings, search, map all free; only the dish layer gated) — a document that predates and was never updated against the fact sheet's FINAL 2026-07-09 gate-everything call. That's a live internal conflict, not just an external one: the team has already built and reasoned through a version of "which surfaces must stay open for the loop to work," then shelved it as an env-gated future pivot rather than resolving whether _shared-link recipients specifically_ should live under the old tiered logic even while direct app installs live under gate-everything.

**Adversarial panel must still resolve:** (1) exactly how rich the slug is — full scores + evidence quotes, or ranks-only — and whether the answer differs for lists vs. poll results vs. single-dish shares; (2) the slug-arrival funnel: quiz-skip or full onboarding, and what the paywall shows a recipient whose city isn't seeded; (3) whether share cards/artifacts are launch-blocking (built as first-class growth engine pre-launch) or post-density polish; (4) whether an invite-style unlock (Nicole/Wink pattern) has any place in a hard-gate model, or whether it contaminates the pay-now message; (5) whether `monetization-and-gating.md`'s shelved tiered-free logic should be formally revived, scoped ONLY to the shared-link/public-web surface (not reopened for direct app installs).
