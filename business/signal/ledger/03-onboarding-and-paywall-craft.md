# 03. Onboarding & Paywall Craft

## Where the evidence points

The conversion machine is the single best-evidenced topic in the corpus — and also the most echo-chambered, because most of it descends from one lineage (Cal AI → Quittr → Clear30 → PushScroll → Caleb Dean, all screenshotting each other's onboardings) amplified by a host whose product IS the paywall. Even so, three claims replicate across genuinely independent first-party A/B tests and should be treated as settled for Crave: (1) commitment-building onboarding friction raises paywall conversion — length is a qualification filter, not a leak; (2) the trial ask converts on _de-risking_ (timeline, reminder promise, cancel-anytime), not on persuasion copy; (3) annual-as-default with the trial attached to the annual SKU is the revenue-maximizing plan structure. Crave's 17-step quiz + $39.99/yr-with-trial + $7.99/mo-pay-now design is, almost step for step, what the corpus's strongest sources converged on — the build is validated, and the remaining work is paywall-screen craft plus instrumentation, not architecture. The benchmarks worth planning against are Vahe's: trial-start >15% of paywall viewers, trial→paid >30%, no-trial install→paid 4–10% (aspire to >10%). The genuinely open question is the one the corpus splits on: whether to show real product (Austin data) before the card ask. And the sharpest finding is negative: the decline/abandonment discount machinery this scene treats as standard practice sits squarely in the Guideline 3.1.2(c)/3.1.1/5.6 enforcement zone that got Cal AI briefly pulled in April 2026 for divide-down pricing and a trial-toggle obscuring auto-renewal (app returned after fixes) — **[external fact, web-verified 2026-07-12 — not from the corpus; TechCrunch/MacRumors/9to5Mac, 2026-04-21]**, not a claim any transcript makes — for a hard-gated app with no free tier to retreat to, that entire tactic family is radioactive regardless of its measured lift.

## Consensus

- **Quiz-as-investment onboarding raises conversion (≥7 sources, several first-party A/Bs).** Cal AI A/B-proved purposeless "why are you doing this" questions raise paywall conversion (thebrettway--20241128, first-party, no numbers). Quittr: 12-page quiz, deliberately long "to filter out people who aren't serious," ~15% download→paid, 98–99% per-step completion (thebrettway--20250210, claimed). Coconote: onboarding ~5→13 screens → +16% trial conversion (superwall--20260412, claimed first-party). Copia: adding social proof + tap-through questions took install→paywall from 50–60% to 85–90% (superwall--20251022). Clear30: 20–30s dwell on goal questions with <5% drop = "good friction" (superwall--20250828, dashboard shown). Bloom: splitting one dense screen into ~5 story-style screens lifted conversion; _removing_ the advance button beat having one (superwall--20250707). Glow: progress bar + commitment screen moved completion 74%→83%; the "fewer personal questions" variant produced ZERO conversions (arthurspalanzani--20260110, dashboards shown). Echo risk: high — Quittr copied Cal AI, Clear30 copied Cal AI/Ahead, PushScroll copied Clear30 off this same podcast. But Coconote, Bloom, and Glow are independent lineages with their own A/B data, so the mechanism survives the echo discount.
- **Trial-forward, de-risk-first framing (5+ sources).** Vahe: say "free" 5–7×, label the annual card "Try it free," promise the reminder (superwall--20251116). Pingo: the trial-timeline "you'll be reminded 2 days before, cancel anytime" paywall beat value-prop and social-proof variants; even reminder timing moved conversion (superwall--20260419). Sunflower: a pre-paywall priming screen ("we'll remind you before your trial ends") → +46% trial conversion, +~48% revenue, +70% annual revenue (superwall--20260323, slide shown, two revenue figures given). Clear30: Blinkist timeline + "no payment due now" + a "this costs money and that's fair" screen (+3%) (superwall--20250828). Parra: Blinkist trial-timeline is a years-long durable winner with no half-life; "no commitment, cancel anytime" is a near-universal micro-bump (spine/01). Echo risk: the Blinkist timeline is one mass-copied artifact — but its replication across independent apps is itself the evidence.
- **Hard paywall beats freemium for utility apps (5 first-party conversions of the same experiment).** Cal AI/GrindClock: free→hard = more revenue on fewer downloads (thebrettway--20241128). Quittr: free tier killed usage AND revenue (thebrettway--20250210). Stronger: accidental hard-paywall bug → ~+25% overnight, made permanent (superwall--20250815). Cardstock: free+sub switch was the biggest jump ever, then hard paywall → ~$30k/mo; users "bought sight unseen at the first paywall" (superwall--20251128, App Store Connect shown). Nicole: hard-gate-immediately-after-onboarding beat let-them-browse by +50% conversion (superwall--20260511). Massive: waitlist converted ~0.5%; "charge from day one" (superwall--20250803). Echo risk: severe — 47/64 transcripts are Superwall-ecosystem content and hard-paywall survivorship is the house genre; the soft-paywall failures don't get booked. Still, five separate before/after flips in the same direction is real signal for utility-shaped apps.
- **Annual default, simple plan menu (5 sources).** Sunflower's ladder: monthly → +annual → annual-as-default-selected → pay-upfront-with-trial-toggle, each step adding revenue (superwall--20260323). Pingo: $99.99/yr hard-pushed, monthly hidden behind "view all plans" with no trial (superwall--20260419). Sway: annual default = the LTV posture (superwall--20260505). Parra: 2 plans beat 3; order by duration; highlight annual; generic "Continue" beat descriptive CTA (+111% combined test); bullet-list single-page paywall is the cross-category benchmark; comparison tables lose (spine/01, superwall--20260208). Cal AI $30/yr "most take yearly"; Quittr and Clear30 majority-yearly (thebrettway--20241128/20250210, superwall--20250828).
- **Benchmarks cluster (2–3 sources each).** Trial-start ≥15% good / 34% best-in-class; trial→paid ≥30% / 50–55% best; pay-now install→paid ≥10% really good, 4–10% okay, <4% problem; multi-step paywall +20–40% (all Vahe, superwall--20251116 + spine/09). Parra: average paywall ~8%, 15–20% after iterations; ~50% of trial-starts cancel (spine/01). Independent corroboration at the app level: Glow download→trial ~14% / trial→paid ~31% (arthurspalanzani--20260110, dashboards); Copia paywall→trial 15–20% (superwall--20251022); Locked ~10% download→sub (superwall--20260607); year-1 resubscribe 50/40/30/20 = amazing/good/ok/bad (superwall--20260708); health/fitness annual renewal ~30% (thebrettway--20260302).

## Conflicts

- **Hard gate vs. let-them-in.** Position A: hard paywall, sell the outcome, never show features (Cal AI, Quittr, Nicole's +50% A/B, Cardstock). Position B: "if you do a hard paywall it's because your app is bad" (Copia's Phoenix, superwall--20251022); "not thinking big enough" (Symmetry, arthurspalanzani--20260702); declinable paywall + retention-led conversion (Jungle, superwall--20251102); Fitted ripping its hard paywall out (superwall--20251121). What it's really about: **retention and capital, not paywall ideology.** Every soft-paywall advocate is either VC-cushioned, network-effect-shaped, or lacks PMF (Fitted admits no PMF; Jungle concedes retention-led monetization needs cash-flow patience a bootstrapper doesn't have). Utility apps with day-one value monetize the gate; Crave is the latter.
- **Show the product before the wall?** Position A: paywall before the aha — Sway A/B'd photos-before vs after and paywall-first won; Nicole's +50%; Quittr never shows features. Position B: gate at the moment of value — Halo AI's skippable-then-hard double paywall converted 16.5% combined, beating either alone (superwall--20260329); Coconote's second paywall after a full recorded lecture was "a big win" (superwall--20260412); Sprout gates on the first right-swipe. The reconciliation is in Sway's own concession: paywall-first won _because their aha required user effort_ (upload photos); "if you can show the aha cheaply pre-paywall, that's better" (superwall--20260505). Crave can show real Austin rankings for free at zero marginal cost — the corpus's own logic says the conflict resolves differently for Crave than for Cal AI.
- **Onboarding length: 15 seconds vs 40 questions.** NGL: 15–20s, one action per screen, straight to the core loop (thebrettway--20250325, spine/08). Cal AI/I Am-style: dozens of quiz screens. Resolved by business model: NGL serves a free viral loop where friction kills K-factor; the quiz serves a hard paywall where friction builds commitment. Secondary axis nobody but Pepai names: **age** — under-25s abandon long onboarding, so Pepai branches flow length on an age question (superwall--20260628). Crave's paying demo (25+ with dining budget) sits on the long-quiz side.
- **Decline-cascade orthodoxy vs. the Apple pull.** The scene's standard kit — X-out → drawer reframe → 25–33% one-time offer (Parra, spine/01), transaction-abandonment discounts annual-only + spin-the-wheel (Vahe, superwall--20251116), 50%-off decline paywall (Nicole), ~80%-off abandonment (PushScroll), 55%-off abandoned-cart + longer-trial re-offers (Pingo), exit/abandoned-cart paywalls (Halo) — sits squarely in the decliner-re-prompt + divide-down class that Apple briefly pulled Cal AI over in April 2026 (Guideline 3.1.2(c)/3.1.1/5.6; business-model.md Apple-proofing rules 1–4) — **[external fact, web-verified 2026-07-12 — not from the corpus; the cited transcripts contain no such event]**. Nobody in the corpus reports or updates on this event: no raw states it, and the framing that guests before/after some "pull" keep teaching the same mechanics is not a corpus finding, just a plausible chronology given the external date. The disagreement is not empirical (the lifts are probably real) — it's about who bears tail risk. A solo founder with a hard-gated app bears all of it.

## Noise

- **Weekly-plan wins** (Halo $8.99/wk, PushScroll weekly>monthly, Social Wizard $9.99/wk, Glam Up weekly-only, NGL $1–8/wk): artifacts of impulse/teen/single-session categories; Parra and Blue Throne both note weekly fits short-duration need and damages LTV/exit math. Crave is repeat-use; discard.
- **Claim's trial removal** (34% trial-start, 50–55% trial→paid, then paid-only made more): requires high volume + desperate intent + proven funnel — the wrong lesson for a zero-brand-trust city launch (superwall--20251116).
- **PrayScreen's paywall→ads pivot** (2.5% conversion, 60% D30 on a 4×/day ritual): retention shape Crave doesn't have; his only paywall advice was "show another paywall" (superwall--20250928).
- **Fitted removing its hard paywall**: symptom of no PMF ($0.25/download lifetime — a **derived** figure, $150k revenue ÷ 600k downloads, distinct from the raw's separately-stated 1–15¢ acquisition-cost figures), not evidence against hard gates for retentive products (superwall--20251121).
- **Nicole's dark kit** — fake pre-paywall scan (inference only after payment), blurred results, forced "I rated it" attestation: conversion-effective and trust-toxic; for an objectivity-branded data product these are brand poison and 5.6-adjacent (superwall--20260511).
- **"80% of effort goes to onboarding+paywall"** and "422 profitable paywall experiments" framing: the host sells paywall tooling; directionally fine, but the corpus structurally over-weights paywall work vs. distribution because that's the sponsor's product.
- **Vahe's 1.1%→4% case study**: rode 40–50k organic installs/mo — the paywall work is real, the distribution position isn't Crave's (superwall--20251116).
- **Session-start / every-N-actions placement checklists**: mostly moot for a gate-everything app — the wall IS the app entry.
- **Social Wizard's rigged onboarding demo** — an LLM scores every user's practice reply but is capped at 50% so everyone "fails" before the pitch (thebrettway--20250904, first-party admission): deceptive-by-design, and incompatible with an evidence-receipted integrity brand; discard the mechanic, not the underlying "let them see the product working" instinct.
- **Darcy's paid-upfront (no subscription, no paywall infra) model** (arthurspalanzani--20260523): "let the App Store do the monetization" instead of building a paywall at all. Structurally incompatible with a continuously-updating, infra-costed Score — but the psychological insight behind it ("subscription fatigue makes a clean one-time/upfront ask feel like relief") is worth echoing in paywall _copy_, not architecture.

## Crave transfer

**The v1 paywall spec is effectively dictated by the corpus, and it's compliant:** single-page bullet-list benchmark (USP + 3–5 bullets + social proof), 56–65pt "Continue" CTA with chevron, "No commitment, cancel anytime" line, two plans ordered by duration with **annual default-selected and labeled "Try it free" — with $39.99/yr as the most prominent number** (rule 1), inline auto-renew terms + a Blinkist trial timeline ending in "we'll remind you before your trial ends" (Apple literally sends that reminder for Crave's store-managed intro trial — Crave gets the corpus's best-measured lift mechanism for free), and a Clear30-style price-transparency screen before the wall. Real-app video paywall = test #2. Keep the 17-step quiz; add a progress bar + commitment screen (Glow's 74%→83%), mirror quiz answers back with social proof (Clear30), and wire quiz answers into paywall copy ("your taco-obsessed Austin profile is ready" — spine/06).

**What changes if X:** if install→paid on the monthly pay-now path runs <4% after real traffic (Vahe's "problem" line), the corpus's remedy stack is: richer pre-wall preview of live Austin rankings (the Sway concession + Halo 16.5% pattern), then trial-toggle mechanics (~10% of users buy outright when offered the choice), then price/packaging tests — _not_ decline discounts. If trial→paid runs <30% with a good trial-start rate, that's a product/aha problem (Parra's diagnostic), not a paywall problem.

**Discipline:** don't burn pre-launch weeks on paywall A/B — no traffic means no significance (Parra's best-ROI clients are ≥$100k/mo; Alexey's floor is ~100 purchases/variant; Stronger only trusts macro tests). Ship the benchmark, instrument trial-start / trial→paid / install→paid from day one, test only drastic changes early. And hold the compliance line as a hard constraint: no divide-downs more prominent than the billed price, no decliner re-prompts, no fake scarcity, no forced ratings — the expected value of a +20% decline-flow lift is negative when the downside is removal of a one-app, hard-gated business.

**Adversarial panel must still resolve:** (1) exactly how much live Austin data to show pre-wall — the corpus's two best A/Bs point opposite directions and neither tested a data-density product; (2) whether Apple-sanctioned StoreKit win-back/cancel-flow offers (Coconote's 27% cancel-flow save) fall inside or outside Crave's own "never re-prompt a decliner" rule; (3) whether $7.99 pay-now monthly should exist at all at launch vs. annual-only-with-trial, given ~10% skip-trial demand and the monthly path carrying the whole <4% risk; (4) the age question — whether to adopt Pepai-style flow branching now or post-launch.
