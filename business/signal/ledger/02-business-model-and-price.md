# 02. Business Model & Price

## Where the evidence points

The corpus is loud on the mechanics of a hard paywall and nearly silent on whether Crave's _category_ should have one — most guests are AI-wow utilities where the paywall is the only moat, whereas Crave's data-density moat exists independent of billing. Within that limit, the hard-paywall camp is coherent and well-evidenced: Cal AI, Quittr, Glow, and Pepai all converged on "$X0/yr or $Y/mo, trial on annual only" with founders who tried the freemium alternative and reversed it (Cal AI's GrindClock; Quittr's own A/B). Crave's $7.99/mo·$39.99/yr·annual-only-trial structure sits almost exactly on top of two of those (Glow's $10/$40, Pepai's $10/$40) — convergent, but also an echo chamber: all four are Superwall-ecosystem guests who watch the same podcast and copy each other's screenshots (Parra's own testimony admits this — a paywall that wins in one app "can flounder completely" in another). The strongest _outside_ challenge isn't soft-vs-hard, it's price _level_: Blue Throne's M&A lens argues the post-ChatGPT ceiling is $20/mo and that under-$10 pricing is bootstrap-hostile because every install is scarce with no ad budget — a real tension with $7.99/mo that the panel must adjudicate, not dismiss. Weekly plans are the one place the corpus is actually unanimous: they win only for impulse/short-duration/price-sensitive-young categories (NGL, PushScroll, Halo AI, RizzGPT, Locked), never for adult repeat-use utilities — Crave's no-weekly call is the best-supported single decision in this section. Trial mechanics (annual-only, Blinkist-style reminder timeline, hide-the-duration framing) are cheap, well-replicated, and safe to adopt wholesale. Regional pricing is real machinery (NGL's GDP tiers, Price Localize PPP) but irrelevant at a single-US-city launch — park it. The capital-velocity argument (annual cash upfront funds the next month's growth) is the most honest fit for a bootstrapper with no ad budget, and it's also the argument Crave's own "loosen-risk overridden for capital-return speed" decision already encodes.

## Consensus (≥2 independent sources)

- **Hard paywall beats freemium for a utility with no pre-existing loyalty, tested head-to-head.** Cal AI (GrindClock A/B: free+ads→premium reversed to hard paywall, "more revenue... fewer downloads," `thebrettway--20241128--zach-yadegari-cal-ai.md`), Quittr ("no one used the app when it was free, everyone used it when it was a hard paywall," `thebrettway--20250210--quittr-hard-paywall-influencer-cpm.md`), Stronger (accidental hard-paywall bug → kept it, "~25% boost," `superwall--20250815--fade-in-viral-format-600k.md`), TikTok-slideshow budget app (switched hard, "sales comes crazy," `arthurspalanzani--20260623--tiktok-slideshows-100k-downloads.md`). Quality: three first-party A/B claims + one anecdote, all self-reported, all survivors. Echo risk: moderate — all Superwall-adjacent, all incentivized to sell the tactic.
- **Annual-only trial + a large monthly/annual price gap is the dominant structural pattern**, and it is nearly identical to Crave's own: Cal AI $30/yr·$10/mo (`thebrettway--20241128...`), Glow $40/yr·$10/mo (`arthurspalanzani--20260110-glow-affirmation-app-2k-challenge.md`), Pepai $40/yr·$10/mo (`superwall--20260628--pepai-peptide-app-100k.md`), Pingo $99.99/yr with monthly hidden and trial-less (`superwall--20260419--pingo-500k-creator-viral-playbook.md`). Quality: claimed, first-party self-report, no independent verification. Echo risk: HIGH — these four are the same podcast ecosystem citing each other and Cal AI explicitly.
- **Weekly plans are a category-fit decision, not a universal lever.** Works: NGL (GDP-tiered $1–8/wk, `thebrettway--20250325--ngl-250m-downloads-organic-virality.md`), PushScroll (weekly beat monthly, "only a little extra ARPU," under-18-skewed, `superwall--20251018--pushscroll-30k-organic-playbook.md`), Halo AI ($8.99/wk beat annual, self-admits entertainment demand "not lasting," `superwall--20260329--300k-mrr-ugc-machine.md`), Locked ($30/yr or $7/wk teen impulse app, `superwall--20260607--14-year-old-14k-month-ai-app.md`), RizzGPT ($7/wk flat, `thebrettway--20240801--blake-anderson-rizzgpt-umax-cal.md`). Doesn't work / explicitly rejected: Blue Throne ("fine cash-flow business, bad acquisition asset," only for short-duration-need categories, `superwall--20260708--blue-throne-six-rules-exit.md`), Parra ("declining for social/multiplayer," born of AI-wrapper churn-and-burn, `superwall--20260208--4000-paywalls-lessons.md`). Quality: mixed claimed/first-party. Echo risk: low — this is genuine disagreement resolved by category, not a copied talking point.
- **The Blinkist-style trial-timeline paywall (unlock → mid-trial reminder → billing-day notice) is a durable, multi-year winner with no reported half-life.** Clear 30 (`superwall--20250828--clear30-quit-weed-app.md`), Parra (`superwall--20260208--4000-paywalls-lessons.md`), Vahe/Claim (`superwall--20251116--paywall-playbook-50m-experiments.md`), Pingo ("took it from a Superwall podcast," `superwall--20260419--pingo-500k-creator-viral-playbook.md`), Sunflower (+46–70% claimed on adding the priming screen, inconsistent numbers 56% vs 48% within the same interview, `superwall--20260323--sunflower-1m-arr-10-months.md`). Quality: claimed, no baselines given anywhere. Echo risk: HIGH — this is the single most copied mechanic in the whole corpus.
- **~50% of trial-starts cancel before conversion, industry-wide.** Parra states this explicitly as an aggregate (`superwall--20260208--4000-paywalls-lessons.md`); Vahe's Claim case (34% trial-start, 50–55% trial-to-paid) is consistent with roughly half converting (`superwall--20251116--paywall-playbook-50m-experiments.md`). Quality: claimed aggregate, no sample size. Not really independent (same consultant ecosystem) but internally consistent.

## Conflicts

- **Hard paywall (Cal AI, Quittr, Glow, Pepai) vs. explicit anti-hard-paywall (Symmetry, `arthurspalanzani--20260702--symmetry-billion-views-ugc-machine.md`).** Mauro's thesis: "people think they can make more money with a hard paywall... that's true if you have a shitty product... but they are not thinking big enough" — cites Tinder/Duolingo/Spotify. This is really a disagreement about **product category and time horizon**: Symmetry is a daily-habit fitness+social app betting on years of retention-driven ARPU growth; the hard-paywall cohort are all single-session-value utilities that need revenue on day one because there's no habit loop to wait for. Crave is closer to Symmetry's _retention_ profile (repeat-use, local, no cold-start) but closer to the hard-paywall cohort's _capital position_ (bootstrapped, no runway to wait years) — the corpus doesn't resolve this combination for us.
- **Hard-gate-everything (Cal AI et al.) vs. soft-then-hard hybrid gated by cost, not category (YourMove AI, `superwall--20260104--yourmove-ai-seo-30k.md`; Jungle, `superwall--20251102--ai-study-app-100k-mo.md`; Halo AI's double-paywall, `superwall--20260329--300k-mrr-ugc-machine.md`).** YourMove keeps its core soft and hard-gates only the AI-photos feature because "the compute cost... is quite high" — a _unit-economics_ argument, not a philosophical one. Halo AI's skippable-then-hard-gate-at-first-action combo has each individual paywall converting at 16.5%, and the combined two-paywall funnel converts higher than either alone (exact combined figure not stated). This directly questions Crave's gate-everything-at-onboarding-end design: none of Crave's core surfaces (search, map, Score) carry Cal-AI-scale inference cost, so the cost-based argument for hard-gating doesn't obviously transfer, but the psychological argument (let people taste the Score before the wall) is untested for Crave specifically.
- **$29.99–39.99/yr "market-settled" AI-wrapper band (Parra, `superwall--20260208--4000-paywalls-lessons.md`) vs. the ChatGPT-$20/mo price-ceiling-reset argument (Blue Throne, `superwall--20260708--blue-throne-six-rules-exit.md`).** Josh's math: $4.99/mo → 500k payers vs $20/mo → 125k payers for the same $30M ARR, and "the cheapest path... is fewer payers at higher prices" — most load-bearing precisely for a founder with no UA budget, because every install is scarce. Waking Up ($20/mo/$150/yr, ~25k downloads/mo → $1M+/mo, a 10x-normal downloads-to-revenue ratio) is his proof case. This is really a disagreement about **whether the product carries $20/mo urgency**: Waking Up has a celebrity-authority moat Crave doesn't have; Parra's $29.99–39.99/yr band is calibrated to AI-wrapper inference-cost economics Crave doesn't share either (Crave's data-density value doesn't map to either precedent cleanly — it needs its own ceiling test, not an imported anchor).
- **Trial-removal as the mature end-state (Claim, `superwall--20251116--paywall-playbook-50m-experiments.md`) vs. trial-as-mandatory-trust-signal for a new brand.** Vahe is explicit about preconditions: high install volume + 34% trial-start + 50–55% trial-to-paid + an acutely-motivated user _before_ removing the trial. Crave at launch has none of these proven yet (zero installs, unmeasured funnel, no urgent pain point comparable to a legal settlement). This is a **sequencing** disagreement, not a real conflict — the corpus itself gates the tactic behind metrics Crave hasn't earned.
- **Cal AI's own two eras contradict each other on aggression.** The pre-acquisition playbook (spine `06`) is the hard-paywall template everyone copies; the post-acquisition interview (spine `07`, `thebrettway--20260302--zach-yadegari-cal-ai-sale.md`) is a pre-sale victory lap and contains **no mention** of an Apple pull — that claim is not in this or any cited transcript. Apple did briefly pull Cal AI in April 2026 for divide-down pricing and a trial toggle obscuring auto-renewal (Guideline 3.1.2(c)/3.1.1/5.6; the app returned after fixes), but this is **[external fact, web-verified 2026-07-12 — not from the corpus; TechCrunch/MacRumors/9to5Mac, 2026-04-21]**, not something any guest reports. The panel must not average the pre- and post-acquisition eras into "aggressive monetization worked" — it worked AND (per that external record) got the app briefly removed; Crave's Apple-proofing rules (business-model.md) already draw this line on independently-sourced Guideline exposure, but every "steal this paywall" testimony in the corpus predates and is silent on the pull.

## Noise (discard for Crave)

- **Regional/GDP-tiered pricing (NGL, Glow, Price-Localize prayer app, TikTok-slideshow app) — not applicable at launch.** All four are global or multi-country apps; Crave launches in one US city. Worth a bookmark for the second-city-abroad question, zero signal now.
- **Symmetry's "lower ARPU, lower cost, same margin" Spain-first argument — geography mismatch, not a pricing lesson.** It's an argument about _labor and content cost_ scaling with market GDP, unrelated to what Crave should charge in Austin.
- **Darcy's paid-upfront, no-subscription model (`arthurspalanzani--20260523--paid-upfront-app-facebook-groups.md`) — wrong monetization shape entirely.** Crave has an ongoing collection/inference cost structure that a one-time purchase can't fund; Darcy's thesis ("subscription fatigue, breath of fresh air") is the opposite of what Crave is selling. Keep as a reminder that _some_ segment resents subscriptions, not as a pricing option.
- **Sunflower's "subscriptions are dying, apps become lead magnets for services" hot take (`superwall--20260323--sunflower-1m-arr-10-months.md`) — VC-narrative, not evidence.** Kobe explicitly concedes Sunflower's playbook required $6.5M in VC funding he says was necessary; this is a vision statement from a raised founder, irrelevant to a bootstrapper's pricing decision.
- **Any single-app conversion-rate or ARPU number presented without a baseline** (Parra's 10%/111%/8→15-20% lifts, Vahe's 1.1%→4% case, Halo AI's 16.5%) — these are vendor-marketing aggregates from consultants and podcast sponsors selling the tooling that produced them (Superwall itself), with no sample sizes or independent audit anywhere in the corpus. Use as _directional_ inputs to a revenue model, never as a target to hit.
- **Discord's "free forever, monetize status not utility" arc (`thebrettway--20241229--discord-200m-user-growth.md`) — wrong company entirely.** A VC-funded network-effects platform choosing not to gate its core loop says nothing about whether a bootstrapped local-utility app should gate its core loop; the guest's own framing (people pay to be selfless/for status among friends) has no analog in Crave's single-player Score.

## Crave transfer

The fact sheet already reflects the strongest reading of this evidence: hard-gate-everything, $7.99/mo pay-now or $39.99/yr with a ~1-week annual-only trial. That structure is directly supported by the two closest analogs in price _and_ mechanic (Glow, Pepai), by the general hard-paywall-beats-freemium consensus, and by the capital-velocity logic every annual-first testimony repeats (Quittr: yearly cash "is easy to scale"; Vahe: default-annual captures upfront cash) — which matters specifically because Crave has no ad budget and needs its own reinvestment engine. What changes if the panel takes Blue Throne's ChatGPT-reset challenge seriously: plan an explicit price-ceiling test cohort (10–20% of new subscribers pushed to a higher annual price, e.g. $59.99–79.99/yr) rather than treating $39.99/yr as settled — Crave's dish-level data density is a real, non-AI-wrapper moat that the $29.99–39.99 band was never calibrated for, and under-$10/mo may be leaving bootstrap-critical margin on the table. What Crave should NOT copy from its closest analogs: the Cal AI aggression (divide-down pricing, decliner re-prompts) that sits in Apple's 3.1.2(c)/3.1.1/5.6 enforcement zone — already excluded by business-model.md's Apple-proofing rules, and correctly so: Apple briefly pulled Cal AI over exactly this in April 2026 **[external fact, web-verified 2026-07-12 — not from the corpus; the cited corpus transcripts contain no such event]**, not a finding the corpus itself reports. The YourMove/Halo AI soft-then-hard-gate pattern is worth a real experiment (not a philosophy import): Crave's core surfaces don't share Cal-AI-scale inference cost, so there's no unit-economics reason to hard-gate before first value, only an unproven psychological one — a browse-then-gate-at-first-search variant is cheap to A/B against the current design once there's install volume to test with.

**What an adversarial panel must still resolve:**

1. Does Crave's dish-level data density carry ChatGPT-tier ($20/mo) willingness-to-pay, or is $7.99/mo already close to this category's ceiling — and how would we actually run that price-ceiling test pre-launch with zero installs?
2. Is gate-everything-at-onboarding-end the right placement, or does Crave's category (a browsable, visual product with no per-query inference cost) argue for a taste-then-gate hybrid the way Halo AI's double paywall (16.5% per paywall, combined higher) suggests?
3. Which renewal number should the model bracket against — Cal AI's ~30% (discipline-demanding health/fitness, per Zach, `thebrettway--20260302--zach-yadegari-cal-ai-sale.md`) or business-model.md's ~44%-at-12-months RevenueCat lifestyle-category benchmark — and does Crave's repeat-use-utility profile argue it should land above BOTH, given the corpus's own claim that local-density retention curves look nothing like AI-wrapper churn?
4. Is the annual-only-trial / monthly-pay-now-no-trial split (mirroring Riz's trial-toggle economics) actually optimal, or does Crave's zero-brand-trust city launch argue for offering the same short trial on the monthly path too, at least until funnel metrics are strong enough to earn Vahe's trial-removal preconditions?
