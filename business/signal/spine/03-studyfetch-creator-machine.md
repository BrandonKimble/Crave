## The machine (this is the most complete creator-program blueprint in the corpus)

**Three-lane creator mix, deliberately combined:**

1. **Hundreds of ordinary UGC creators** ($ couple-hundred to couple-thousand/mo) mass-
   posting product content.
2. **Influencer sponsored posts** on their own accounts (occasional, goal-dependent).
3. **The differentiator — influencer-run ZERO-FOLLOWER brand accounts:** pay a ~750k-
   follower niche influencer five figures/month (≈$10k/mo, 20–30 videos/mo retainer) to
   start a brand-new "StudyFetch <name>" account from zero. Their loyal audience follows
   over; trust transfers; the fresh account outperforms everything else. Rationale:
   **"UGC is becoming less and less effective because audiences understand the videos
   are ads"** — the zero-follower influencer account is the authenticity hedge.

**Deal-structure numbers (rare hard data):**

- Influencer separate-account retainers: 5-figures/month, multi-month; 10–20× ordinary
  UGC creator rates. Rates falling as the tactic becomes known.
- Base rate: **$50–$2,500 per video** (they pay premium for talent even w/o followers).
- Bonus: **$100 per 100k views** ("pretty standard across creator programs").
- TikTok Shop creators preferred — affiliate-paid background means they know how to SELL
  outcomes, not just perform.

**Selection criteria:** engagement quality over absolute views — comment density and
whether comments are _about the product/creator relationship_ (e.g. 160k views, 1.6k
bookmarks = good). Influencers whose face = the niche ("when they see her, they think
studying"). Cross-check IG-found creators on TikTok; if weak on TikTok, pass.

**Platform geo mechanics (matters enormously for a city app):**

- TikTok = higher-intent audience than Reels; Reels sprays internationally (viral Reel
  can convert ~zero); US-based creator posting on TikTok → ~80%+ US audience.
- Their rule: non-US creators post TikTok, not Reels. General principle: **the creator's
  own audience geography is the targeting mechanism for organic.**

**Content laws:**

- **Product-as-the-hero converts; product-tacked-on doesn't.** "When the product itself
  is the reason the video does well, you're in the green." A 5-second product cameo at
  the end of an unrelated video converts poorly.
- Features were _designed for the camera_ (live-lecture auto-notes: film screen, hands
  visibly off keyboard = the wow). Product decisions and content strategy co-designed.
- The 75M-view lecture-hall video (Dec 2023) = "hundreds of thousands of signups";
  reproducible only if the product moment is genuinely novel ("the product has to be
  original — nobody had seen this tech in school yet").
- Don't override a creator's native style; let lifestyle creators weave the product in
  2–3× naturally (no hard CTA needed when the audience is curious about what she uses).
- Hard CTA that works: plain-text URL overlay in the final seconds.
- Problem/solution format worked in 2023–24, now saturated.
- Internal dashboards scan trending formats across OTHER niches for idea import.

**Team/ops:** outbound-first creator sourcing (TikTok/IG DMs, email, LinkedIn) — inbound
applications alone select for mediocrity; contractors for creator ops failed (misaligned
incentives); full-time creative managers talk to creators daily. First growth hire for a
non-Gen-Z founder: a _marketing generalist_ (short-form is "just another channel...
formula of trial and error"), ideally with TikTok Shop experience.

## Crave transfer

1. **The zero-follower brand-account mechanic is the single most Crave-relevant idea in
   this transcript** — the local variant: a trusted Austin food creator runs a fresh
   "Crave ATX"-style account. Trust transfer + audience-geography targeting solves the
   "how does UGC target one city" problem better than any spray-and-pray UGC. Austin
   food creators are far smaller than 750k, so the retainer math scales down from $10k/mo
   to something a bootstrapper can pay — and their audience is _naturally_ Austin-heavy
   (the geo analog of "US creator → 80% US audience").
2. **Product-as-hero:** Crave's dish-search demo (type "best birria" → ranked dishes on
   a beautiful map) is inherently filmable wow. The screen-recording demo IS the format.
   Content strategy and product demo-ability should be co-designed, as they did.
3. Their scale numbers came from a UNIVERSAL audience (every student on earth watches
   study content). Crave's addressable viewer pool per city is orders smaller —
   expectations for view counts and the value of a national viral hit must be discounted
   accordingly (a 75M-view video mostly reaches people in cities Crave hasn't seeded).
   This is the strongest corpus argument FOR multi-city data coverage before/alongside
   big UGC pushes — or for creator deals whose audiences are city-concentrated.
4. Deal benchmarks to reuse: $100/100k views bonus, $50+ per-video base for small
   creators, retainer-with-bonus blends, TikTok-Shop-background selection filter.
5. Their "hundreds of creators" phase is a POST-revenue scale move (and post-$11.5M).
   The transferable seed-stage kernel is: founder-made product-demo content first
   (Kieran's own first video), then a handful of carefully-chosen local creators — not
   a creator army.
