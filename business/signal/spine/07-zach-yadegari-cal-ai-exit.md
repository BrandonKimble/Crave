# Spine notes — Zach Yadegari sells Cal AI to MyFitnessPal (thebrettway, 2026-03-02)

**Who/when:** Zach at 18, the day before announcing the acquisition. Cal AI 2025
revenue = $30M; Jan 2026 = $5.7M (≈$50M ARR run rate); ~30 FTE + VA team; sold 100%,
no retained equity, ~1.5 years launch→sale. **Critical timeline note: Apple pulled
Cal AI in April 2026 — one month AFTER this interview** (Guideline 3.1.2(c)/5.6, the
divide-down pricing + decliner re-prompts; see business-model.md Apple-proofing).
The playbook sold the company AND got the app pulled. Both facts are real; the
blueprint must keep both.

## The growth arc ($1M/mo → $5.7M/mo) — what changed

1. **Influencer-only hit a ceiling ≈ $2M/mo:** they'd worked every big fitness
   influencer, and fitness audiences overlap heavily — diminishing bang per deal.
   Broadened to non-fitness influencers (helped some). Lesson: a single niche's
   influencer pool saturates; the overlap is invisible until you've paid for it.
2. **The real unlock = performance ads** (TikTok/IG/FB) — but only AFTER the organic
   era built awareness: "we were so big with influencers before running paid ads…
   people that have seen us all over social media — finally, time to buy." Brett's
   framing, Zach's agreement: **organic builds the goodwill; paid ads liquidate it.**
3. Affiliate creator program (Tribe, % of revenue, WhatsApp comms) layered on top.

**Paid-ads specifics (first-party, expensive lessons):**

- Agency first → capped at $5k/day, badly structured; took it in-house ("an agency
  will never care as much"). Simple structure: ONE scale campaign (CBO) + ONE testing
  campaign. **No interest targeting, no lookalikes — "you target with creative";**
  even retargeting is done in the copy ("you've probably seen our ads before").
- **Subtle influencer integrations FAIL as paid ads.** The organic magic (3-second
  cameo in a day-in-my-life) doesn't convert under paid distribution. Winning ad
  creative = the whole video about the app, direct-selling, 5 seconds in, often no
  talking — visual demo + hook caption ("how I'm cutting for the summer"). That flip
  broke them from ~$10–15k/day ceiling to ~$40k/day (and later $7k→much more).
- Optimize the pixel for **trial start**, not purchase — Apple privacy kills signals
  older than ~24h and their trial is 3 days; model true conversion off-platform.
- Attribution sauce: **App Store custom product pages** per ad campaign → App Store
  Connect shows revenue per page; inflate ~30% for watch-then-search users. AppsFlyer
  "never really" delivers; incrementality tests (channel off/on) are the honest tool.
- MrBeast: $500k for a behind-the-scenes integration — direct ROAS slightly
  unprofitable, but the brand authority unlocked better creator deals afterward.

**Benchmarks:** annual health/fitness subscription **year-1 renewal ≈ 30%** (industry
average per Zach). $30/yr price held all the way to $50M ARR. Bootstrapped = must be
profitable immediately, every channel.

**Org lessons:** $1k/mo geo-arbitrage devs = quality ceiling; hiring real talent was
the inflection where "the company started growing on its own." Founder absence for a
month → morale + numbers dipped. Speed as the #1 stated core value ("get Frank on the
call right now"). Marketing > building ("worst app + amazing marketing still makes
money; perfect app + worst marketing makes zero").

**The extreme of product-for-camera:** they shipped a SECOND build of the app for
creators — giant CalAI wordmark on every page + a fake perfect 30-day streak — the
"McDonald's burger" version that exists only to be filmed.

**Caveat he volunteers:** all this sequencing applies to pay-per-user subscription
apps. "If it's a social media platform this advice would be completely different."

## Crave transfer

1. The canonical channel sequencing is now fully specified by its best practitioner:
   founder content → creators/influencers (organic) → repost archive → paid ads with
   DIRECT-RESPONSE creative once awareness exists → ASA competitor keywords. Crave's
   blueprint should adopt the ladder but expect the influencer-ceiling dynamic to
   arrive much earlier in a single city (Austin's food-influencer pool is small and
   its audiences overlap heavily — the $2M/mo saturation lesson scaled down).
2. Trial-start pixel optimization + custom-product-pages attribution are directly
   reusable mechanics whenever Crave turns on any paid spend.
3. 30% annual renewal (discipline-demanding category) vs our business-model.md's 44%
   (RevenueCat lifestyle data) — bracket Crave's model with both.
4. "Organic first, ads liquidate the goodwill" resolves WHEN ads enter the blueprint:
   not at launch; after Austin knows the name.
5. The pull-after-the-playbook point: Cal AI's monetization aggression produced BOTH
   the $30M year and the April-2026 removal. Crave copies the machine, not the
   divide-down/decliner-reprompt tactics (already codified in business-model.md).
