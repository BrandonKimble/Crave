# Spine notes — Zach Yadegari, Cal AI at $1.12M/mo, age 17 (thebrettway, 2024-11-28)

**Who:** Zach Yadegari — Cal AI co-founder/CEO (photo→calories app; $1.14M Oct-2024
revenue shown live in App Store Connect; 6 months post-launch; 50% profit margin;
bootstrapped; 12 staff + 4 co-founders incl. Blake Anderson of Umax/RizzGPT). Prior:
"Totally Science" unblocked-games site ($60k/yr, sold $100k at 16, grown to 5M users
via his own TikToks). **This is the origin playbook the whole Superwall ecosystem
quotes** — and the closest thing to the source of Crave's own hard-paywall decision.
Evidence: first-party, receipts shown on camera. Interview predates the April-2026
Apple pull and the MFP acquisition — read spine/07 for how the story ends.

## The claims that matter

**Marketing-before-product validation:** they picked Cal AI from several ideas
_because_ they knew exactly how it would blow up — "hundreds of health & fitness
influencers whose audiences would love the app." Rule: if there's no niche/influencer
ecosystem to tap, growth is 10× harder. Product must be **inherently viral**: a
2-second visual wow (photo → calories) that needs no explanation.

**The influencer DM machine (his numbers):**

- 1 in 100 influencers answer DMs; 500 DMs → ~2 good ones. DM beats email (email
  reaches the manager; rates jump). Scale: **150 influencers on monthly retainers**,
  paying **$100/mo to $100k/2-months** per creator.
- Sourcing: create a fresh TikTok account, engage only with niche content until the
  FYP becomes a creator-sourcing feed; VAs scrape into sheets. (Now productized as
  viral.tech.)
- **Deal math = RPM vs CPM.** Estimate revenue-per-1k-views (start guessing $5 RPM),
  pay $2–3 CPM flat-upfront based on the creator's trailing average views. Creators
  won't take pure CPM (except tiny ones); they want a number — so give them their
  number but stretch the video count ("$4k means $4k, I don't care how many videos"),
  bundle 4 videos upfront for discounts, add minimum-view clauses (keep posting until
  guaranteed views hit). "Creators are a dime a dozen — for every one there are 12
  identical." Engagement quality (real comment communities, not engagement-pod rings)
  adjusts what you'll pay.
- Attribution = overlay posting times/views against App Store Connect revenue curves.
  Link tracking is dead (viewers go straight to the App Store).

**Content law — the seamless integration:** NO script reads, no "this video is
sponsored by." "What I eat in a day" videos where Cal AI is never _named_, just used —
the app name is **deliberately placed at the top of the UI** so it's legible on
camera; viewers ask "what app is that?" in comments; the team **plants and pins
comments** and replies to everyone. Only hard rule: product on screen in the first
15 seconds. This is product-design-for-camera, same as StudyFetch.

**Hard-paywall philosophy (the origin of ours):** "We are a premium-only app — you
can't use the app without paying — but we do have a free trial [3-day]." Learned from
Grindclock: free-with-ads + premium upsell → flipped to paid-only → **more revenue on
fewer downloads**. "Once people are used to something being free, they're used to it
being free." Utility apps "shouldn't be scared to just hard paywall." Pricing:
**$30/yr or $10/mo, most take yearly.**

**Onboarding psychology (A/B-proven):** questions with _zero functional purpose_
raise paywall conversion — asking "why are you doing this / what's your goal" makes
users internalize the need and sunk-cost the flow before the wall. (Crave's
onboarding quiz steps are this pattern already.)

**Growth sequencing (his explicit recipe):**

1. Influencer/UGC to get off the ground (highest profitability, easiest start).
2. Repost every influencer video on owned brand accounts — tens of millions of free
   views/mo (works on IG; TikTok flags duplicates).
3. Paid ads only AFTER the creative archive exists ("90% of paid ads is creatives";
   the influencer archive is the moat) — $7k/day on Meta, profitable daily.
4. Apple Search Ads on competitor keywords — doubles as product-quality validation
   (profitable on a rival's keyword ⇒ you're the better product).
5. (Considering TV.) Also: Apple pays ~2 months late — cash-flow squeeze while scaling.

**Misc:** single-prompt GPT scan was already 80% accurate (= FDA label tolerance);
consumers expected x-ray vision — first-scan education flow needed. Grindclock left
unmarketed flatlined (churn ≈ organic inflow) for a year. Multi-app studio logic:
amortize the influencer network across apps; bundle deals to cut CPM.

## Crave transfer

1. **Crave passes Zach's validation test loudly:** food is one of the largest creator
   ecosystems on earth, including _city-specific_ food creators — the exact
   pre-existing audience infrastructure he demands. And Crave's demo (type "best
   birria" → ranked map of dishes) is a 2-second visual wow.
2. **The RPM/CPM frame quantifies the one-city question.** Crave's RPM is
   geo-diluted: a national food video's viewers are ~1–3% Austin, so revenue-per-1k-
   views collapses unless (a) the creator's audience is Austin-concentrated, (b) the
   content's city-hook makes the algorithm geo-sort, or (c) more cities are live.
   The multi-city decision should be modeled AS an RPM multiplier, not vibes.
3. Name-on-screen UI placement, planted/pinned comments, first-15-seconds rule,
   DM-not-email, bundle-and-retainer negotiation — all directly liftable tactics.
4. His hard-paywall+trial+$30/yr+most-take-annual = the exact template Crave adopted.
   Caveats for the panel: Cal AI is a habit/outcome app with instant single-session
   wow and a UNIVERSAL audience; and this interview is pre-Apple-pull (the pull was
   for HOW they sold, not THAT they hard-paywalled — see business-model.md
   Apple-proofing rules).
5. Onboarding-investment psychology validates Crave's quiz steps; consider wiring
   quiz answers into the paywall screen copy ("your taco-obsessed profile is ready").
