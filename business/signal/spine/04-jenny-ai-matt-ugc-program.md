## The claims that matter

**Creator sourcing heresy (the anti-StudyFetch position):** don't hire influencers or
look at stats at all — scout **"camera charisma"** in completely unrelated niches
(beauty, lifestyle, pets) and train them. Checklist: (1) emotive delivery — especially
the _eyes_ (they have enough volume to hypothesis-test this; more eye-emotion = more
views), (2) aesthetic first frame (proxy for innate "viral sense": lighting, framing,
even on-screen text proportions), (3) extroverted natural storyteller ("a yapper").
Best creator: beauty-niche, zero UGC history, took **25 videos to crack 10k views**,
then earned $5k+/mo in bonuses. Patience + coaching relationship > transactional. You
get _much better deal terms_ because you're not paying for follower counts.

**Founder-led content first — corpus consensus point #2 (Kieran said the same):**
his own first-ever TikTok (borrowed "absurdly long URL" format, adapted not copied)
did 245k views and later got him acquired. "If you're not willing to do it, it's hard
to expect others to." It also solves the creator-outreach chicken-egg: **you can't
recruit creators without a track record to point at** ("it's easier now with 75M views
than getting the first 10"). Cold DM craft: short, casual, all-lowercase for Gen-Z,
_specific_ social proof, no links.

**Outreach economics:** response rates from non-influencer hidden gems are LOWER than
influencer outreach (they don't check DMs, they're skeptical of scam offers) but
close-rate on call is high. 80 creators in month 1 = "violent amount of effort"
(16-hr days) — no shortcut to the doomscroll-crawl that finds hidden gems.

**Viral sense as a discipline:** prescribed doomscrolling — 30 min/day IN niche +
30 min/day OUT of niche (out-of-niche formats are the innovation source). Never copy
frame-by-frame (a competitor cloned outfits + backgrounds pixel-for-pixel and flopped);
ask "what one element can I do BETTER" and iterate 100×. Luck is real and dominant at
the margin: same script, same creator, 3 days apart → **2,000× view difference**.
Optimize surface area for luck; don't assign causality to single outcomes.

**Engagement bait ≠ rage bait (his signature move):** craft content that divides
opinion ~50/50 so defenders battle attackers in the comments (the algorithm feeds on
it). Ruby's 5-second "girl in the library with no tabs, no music, no Jenny AI… like a
psychopath" clip → screenshot → **30M views on X** via quote-tweet wars. The sweet spot
plugs the product inside the controversy. And the discipline: **"a viral study TikTok
that didn't mention Jenny is completely useless"** — views without the product named
are vanity. Harder-plug videos with fewer views often out-convert bigger ones.

**Product-from-format (the inverted pipeline):** their next experiment starts from a
proven TikTok format in another niche and builds the product feature backwards from it.
Public video stats = "spying on product-market fit" / content-market fit.

**Hiring:** for growth roles, acquihire young founders (survival-trained on conversion,
not just creativity); TikTok Shop creators as the affiliate-trained alternative.
