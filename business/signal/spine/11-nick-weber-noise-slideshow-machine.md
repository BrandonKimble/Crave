# Spine notes — Nick Weber, Noise (mass-slideshow distribution) (superwall, 2026-01-25)

**Who:** Nick Weber — co-founder of Noise, a mass-posting platform (50,000 posters;
2.5B views across platform founders in year one; pays posters per view). Ex-Playbite
(app founder; the platform started as their internal growth hack). **Incentives:**
he SELLS the platform ("the alternative to Meta ads"); all numbers self-reported.
But this is the most explicit mechanical model of TikTok distribution in the corpus.

## The system

**Two-pronged doctrine (run simultaneously):**

1. High-effort UGC hunting viral hits with a SOFT CTA → awareness + curiosity.
2. Thousands of cheap slideshow VARIATIONS with a HARD CTA → function as a
   **retargeting layer**: TikTok shows similar content to people who engaged with
   the viral, so the ground-game catches them with the explicit ask. Power law by
   design: virals spark, 500–2,000-view posts convert.

**Shots-on-goal mechanics:** 100k+ posts/day platform-wide; creators DON'T create —
the platform serves fully-baked randomized variations (hook images, captions,
hashtags, ordering); posters just post on schedule. Micro-details decide outcomes:
"don't get a second job" went viral where "everybody needs a side hustle" flopped;
egg photos outperform other food hooks ("eggs theory" — relatable, cheap, healthy,
instantly legible); washing-dishes B-roll worked, vacuuming didn't. Nobody can
predict these — you test at volume. Playbook lifecycle: ~1–3 weeks / few hundred
posts; no hit → pause, slot the next.

**Numbers:** ~**$0.30 installs** average for their own app via the platform; one
campaign ran **$0.08 installs for 3 days** and hit #7 in category. Brand sets the
CPM off its target CPI. Comment-seeding at scale: a planted "I use the noise app
for this" comment with 1,686 likes ≈ 1,000+ downloads. **Videos with 500 views
still convert** — the algorithm routes tiny distributions to precisely the right
people.

**TikTok mechanics theory:** each content space has a **daily views budget** (a
trend has a TAM of interest; flooding a space spreads the same views thinner —
saturation is structural, not moral). Pasting text in-TikTok beats baked-in text
(real-user signal). Copy-don't-create: they hit top-5/top-7 in category twice by
re-skinning proven formats ("great artists steal"); Reddit's popular subreddits
(r/RoastMe) validate content demand before you make the format.

## Crave transfer — this one is big

1. **Crave's ranking data is a native slideshow factory.** "Top 5 tacos in Austin
   locals gatekeep" listicle slideshows are ALREADY a proven, massive local-food
   content genre — and Crave can generate unlimited, defensible, evidence-backed
   variants programmatically (per dish, per neighborhood, per occasion, with score
   cards + photos). The product output IS the ground-game creative. No other app in
   this corpus had a content factory built into its data model.
2. **The "daily views budget" logic FLIPS the geo-dilution problem.** Austin-food
   content has a bounded TikTok audience — but that audience is precisely Austin
   eaters (+ visitors). Hyper-local listicle content self-geo-sorts via the interest
   graph. Volume in a narrow space with hard CTAs beats chasing national virals
   whose viewers can't buy.
3. Two-pronged structure for Crave: founder/creator UGC (demo-wow, ranking
   controversies) = the viral layer; programmatic listicle slideshows with "full
   ranking on Crave" CTAs = the conversion/retargeting layer. Comment-seeding with
   the actual evidence quotes (receipts!) is both honest and potent.
4. Slideshows are trivially cheap to produce (Canva → automated) — a solo founder
   can run this WITHOUT a Noise-scale platform: 2–3 posts/day across a few accounts,
   the Alberto budget-app pattern (3×/day, 6–7 accounts, Spain-first) corroborates
   at indie scale.
5. Caveats: mass-posting/account-farming has ToS and brand-cheapening risk; Crave's
   integrity brand (evidence-backed scores) argues for the honest end of this
   spectrum — accuracy IS our differentiation, so the listicles should be verifiably
   right, not engagement-bait slop.
