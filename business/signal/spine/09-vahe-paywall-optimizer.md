## Benchmarks (the industry's actual numbers)

- **Trial-start rate** (paywall viewers starting trial): >15% good; 8–11% =
  fixable to 15% "in 90% of cases"; best-in-class 34% (Claim, multi-step).
- **Trial→paid:** >30% good; Claim hit 50–55%.
- **No-trial install→paid (US-heavy traffic):** >10% really good; 4–10% okay;
  <4% = a problem. Case study: 1.1% → ~4% in 5 months on identical traffic.
- Multi-step paywall done RIGHT beats single-screen by **20–40%**.
- ~10% of users offered a trial toggle choose to skip the trial and buy now.
- "80% of the effort should go into onboarding + paywall" (the scene's dogma).

## The tactics that matter for us

**Trial-forward framing (for the annual+trial product):** if you offer a trial,
say "free" 5–7 times across the flow; label the annual card **"Try it free"**, not
"Annual plan"; promise the day-before-trial-ends reminder explicitly (kills the
"I'll forget to cancel" anxiety — arguably _omit the trial length from the price
card_ and show it only in the timeline). This is Blinkist-timeline psychology,
consistent with Parra's notes.

**The trial toggle = Crave's dual-plan in miniature:** default trial ON at a higher
price; "skip free trial" flips to a cheaper immediate purchase (Riz: $9.99/wk w/
trial vs $6.99/wk without). High-intent categories don't need the discount; lower-
intent do. Crave's monthly-pay-now vs annual-with-trial is structurally this — his
data says expect ~90% to stay on the trial leg.

**Claim's trial REMOVAL:** with 34% trial-start AND 50–55% trial→paid AND high
install volume AND an acutely-motivated user, removing the trial entirely made
more money. Preconditions all three metrics strong + pressing problem. (Relevant
as a future test only; Crave launch keeps the decided structure.)

**Pricing craft:** non-.99 "authentic" numbers now out-convert .99 endings
(consumers pattern-match .99 as manipulation; $3.33/mo feels real and pads the
annual to $39.96). Anchor annual against an undiscounted monthly. Pricing method
at start: competitor anchor + cost floor + gut, THEN test cohorts later.

**Placement doctrine:** conversion foundation = paywall placements everywhere
before experimentation — onboarding, session start (1/day), every-N-actions,
out-of-quota, trial-cancel offer, sub-cancel offer, transaction abandonment
(discount ONLY the annual product — weekly/monthly discounts "tested dozens of
times, don't work"). Steal conversion mechanics from e-commerce (Temu/Shein);
spin-the-wheel earned-discount beat a plain discount by double digits.

## ⚠️ The compliance line (our own, not his)

Transaction-abandonment counter-offers, session-start re-prompting, "limited spots
remaining" fake scarcity, and $3.33/mo divide-down display are exactly the
Guideline 3.1.2(c)/5.6 exposure class that got Cal AI removed in April 2026 (post-
acquisition!). Our business-model.md Apple-proofing rules already prohibit: divide-
down bigger than the billed number, re-prompting decliners with different offers.
**Keep from Vahe:** multi-step value-before-price, trial-forward framing, reminder
promise, authentic-number pricing, annual-anchoring, placement completeness
(session-start paywall is fine when the app is hard-gated anyway — the wall IS the
app entry). **Reject:** decliner counter-offers, fake scarcity, prominent
divide-downs. A hard-gated app needs the wall to be compliant MORE than anyone —
there is no free tier to retreat to if Apple pulls it.

## Crave targets derived

Gate-everything means install→paid is the whole business: plan on the 4–10% band,
build toward >10%; trial-start >15% of onboarding completers; trial→paid >30%.
Instrument all three from day one — they are the levers the entire corpus says
matter more than any growth hack.
