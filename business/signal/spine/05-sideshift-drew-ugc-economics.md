## The numbers (deal-structure gold)

**CPM benchmarks for a creator program:** month 1 = $5–8 CPM; month 3 = sub-$3;
mature = sub-$1. **Every top program (>$100k/mo spend) runs $0.80–0.90 CPM.** Sub-$4 =
founder can step back to payouts + monthly check-ins.

**Payment structures ("payment structure is the alpha"):**

- Current best-practice: **$800/mo for 60 videos** (2/day, cross-posted to 4 platforms:
  TikTok/IG/YT/Snapchat) + **non-stacked** view bonuses (hit 1M → the 100k bonus is
  replaced, not added). New/unproven creators: $200–300/mo same package, or pure CPM.
- Prior meta (4 months earlier): $400 base + $2/1k-views. Structures churn fast.
- Pay only the best-performing platform of the 4 cross-posts (hedge structure).
- "Free views": views after the bonus window close cost nothing; big programs
  explicitly optimize free-view share.
- NEVER cheap-cap a winner: a creator did 180M views in 2 weeks, contract capped his
  payout at ~$4k (uncapped ≈ $75k) — brand lost the creator + goodwill. Time-cap,
  don't view-cap; let creators feel like they're winning.
- Pure performance deals (% of spend/sales): all risk on the creator, kills loyalty
  ("35 videos before he made a dollar"); do it only if creators accept it.
- A proven "goat" creator (consistent sub-$2 CPM history): pay $10k/mo retainer without
  blinking. Joseph's corroborating anecdote: study tool paid a ~100k-follower TikToker
  to start a fresh account; ONE video → ~$20k MRR.

**Ops structure:** campaign managers (logistics, recruiting, firing) ≠ content coaches
(craft: "talk faster, look at the camera at minute 3"). Winning ratio **1 manager : 10
creators** (max 15). Suno: ~8 managers, 10k+ posts → 500M views, 14M engagements, #1
App Store music. Founder is deeply hands-on for months 1–2 (video reviews before
posting, weekly creator calls), then the machine runs. Creator funnel must be
frictionless A→Z (DM → 5-min call → notion → submit handles).

**Spark ads (TikTok's boost-an-organic-post) = "a cheat code":**

- Rule: engagement rate ≥7.5–8% → test $20 behind it; run $50–100/day for a week;
  kill when ER <5%. Sub-$1 CPMs typical on sparked high-ER content. GoWish hit #1 on
  the App Store "almost entirely from Spark."
- Don't spark while organic is still ripping — let it climb, then spark.
- Spark priority order: **comment sentiment ("what app is that?") > engagement rate >
  avg views.**
- Sparking a 3–5×-average video also lifts the account's future organic (algo trust /
  shadow-ban exit).
- **Spark can TARGET: countries, ages, audiences** — you post organic, then pay to aim
  the winner at whoever you want. Partnership ads = Meta's version (converts better
  than Spark generally; Meta = targeting-first, TikTok = reach-first).
- Organic-first > straight ads-manager: UGC is "the fastest testing engine for
  positioning," you get organic upside, and spark/partnership ads outperform
  whitelisted brand-account ads. Organic-sourced users are higher quality (bought in).

**Content laws:** the algorithm only cares about engagement rate + watch time.
"Get under people's skin a little, but show value — the easiest way to go viral"
(Suno leans into AI-hate). ER >12% = very viral territory. Emerging: 10-minute
story videos (16% avg watch time, CTA at minute 9). Apps with built-in earn/save
mechanics do best organically; **some teams now design product features specifically
to be viral content** ("build the feature for the format").

**Timeline discipline:** "there's no such thing as instant marketing." 1–2 months to
find your 3 rock creators; expect the CPM curve above; SEO agencies pitch 18 months.
