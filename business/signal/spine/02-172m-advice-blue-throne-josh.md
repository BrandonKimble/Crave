## The claims that matter

**The ChatGPT price reset (his rule 4, the one Brandon half-remembers from this genre):**
pre-ChatGPT the consumer ceiling was ~$60/yr; ChatGPT normalized **$20/mo** for phone
products that genuinely solve a problem. "The cheapest path to $30M ARR is fewer payers
at higher prices": $4.99/mo → 500k payers; $20/mo → 125k. The math is "unforgiving" —
and it matters MOST for founders without UA budget, because every install is scarce.

- Waking Up (Sam Harris): **$20/mo / $150/yr**, ~25k downloads/mo → **$1M+/mo revenue**
  (10× the typical 1:1 downloads-to-revenue-$ ratio; 1:2–1:3 is "pretty good").
- Purpose (Mark Manson): 20k downloads/mo → $200k/mo.
- Advice to anyone under $10/mo: push price up on a 10–20% test cohort until it breaks.
  Find the ceiling.

**Rule 1 — own a narrowly-defined category.** Define the category narrowly enough that
you are _believably #1 in it_ (Flo: not health → women's health; Runna: not fitness →
couch-to-5K, then acquired by Strava as their beginner funnel). "Narrow until you can
win." Too narrow only if nobody believes it's a category (it needs competitors/ecosystem).

**Rule 2 — locals, not tourists.** Revenue multiples are present-value math on LTV; the
leading indicator is cohort retention. Watch the retention curve of your **top-20%-by-
engagement** users — it must flatten, the higher the better. Year-1 _resubscribe_ rate:
50% amazing / 40 good / 30 ok / 20 bad. Resubscription is the honest metric: onboarding
can "trick" someone into paying once; renewal answers "did they get a year of value."
DAU is downstream of downloads — not signal.

**Rule 3 — organic share.** 50%+ of installs organic = great, 35–50 good, <20 = UA
treadmill. His stance: TikTok UGC is _paid, just less measurable_ (you pay for videos,
creators, iterations). The question he asks every founder: **"I don't want to know the
last video that went viral. I want to know the machine you built to replicate it."**
How often do you go viral, how fast can you spin 20–100 videos on a working creative?

- ASO is getting brutally harder: ~80% more apps submitted YoY; legacy apps own keywords.
- Nobody he talks to (4–5 founders/day) has cracked AI search yet — open frontier.

**Weekly pricing:** works only where the _need_ is short-duration (dating, ex-stalking
IG trackers). Weekly = fine cash-flow business, bad acquisition asset. (Matches our
no-weekly decision; different reasoning, same conclusion.)

**Rules 5–6 — exits (lower priority for us now, but shapes what to build):** four buyer
types (aggregators, strategics, PE, IP/acquihire; IPO = 2%). MFP bought Cal AI for the
_distribution engine_, not tech. Sell before the peak — MFP went $475M (2015, Under
Armour, growth story) → ~$345M (2020, PE, past story). Build buyer relationships 6–12
months early.

**Early-stage levers (his direct answer for six-figure-ARR founders):**

1. Crack organic EARLY — don't arrive at $10M ARR on a UA treadmill.
2. Test pricing/paywall/design early and often (Flo ran 100+ experiments/month at scale).
3. **One distribution channel at a time — push it to its ceiling before adding the next.**
   Mastery of a channel beats presence on three.
4. 0→1 requires only: work ethic + decent idea + ONE nailed channel. 1→10 is a different
   sport (multi-channel).

## Crave-specific takeaways

1. **Category ownership is free and available:** "dish-level food discovery" /
   "the best-dish finder" is a believable, winnable category definition — Beli/Yelp/Google
   are restaurant-level. This should shape App Store positioning, keywords, and pitch.
2. **Direct challenge to our $7.99/mo:** the ChatGPT-reset argument says under-$10 is
   leaving money on the table, ESPECIALLY with no ad budget (fewer, better-monetized
   payers is the bootstrap-friendly shape). Counter: food discovery may not carry
   $20/mo urgency; Waking Up had a celebrity moat. → Adjudicate in pricing panel; at
   minimum plan the price-ceiling test cohort.
3. **The machine, not the video** = the standard for our UGC plan. Every distribution
   decision should answer "what's the repeatable machine," not "how do we get a hit."
4. **One channel to ceiling** argues against splitting effort across TikTok + IG + ASO +
   creators simultaneously at launch. Pick the primary; sequence the rest.
5. Crave's structural strength in this framework: local repeat-use utility should produce
   the flat retention curve buyers (and sustainable unit economics) want — the OPPOSITE
   of the churn-and-burn AI-wrapper corpus norm. Retention curve of top-20% users =
   the metric to instrument from day 1.
6. His TikTok-is-paid framing is the honest accounting stance for our creator budget.
