# Spine notes — Joseph Choi, Consumer Club (superwall, 2025-04-04)

**Who:** Joseph Choi — founder of Consumer Club (the Discord where most guests in this
corpus hang out), host of the Superwall podcast (47/64 of our transcripts). Ex-
dropshipping/e-com, ex-Google, newsletter operator. **This is the ecosystem's curator
explaining his own worldview — the lens that selected almost everything else in the
corpus.** Incentives: sells community access, builds/sells tools (the outlier-video
search tool he demos = what became the SpyTalk ad reads), Superwall affiliated.

## The doctrine (know the lens you're reading through)

- **Distribution-first product design:** "Reverse-engineer what would make a good
  viral TikTok moment, then build that as a feature." Start with the channel = start
  with the market; TikTok is "talking to millions of people at once" (research).
- **Tactics flow downhill from commoditized markets:** affiliate → e-com → apps.
  "Affiliates are the best consumer marketers." Tabs Chocolate ($0→$10M/yr, TikTok
  organic, zero-follower teen accounts, no influencers) is the genesis story of the
  whole mass-UGC meta.
- Format meta history: talking-head → slideshows/carousels → HeyGen AI-avatar-over-
  slides → faceless AI-voice + B-roll. Formats saturate; **inversion** dynamics create
  the next wave (Sam Sulek's raw 50-min videos vs MrBeast maximalism; authenticity is
  itself a recurring market). All layers still work simultaneously at different
  saturation prices.

## The mechanics worth stealing

1. **Soft CTA + comment-section conversion:** nobody clicks link-in-bio; viewers close
   TikTok and SEARCH the App Store — so the app name must be legible on screen. The
   sale happens in comments ("what app is that?" → creator reply). **Video replies to
   comments act as organic retargeting** — the algorithm shows the reply to the people
   who engaged with video #1; the reply is where you hard-sell.
2. **Outlier detection:** judge a format by how much a video outperformed ITS OWN
   account's median (algorithm heat, not follower heat). Then brief creators to
   copy-adapt the proven video exactly — you're paying for their time, not their
   creativity ($ tens vs $1–2k/video).
3. **The NGL high-school seeding tactic (tribal accounts):** private accounts named
   "<HighSchool> + app", school logo as avatar, mass-follow the students → tribal
   trust → follow-backs → first post lands on the whole school. "Alumni-network
   trust."
4. Account-farm meta (know it exists; decide ethics separately): 5 accounts/phone ×
   3 posts/day; US SIM cards shipped to Philippine creators; warm-up protocols.
5. **Agencies don't work in an underpriced channel:** the people who are actually good
   run their own apps; agency lifespan ≈ 6 months; the founder must personally learn
   the channel, then hire an operator to run what the founder figured out. (Third
   independent source for agency-skepticism.)
6. **Web-to-app funnels:** hidden margin/attribution channel (web onboarding → Stripe
   checkout → download app with sub active); Superwall productizing it.
7. Starter bar: pick channel hypothesis → 3 proven formats from the niche → iterate;
   **cracking 5,000 views = "there's something to it."**
8. "Everything is media. You pay for content one way or another — own it rather than
   rent it." Verticalized copies work ("Cal AI for vegans at 1/10 the revenue is
   still $200k/mo").

## Crave transfer

1. **Tribal-account seeding is the closest corpus analog to city-launch mechanics:**
   the "Austin" identity is a tribe with intense food self-regard. An Austin-branded
   content account (plus neighborhood/tribe variants) with mass-follow of local
   accounts = the NGL high-school move at metro scale. Combine with StudyFetch's
   zero-follower influencer account and Jenny's charisma doctrine → a coherent
   local-UGC playbook emerges across three independent sources.
2. Comment-conversion machinery is free for Crave: controversial rankings generate
   the "what app says that?" comments natively; video-reply retargeting turns every
   ranking argument into a second touch.
3. Distribution-first feature design, Crave version: make the shareable artifact (the
   "Top 10 birria in Austin" ranked card / screenshot-able score card) a first-class
   PRODUCT feature — the content IS the feature. Fits the existing share-modal work.
4. Web-to-app funnel belongs in the blueprint's later phases: Crave already runs
   dual-rail billing; web checkout nets ~97% and is Apple-legal out-of-app.
5. Epistemic note for the ledger: Choi's selection function explains the corpus's
   skew — distribution-first, TikTok-native, young-founder survivor stories. Where
   the corpus "agrees," check whether it's just Choi's lens echoing.
