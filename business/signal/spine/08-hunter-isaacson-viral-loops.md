# Spine notes — Hunter Isaacson (NGL, Wink, Bags) on viral loops (superwall, 2025-07-25)

**Who:** Hunter Isaacson — NGL (150M MAU, multi-8-figure/yr, still growing 3 yrs in),
Wink (70M downloads), Bags (crypto), 70M+ downloads pre-NGL. **The one voice in the
spine whose engine is PRODUCT-LED loops, not paid creators** — the adversarial
counterweight to the Cal AI/StudyFetch/Sideshift school. Evidence: first-party.

## The claims that matter

**Platform arbitrage:** NGL was started the week Instagram opened link stickers to
everyone (Oct 2021). Framework: find an existing internet behavior (anonymous Q&A —
Formspring→YikYak→Yolo lineage) and re-mount it on the biggest available
distribution graph. The graph choice IS the growth strategy.

**Virality is engineered, then sparked:** app built in 1 month → **six months of
nothing** → $100-here-$100-there influencer tests → one school on the other side of
the world lit up (forced an Android build) → ONE paid TikTok (girl reading a
controversial anonymous message, "guess who sent this") activated the world in a week.
Even the best viral loop needed a paid spark and half a year of iteration.

**Loop mechanics (the craft):**

- The loop must live in owned real estate: the Instagram bio link. Churned users
  re-enter because _their friends_ still post links — churn with a high re-download
  rate is a healthy loop, not a failure.
- Onboarding 15–20 seconds; ONE flow, ONE funnel, ONE north-star metric (posted link
  - received ≥2 replies).
- CTA copy sells the end result, never the install: **"Get your own messages"** beats
  "Download this app."
- Multi-step tutorials (4–6 taps, share-menu installs, Safari extensions) are fine —
  but ONLY when the user is already sold on the value before the tutorial starts.
- "I put invite screens in every product. Forcing users to share is an endorsement."
  PLG math: real CPI = paid CPI ÷ (1 + invite rate).
- Zoom University: meme-native GTM (embraced "Zoom University" joke, posted on meme
  pages) → 40k waitlist, 7:1 female ratio via comfort-in-pairs design.

**Monetization pole:** NGL = weekly subs $1–7 priced by country GDP, a tiny % of a
massive free base pays. The exact opposite of hard-paywall utility. (Not Crave's
model; useful as the boundary case.)

**His $20k/mo growth-budget answer:** split into many small UGC/ad tests (never one
big creative), while simultaneously building the invite/share mechanic so every paid
user drags a friend — the flywheel = hook content + share loop. Don't start in games/
crypto (paid-dominated, brutal).

## Crave transfer

1. **The dinner-table loop is Crave's NGL-link moment.** "Where should we eat?" is
   one of the most common group-chat questions in existence. A shared Crave list /
   dish-ranking dropped into iMessage is an endorsement + a curiosity hook, exactly
   Hunter's share-as-endorsement mechanic. Crave already has the machinery (share
   modal, public list slugs, collaborators, friend graph). The blueprint should treat
   share-out surfaces as a first-class growth engine, not a feature.
2. **Tension flagged for the panel: gate-everything vs the loop.** A hard paywall on
   EVERYTHING means a shared link hits a wall for the recipient. Mitigation already
   in the codebase: favorites share/public controllers are @AllowUnentitled — public
   web views of shared lists exist. Decision needed: how rich is the logged-out/
   unpaid view of a shared list/ranking? Rich enough to hook, gated enough to sell.
   This is the single sharpest business-design question the corpus has surfaced so
   far, and neither the paywall camp nor the viral camp answers it for us.
3. Onboarding-speed conflict resolved by model: NGL's 15-second onboarding serves a
   free viral loop; Cal AI's long investment quiz serves a hard paywall. Crave is the
   latter — keep the quiz, but the SHARED-LINK entry path (from a friend's list)
   should skip to value in seconds, not walk the 17-step quiz.
4. CTA language lesson applies verbatim: market "See Austin's best dishes, ranked" /
   "See the full ranking" — never "Download Crave."
5. Six-months-of-nothing + paid-spark: even loop-native products need patience and
   small paid ignition. Budget for ignition experiments, not a launch-day miracle.
