## The position

Every shared Crave artifact — a ranked list, a poll result, a single dish card — should render **fully and freely on the public web**: dishes, ranks, scores, and a taste of the evidence, no account, no wall, no blur, watermarked and branded. This is not a concession that weakens gate-everything; it is the one exception gate-everything's own logic requires, because the shared slug is not a use of the product, it is an advertisement for it, and an ad that hides its own product doesn't work. `monetization-and-gating.md`'s shelved tiered-free logic should be formally revived — not reopened for the app itself, which stays hard-gated at onboarding end exactly as decided — but re-scoped as the permanent policy for the public/shared-link surface only. The share card and its web view are launch-blocking growth infrastructure, not post-launch polish, because they are Crave's only paid channel for a founder with no ad budget.

## The affirmative case

The corpus's sharpest distinction for this panel is between two structurally different loops, and Crave's dinner-table use case is unambiguously the one that survives a paywall. **Reciprocal-usage loops** (NGL, Wink, Lobby, Discord) require the _recipient_ to open and use the app to complete the loop; every working example in 64 transcripts is free, because a paywall "decapitates the loop at the recipient" (`ledger/07`). **Artifact loops** (Darcy's watermarked spending card, Cal AI's shareable streaks, Lovely's widget screenshot, Stronger's heatmap) require the recipient only to _see an output_ — and these coexist with paid products, including Darcy, the corpus's one paid-upfront app with a functioning share loop, reportedly ~$20k in its first month off a watermark loop plus a public Facebook group (`arthurspalanzani--20260523`, claimed/unverified but the only paid-app precedent in the whole corpus). Crave's loop — "where should we eat" answered by a dropped-in ranked list — is an artifact loop by construction: the recipient needs the _answer_, not the app (`ledger/07`).

That reframes the wall/teaser/rich-view choice, and the evidence on it is not close. Zero sources across 64 transcripts show a walled shared artifact working — "even the hardest-paywall operators gate _usage_, never the shared artifact" (`ledger/07`): Cal AI's badges and reposted creator videos are public by design, the same founder who runs the corpus's most aggressive hard-paywall playbook. Teaser/blur has exactly one precedent, Glam Up's blurred fake scan, and it ships from the same founder (Nicole) who runs forced-rating dark patterns elsewhere in the corpus — a tactic bundle, not a standalone lesson, and one that inverts the moment it touches Crave: blurring an answer a friend just explicitly endorsed reads as bait-and-switch at the exact instant of maximum trust, directly contradicting the no-pay-to-rank integrity brand. Rich-open is the only option with load-bearing support: it mirrors NGL's structure exactly (recipient gets the _receiving side_ of the product free and complete; conversion sells them their _own_ generative version), it's how the entire "share artifacts are designed into the product" consensus operates (seven-plus sources: Stronger's dramatic screenshots "are what make us money," Strava-style watermarked cards, Lobby's watermarked group photo to Stories, features "build[t] for the camera" — `ledger/07` consensus #2), and Hunter Isaacson's structural claim applies directly: "forcing users to share is an endorsement" only holds if what's shared is worth endorsing.

Discord's super-node economics dissolve the obvious conversion objection. The person who organizes where six people eat is the one who'll pay; the other five are audience, not lost revenue — "most of those features are never used by any of their members" (`ledger/07`). The slug's job is advertising and recruiting the _next_ group's organizer, not converting all six recipients. That reframes "won't a free rich view cannibalize installs" as the wrong question: category evidence says it won't even if it tries. Food is maximally public and group-decided, the opposite pole from Quittr/taboo categories where sharing is structurally near-zero (`ledger/07` consensus #4) — Crave sits on the favorable side, and Nicole's invite-3-friends test found _no material conversion loss_ from a free-share mechanic (sharers converted anyway; three real installs is harder than paying, `superwall--20260511`).

Cost is close to zero and reversibility is total. The fact sheet already confirms the code supports it — `@AllowUnentitled` on free surfaces is built and env-gated (`crave-fact-sheet.md`) — so opening the slug is a product-policy toggle, not new engineering. `ledger/07`'s own "what changes if X" framing states the downside floor explicitly: if slug-to-install conversion proves negligible after six months, the rich view still costs nothing, because it remains brand plus SEO surface — the same latent-demand logic YourMove used for organic search traffic applies to "best birria austin"-class queries landing on a Crave list page.

## Pre-empting the other side

**"A free full answer removes the reason to install."** This is the paywall camp's strongest instinct and I concede its logic in the abstract — Stronger's own testimony is "nobody sends a paid app to their friends," and K-factor was never even instrumented there (`ledger/02`, `ledger/07`). But that testimony is explicitly reframed by the ledger itself: "best read not as 'loops are impossible behind paywalls' but as 'we never built the artifact' — a choice, not a law" (`ledger/07`). Stronger never tested a rich public artifact against a paywall; they simply didn't build one. Absence of a counter-test is not evidence the counter-test would fail.

**"This is scope creep on a hard-paywall decision the owner already made."** Fair, and I hold the line the owner drew: the _app_ stays gate-everything at onboarding end, full stop, no dilution there. But the owner's decision governs the app; the public web is a different surface with a different job (advertising, not product), and `ledger/07` explicitly frames this as a live _internal_ conflict already — `monetization-and-gating.md`'s tiered-free architecture predates the gate-everything call and was shelved, not resolved, against it. I am not asking to reopen the app; I am asking to close that unresolved internal conflict in the one place — the shared slug — where the corpus's evidence is nearly unanimous.

**"Rich-open cannibalizes the paid dish layer specifically."** This is the sharpest version of the objection and deserves an honest answer, not a dodge. If a shared list includes the gated dish-level ranking (`monetization-and-gating.md`'s hero: "best birria in the city," dish scores, evidence receipts), a non-paying recipient gets the paid product's crown jewel for free, every time, forever, with no funnel back. I do not have corpus evidence resolving restaurant-list-shares vs. dish-list-shares as separate policy questions — `ledger/07` flags this explicitly as unresolved ("whether the answer differs for lists vs. poll results vs. single-dish shares"). My honest position: restaurant-level rankings and poll results should render fully rich (this is where the loop evidence is strongest and the product isn't the dish hero); a shared _dish_ ranking or dish list should render richly too but the recipient's own search from that slug should hit the same gate a fresh install hits — the artifact proves the product works, the _product_ still requires paying. That is a scoping compromise, not a full concession.

## Concrete implementation

1. **Public web view, no auth wall, at launch (Q3 2026 target alongside city-1 seed).** Every shareable artifact — restaurant list, poll result, single dish/restaurant card — gets a canonical public URL rendering full ranks, scores, and 2-3 evidence-quote snippets, built on the existing `@AllowUnentitled` primitive.
2. **Watermark + brand on every rendered card**, matching the Darcy/Lobby/Strava pattern: Crave logo, the city, a "ranked by Crave — no pay to rank" line reinforcing the integrity brand at the exact moment of highest trust.
3. **CTA sells the recipient their own version**, never "Download Crave": e.g., "See Austin's every dish, ranked" — mirrors NGL's "get your own messages" mechanic.
4. **Instrument from day one**, per `ledger/07`'s explicit anti-pattern warning (Stronger's unmeasured K-factor): track share events, slug views, slug-to-store taps, and add Jungle's onboarding "how did you hear about us" question at launch, not retrofitted.
5. **Formally amend `business/monetization-and-gating.md`**: mark it superseded for the direct-app surface (gate-everything wins there), but re-adopt its free/paid map verbatim as governing policy for public-web shares — restaurant rank/score/evidence open, dish-list/dish-search shares open-to-view but click-through to a fresh dish query still hits the paywall.
6. **Skip-the-quiz slug-arrival funnel** (flagged in `ledger/07`'s open items, resolved here in favor of speed): a user who arrives via a shared slug and taps through to install should not repeat the full onboarding quiz before hitting the paywall — shorten time-to-paywall for a warmer, referred lead.

## What would prove me wrong

If, after six months of Austin operation with the rich view live, slug-view volume is real but slug-to-store-tap conversion is near-zero even among viewers who return to the same slug multiple times (repeat viewing without any install intent) — that would falsify the "slug is the ad" thesis and argue the loop is a dead-end curiosity rather than a funnel, at which point the compromise on dish-shares should tighten further rather than loosen. Equally, if early cohort data shows meaningful users treating shared dish-rankings as a full substitute for installing (screenshotting/re-sharing dish lists at a rate that visibly suppresses install-per-share versus restaurant-list shares), that would validate the dish-layer carve-out I already conceded above and argue for narrowing it even further — teaser-only on dish shares specifically, restaurant/poll shares staying fully rich.
