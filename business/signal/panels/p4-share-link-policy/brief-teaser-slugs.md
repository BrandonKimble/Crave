# Brief: The Shared-Link Wall — Protective Teaser Position

## The position

The public share slug should render a **teaser, not a wall and not a rich full view**: the #1 ranked result shown in full (name, one line of why), ranks 2–10 shown as blurred/locked cards with a "See all 10 + every dish, ranked → Crave" CTA into the standard $7.99/mo·$39.99/yr paywall — no login or payment required to load the page, only to see past it. This preserves gate-everything's structural logic (curiosity, not satisfaction, converts) and is not a new invented pattern — it's the same locked-but-visible blur mechanism Crave has already decided and shipped for the in-app dish-list teaser, extended to a second surface. I hold this while conceding what the record shows: no corpus source ever ran a reciprocal-usage loop through a genuine pay-wall at the recipient side, and the ledger's own recommended lean for this panel is the rich public view. Real costs against me, handled below, not hidden.

## The affirmative case

**1. This is already Crave's own decided mechanism, not an import.** `business/monetization-and-gating.md` states the in-app dish-hero pattern directly: "Locked-but-visible, not hidden... Gated dish sections show a blurred 'Top 8 dishes, ranked → Crave+' teaser — visible-and-locked beats invisible (creates the curiosity gap)." Whatever the fate of that document's shelved tiered-free/paid feature map (superseded by the FINAL 2026-07-09 gate-everything call per the fact sheet), the _teaser mechanism itself_ — blur plus a curiosity gap, versus either full concealment or full disclosure — is a standing UX decision Crave has already made and is comfortable defending to Apple. Porting it to the shared slug is consistency, not a fresh risk.

**2. The ledger's own "taste vs. crutch" test argues against a full rich render for a group decision.** The same document asks of any free surface: does it whet the appetite (taste → free) or satisfy the need so the user never upgrades (crutch → gate)? A fully rendered list — ten restaurants, every score, every evidence quote — completely answers "where should we eat tonight" _and_ every future "where should we eat" for that group, for any craving, forever. That's the textbook crutch. A revealed #1 with blurred 2–10 answers tonight's actual ask (the group needs one restaurant, not ten) while leaving next week's different craving unanswered without the app — the taste, not the crutch.

**3. Discovery vs. decision gates the SECOND layer of the reveal, not the first.** Monetization-and-gating.md's frame: discovery (surfacing something you didn't know existed) is Crave's un-substitutable value; a decision, once discovery has happened, is comparatively substitutable (ask the server, read the menu). The friend's link already delivered the un-substitutable moment — discovery happened the instant #1 is revealed and endorsed. Ranks 2–10 with full scores and evidence is decision-depth on a choice the group has, practically, already made for tonight — exactly the layer Crave's own doctrine says is safe to hold back.

**4. Internal consistency with the hard-paywall consensus itself.** Ledger 02's least-contested finding: hard paywalls beat freemium head-to-head when tested (Cal AI's GrindClock A/B reversal; Quittr — "no one used the app when it was free, everyone used it when it was a hard paywall"; Stronger's ~25% overnight lift on an _accidental_ hard-paywall bug, K-factor never measured). Every one of those wins by **showing less at the free layer**, not more. A "rich" public slug rendering the identical full dish-level ranked list + evidence a paying subscriber sees would make the anonymous web visitor's free experience _more generous_ than the logged-in in-app free tier, which already blurs the dish hero behind Crave+. That asymmetry is the rich-view camp's unresolved tension, not mine.

**5. Curiosity beats satisfaction — with a load-bearing number.** Nicole's Glam Up (`superwall--20260511`) let users start a real scan and blurred the _already-computed_ result before charging to reveal it — claimed 1M users/6 months, peak ~$150k MRR, >$600k lifetime revenue. That number is attached to "compute something real, reveal a taste, blur the rest, sell the reveal" — the same shape as revealing #1 and blurring 2–10. Super-node economics (Discord, ledger 07) explain why this doesn't cost the loop: the organizer is the one curious enough to tap through on the other nine spots — the other five recipients only need the practical answer (go to #1), which the teaser still gives them free, completing the reciprocal step Hunter Isaacson's framing cares about.

## Pre-empting the other side

**"No source anywhere shows recipient-must-pay reciprocal virality working" (ledger 07, point 3) — and no one ran a loop through a hard wall.** True, and I'm not testing that claim: my design has _zero_ wall or login to load the slug — the recipient completes the reciprocal step entirely free. The narrower, real concession: no corpus example proves a _blurred-depth_ teaser specifically, at a share surface, converts as well as a fully open one. I'm arguing from Crave's own adjacent, already-shipped decision plus adjacent-category evidence (Glam Up), not from a share-loop precedent that doesn't exist. A skeptical judge is right to discount this to "plausible, not proven."

**Glam Up is a first-session dopamine app bundled with a forced-rating dark pattern and weekly-only pricing — ledger 07 says "Discard" for this reason, and ledger 02 says weekly pricing is the best-supported _rejection_ in the whole corpus.** Conceded — I don't get to keep the revenue number and discard the packaging selectively. My answer narrows the borrowing: Glam Up isn't a sharing precedent at all, it's an onboarding-paywall precedent, so citing it for the sharing question is weaker than the ledger's framing suggests even from my own side. What's load-bearing for me is Crave's own standing in-app teaser design — not new evidence I'm importing, but an existing product decision I'm asking to extend, not relitigate.

**Ledger 07's recommended lean is explicitly the rich view, arguing that blurring a friend-endorsed answer "reads as bait-and-switch at the moment of maximum trust" — the strongest objection here.** Real, and taken seriously: Crave's moat is no-pay-to-rank, receipted honesty, and a blur at the instant of maximum trust is a genuine brand-risk vector. My answer narrows the ask rather than defending an unlimited teaser: the #1 result must be **completely real and unmistakably whole** (no synthetic content, no fake urgency — the Apple-compliance inviolables in `business-model.md` forbid fake scarcity and apply here too), and locked cards must read as "there is more, get the app," using the exact grammar Crave already ships in-app — not a Glam-Up-style gimmick. If that framing itself reads as bait-and-switch in practice, the rich view wins and I concede it.

## Concrete implementation

- Public slug, **zero login and zero paywall gate to load the page** — the reciprocal step is entirely free, matching every working artifact-loop precedent in ledger 07.
- Render **#1 in full**: restaurant name, one supporting evidence line, ordinal rank ("ranked #1 of 10"), but **no raw numeric Crave Score** — mirrors the existing split in `monetization-and-gating.md` (restaurant-level transparency free; numeric dish-level score is the paid depth layer).
- Ranks **2–10**: blurred/silhouetted card (photo blurred, name withheld) — identical grammar to the shipped "blurred Top 8 dishes, ranked → Crave+" teaser, not a pattern invented for the web.
- CTA under the blur: **"See all 10 + every dish, ranked → Crave"** (ledger 07's own recommended copy) into the standard $7.99/mo or $39.99/yr-with-trial paywall — no share-specific discount or urgency framing; `business-model.md`'s Apple-compliance rules (no divide-down, no decliner re-prompt, no fake scarcity) apply equally to a slug-sourced paywall visit.
- **Instrument from day one** — the gap ledger 07 flags on Stronger ("nobody sends a paid app to their friends," K-factor never measured — "we never built the artifact," not a law): track slug view → CTA tap → install → subscribe, and add Jungle's onboarding "how did you hear about us" question (30–40% of Jungle's new users attributed via this exact instrument, ledger 07) so attribution the funnel misses still gets caught.
- Scope discipline: this governs **only** the public share-slug surface. It does not reopen `monetization-and-gating.md`'s shelved tiered-free/paid feature map for direct app installs — the in-app product stays gate-everything at onboarding, full stop.

## What would prove me wrong

- **Share-through collapses, not just conversion.** If a post-launch A/B (teaser vs. rich view) shows the teaser suppresses the _loop's own completion_ — fewer shares because senders or recipients report the reveal feels stingy or untrustworthy, not just fewer viewer-to-subscriber conversions — that's Hunter Isaacson's bait-and-switch cost materializing, and the rich view wins outright.
- **Organic share rate falls below the UA-treadmill line specifically because of sentiment, not volume.** Ledger 07's benchmark (Blue Throne: 50%+ great, 35–50% good, <20% treadmill) is a clean bar. If share rate is low _and_ attributable to "feels like a bait-and-switch" rather than generic low usage, the integrity-brand cost outweighs the conversion protection.
- **Slug→install conversion is negligible regardless of depth.** Ledger 07 flags this itself: if six months show conversion is small no matter how much or little is shown, the teaser/rich-view distinction stops mattering for revenue, and the fuller view wins by default as free SEO and brand surface.
