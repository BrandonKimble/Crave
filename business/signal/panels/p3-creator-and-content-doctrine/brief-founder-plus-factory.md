# Brief: Founder-Led Channel Zero + Programmatic Listicle Factory + Tiny Roster

## The position

Brandon runs channel zero solo for 60–90 days on two native formats: demo-wow screen recordings of the ranked search, and rankings-as-controversy posts ("the #1 birria in Austin is X — fight me") where the product is named intrinsically because the score IS the claim. Underneath that, Crave builds a programmatic listicle factory — an automatable pipeline turning existing dish-ranking data into unlimited, evidence-backed local Top-N slideshows — run as a two-pronged Noise-style model: founder demo/controversy content is the soft-CTA brand engine that must convert on its own merit; the slideshow factory is the hard-CTA ground game, cheap volume that retargets rather than tries to go viral. On top, Crave recruits a tiny 3–5 local-creator roster on the corpus's convergent base-plus-non-stacked-milestone deal grid, locality first, charisma second. Every piece is chosen because it is automatable by a solo founder-engineer, and every controversy format is receipt-backed — no astroturf, no manufactured outrage, no pay-to-rank — because integrity is the product, not a constraint on marketing it.

## The affirmative case

Founder-led-first is the least-contested claim in the corpus: 12+ tellings across all three channels converge on "you cannot vet, brief, or coach creators without personal viral literacy," and receipts from your own account are what _unlocks_ creator recruiting at all ("easier now with 75M views than getting the first 10," ledger/05-founder-led-content.md). This is free and de-risks every later dollar; the corpus's honest counter-cases (Glow: 15 founder TikToks → 2 canceled trials; Clear30: organic an "uphill battle") resolve against the counter-position, not for it — both were generic-format posting on pain-driven products, whereas food is repeatedly named content-native, where the demo IS the entertainment (ledger/05, Conflict A).

The format ladder hands Crave its best slots for free: direct demo 0.5–1% view→download vs. slideshows/memes 0.05–0.2% (ledger/05, ledger/06) — product-as-hero converts 5–10x better than the factory ever will per-view. Crave's demo passes Kelechi's "show me something I haven't seen before" test (nobody has seen dish-level city rankings), and rankings-as-controversy is literally Matt/Jenny's engineered 50/50 engagement-bait mechanic ("Ruby's library video → 30M X views") occurring naturally in Crave's own claim structure (ledger/05 §"Crave's structural gift").

The listicle factory is the deliberately lower-converting hard-CTA prong, and the corpus says that's fine _if you know it_: Noise runs slideshows as "the cheap retargeting layer, not the viral layer," and separately calls food "arguably the best slideshow vertical in the corpus" (ledger/05 §Crave transfer). Crave's factory has a structural edge no corpus operator had — slides are auto-generable from data Crave already computes (evidence-backed Top-N by neighborhood, cuisine, price), which is software work, not content-ops labor, and scales with zero incremental headcount. The corpus's honest ceiling here (Copia: 13M views → 10–15k downloads ≈ 0.1%) is accepted, not disputed, because the factory's job is volume and retargeting, not conversion rate.

The tiny-roster call is the ledger's own conclusion, not an aggressive read: section 06 resolves the elite-vs-army conflict toward "**3–5 local creators max**" for Crave specifically — locality is the binding filter since the For You page can't be geo-fenced, and Austin's food-creator pool is dozens, not thousands (ledger/06 Conflict 3, §Crave transfer). Deal terms are the corpus's tightest price-discovery cluster: $20–50/video base + non-stacking milestones, ~$1–2 effective CPM, chosen by 29/30 creators over higher-EV pure CPM (ledger/06). The one experiment above the grid the ledger names — a zero-follower "Crave ATX" account at $500–2,000/mo, StudyFetch's mechanic at local-food money rather than $10k — is exactly the small, automatable-adjacent bet a solo founder can manage without an agency.

Integrity is load-bearing for the controversy format specifically: ledger/05 Conflict C draws the line — outrage at the _claim_ (fine) vs. outrage at a _deception_ (brand poison — rage-bait, shock-farming, undisclosed AI influencers). Crave's rankings are evidence-receipted by construction, so every post points to quotes and a score; the corpus's no-astroturf losers are excluded by design, not discipline.

## Pre-empting the other side

**Founder time is the scarcest resource, and 60–90 days of daily content collides with launch blockers and the sole-engineer constraint.** Conceded: ledger/05 names the tiebreak rather than dodging it — channel zero wins on the _learning_ dividend alone, since every later creator dollar is priced off it. Mitigation: the demo format is near-zero marginal cost (a screen recording of an existing feature), and the factory is designed to absorb volume so founder hours go only to the two highest-converting formats.

**The factory risks becoming SEO-spam volume, not evidence — Noise's own numbers show meme-tier conversion for slideshows.** The strongest objection, only partially answerable: no corpus operator tested a slideshow factory with receipts baked into every slide (Alberto/Noise/Copia all ship opinion or AI content, not data-grounded content). Crave's citation-on-slide differentiator is a hypothesis this panel cannot verify from evidence alone — it must be gated by ledger/05's own protocol (5–6 formats/2 weeks, 15k-view bar, 70–75% watch-through), not assumed to outperform.

**Locality-first under-weights charisma — the corpus's best deal outcomes (Jenni's 25-video ramp, Nomad Table's $8k month) came from charisma-first talent scouted nationally.** Conceded in part: ledger/06 is explicit that charisma-anywhere only works when "the buyer is everywhere" — Crave's isn't. The ledger's own synthesis (locality as pool filter, charisma applied within it) is correct, but the roster ramp will likely be slower than these national comps, and the plan should budget for that.

## Concrete implementation

Days 1–60: Brandon posts founder-led content only, no creator spend, alternating demo-wow and receipt-backed controversy claims, cross-posted IG + TikTok + FB (ledger/05: IG volume-tolerant, TikTok craft, FB underrated). Apply the corpus's triage exactly: <300 views = account problem, 300–1k = format problem, 1–5k = keep format vary copy; gate at ~15k views and 70–75% three-second watch-through; CTA lives in a pinned comment, never spoken in-video. Budget the corpus's patience curve — 25 videos before a first 10k-view hit.

In parallel, build the listicle-factory pipeline as engineering work: a template system auto-generating Top-N slideshows (tacos under $15, patio brunch, late-night queso) from live Score + evidence-quote data, scheduled once formats validate — code, not content-ops, so it scales without a hire.

Only after a validated winning format does Crave open outreach to 3–5 Austin food/lifestyle creators, locality first via DM, charisma/engagement-density second. Terms: $20–50/video base + non-stacking milestones (~$1–2 effective CPM), no view caps on a winner, perpetual usage rights. Run one zero-follower "Crave ATX" account experiment at $500–2,000/mo. If TikTok/Meta DMA-level Spark/city-radius targeting (verified in crave-fact-sheet.md) confirms live, geo-amplify organic winners at sub-$1 CPMs — the fix for this model's one honest structural gap.

## What would prove me wrong

Two consecutive 2-week founder-content cycles at sub-300 views across formats would falsify "food is content-native" and confirm the niche-adset warning instead — response: shift weight to community seeding and paid geo-targeting, de-prioritizing both the roster and the factory until distribution is proven. If the factory, once built, converts indistinguishably from the corpus's generic-slideshow floor (~0.1%) even with receipts on-slide, that falsifies the "our data beats the corpus baseline" hypothesis and downgrades it to pure awareness spend, not a growth engine. If DMA-level Spark/city-radius targeting fails to hold in a live Austin test — reach leaking heavily outside the metro — the geo-amplification lever disappears, and the honest fallback is creator-identity-as-geo-filter alone, which caps this channel's ceiling far lower than argued here.
