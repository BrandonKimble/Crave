# Verdict: Austin-only on every axis at launch; a 3–5 city dark bench is earned by Austin calibration, not bought as day-one insurance; expansion fires on a measured Austin plateau or two named overrides.

## The call

**Launch posture: Austin-only, totally.** One visible city. Collapse NYC back into the
waitlist branch — it presents as "live" today (`crave-fact-sheet.md`, onboarding) without
having passed any calibration bar, which is exactly the "seeded but not retention-tested
= liability" state ledger 04 names. All founder content, creator dollars, and any Spark
spend are Austin-scoped for the first 60–90 days minimum.

**No dark bench at launch.** Zero bench cities are seeded until BOTH: (a) Austin's full
archive load is complete and score constants are re-derived against production density
and owner-feel-approved — i.e., the calibration _methodology_ exists as a repeatable,
documented pass, not just Austin's numbers; and (b) the five launch blockers are shipped
and `ENTITLEMENT_GATING=enforce` is live. After both, dark-seed **3–5 cities** (not 8–10,
not 25), selected by onboarding-waitlist demand ranking cross-referenced against the 783
already-downloaded archives. Dark means: behind the existing city-pick allowlist,
invisible, zero marketing, zero founder validation hours until a flip trigger fires.

**Minimum Austin density bar before the wall flips on:** full 3-year-window archive
loaded (~15.6k posts); score recalibrated + owner-feel-approved; and a RED-provable
demo check — the top 20 Austin dish-query classes (mined from the corpus's own
highest-volume "best X in Austin" threads) each return ≥10 evidence-receipted scored
dishes. If a flagship query looks thin to a skeptical local, the wall stays off.

**Primary expansion trigger (flip city #2 visible):** two consecutive 2-week
founder-content cycles show flat-or-declining view→install/paid conversion despite ≥3
format variations (the Cal AI niche-ceiling pattern, `ledger/11`), WHILE Austin's paying
cohort holds ≥35% organic share (Blue Throne's "good" band, `ledger/04`). That is
"Austin works but is capped" — the only state in which city #2 is opportunity rather
than escape. **Two overrides fire early:** (1) a genuine breakout — an Austin video
clears ~15k views with ≥70% 3-second watch-through and majority non-Austin view
geography (`ledger/05` gates) → flip the top validated bench city + a bounded $200–500
Spark spend at its DMA; (2) a credible dish-ranking clone appears (PrayScreen 2–3-month
clock, `ledger/01`). **Non-negotiable precondition on any flip:** that city has
individually passed the same calibration + top-20-query density bar Austin passed. No
trigger overrides the integrity veto.

**Waitlist branch for non-live cities:** keep it as built, but treat it as a demand
sensor, not a growth channel (cold waitlists convert ~0.5%, Massive, `ledger/04`). Show
three things: the real Austin product as proof (live ranked-dish demo — the corpus's
"show real data" resolution favors this structurally), an honest "not in your city yet —
we're loading cities by demand" line with real (never fabricated) signup counts, and a
notify-me capture. Its ranking output is the bench-selection input. No email drips, no
fake positions, no scarcity theater — Apple-compliance and integrity lines are inviolable.

## Why

**The dark-seed brief loses on its own concession.** Its stated inversion condition —
"if Austin's score-recalibration turns out to be city-idiosyncratic… the case inverts
from cheap insurance to integrity liability" — is not a tail risk; it is the _current
state of knowledge_. Calibration has never been run once (`crave-fact-sheet.md`, open
caveat). Buying insurance before knowing whether the policy pays out is not insurance.
Its own implementation step 1 ("finish Austin's recalibration first") already concedes
the sequencing; this verdict just makes the concession binding.

**Spark DMA targeting kills the load-bearing argument for a big bench.** Ledger 04's
strongest pre-seed argument ("give diluted reach somewhere to land") was written when
city-level ad targeting was unverified. It's now verified (fact sheet, 2026-07-12):
contain the spend to Austin's DMA instead of pre-building landing zones for overflow.
What survives is the _recapture_ play — and the dark-seed brief's own step 5 only ever
aims recapture at "the top 3–5 metros," which is why the bench is 3–5: a bench larger
than the recapture mechanism can activate is idle inventory carrying ongoing collection
babysitting on a solo founder.

**Home-market-first is the corpus's least echo-risked consensus** (Alberto, Symmetry,
Blue Throne, Lobby — finance/fitness/social, three channels, none citing each other,
`ledger/04` Consensus 4), and it transfers _harder_ to Crave than to any of them because
Crave's product is non-functional outside a dense market. Founder attention is the real
constraint (`ledger/11`: "nobody models a solo technical founder simultaneously shipping
product"), and the ramp evidence (100–200 days to a content winner, `ledger/05`) says
the dilution the bench insures against won't exist during the exact window the bench
would compete for attention.

**But the Austin-depth brief over-gates.** Its condition (c) — Austin clearing Blue
Throne's ~40% year-1 resubscribe before city #2 — is a 12+ month clock on a decision the
mechanized pipeline makes cheap and the PrayScreen clone-clock makes time-sensitive. The
bench's real value is real: once the calibration methodology is proven, $600–780/city
buys optionality against a 2–3-month fast-follower window, and "flip a validated bench"
beats "seed under fire." So the bench exists — it's just earned, small, and dark.

## Discarded as noise

- **The full 25-metro flip** at launch or on any near-term trigger — both briefs
  effectively abandoned it; the ledger calls it an integrity risk "everywhere at once."
- **Nomad Table's "works with 2 people in a place"** — traveling-user network app,
  structural inverse of Crave (`ledger/04` noise list).
- **National view-count/CPM benchmarks** as literal targets — global-TAM physics a
  one-city product cannot access; keep mechanisms, discard magnitudes.
- **Waitlist-as-growth-engine** in either direction — the successful waitlists all rode
  antecedent virality; Crave's is a free byproduct sensor, nothing more.
- **"Seed cities because data is cheap"** as a standalone argument — cash was never the
  bottleneck; ledger 11 says so explicitly.

## Reversal triggers

- **Calibration proves city-idiosyncratic** (bespoke founder-weeks per market): shrink
  the bench to 1 (top waitlist city) and treat each expansion as a mini-launch.
- **Calibration proves trivially transferable** (constants port with light review):
  widen the bench to 8–10 on the same dark terms — the dark-seed brief's world.
- **Two consecutive Austin content cycles show ZERO distribution regardless of format**
  (Marcus Burke's niche-throttle materializing): geography itself may be the constraint;
  dark-seeding to diversify the content surface becomes a legitimate hedge.
- **A funded dish-level competitor ships multi-city before Austin clears its bar:**
  speed-to-multi-city becomes the defense; accept a lighter per-city validation pass and
  say so in-product.
- **Spark DMA targeting underperforms in execution** (bleed, spiking city-level CPMs):
  the recapture math weakens and the bench earns +2–3 cities as the substitute mechanism.

## Owner-conditional items

- **Bench budget** (~$2–4k for 3–5 cities at the $600–780 floor; NYC runs higher) —
  Brandon's call on timing against runway.
- **On-camera willingness** — the 60–90-day channel-zero protocol assumes founder-shot
  content; if Brandon won't be on camera, the screen-recorded demo-wow lane still works
  but the plateau trigger's cycle cadence needs recalibrating.
- **"Owner-feel-approved" score calibration** is by definition Brandon-only; it is the
  single gate everything downstream waits on.
- **Which city is #2** if waitlist volume is thin at decision time — Brandon picks from
  the archive-density shortlist.
