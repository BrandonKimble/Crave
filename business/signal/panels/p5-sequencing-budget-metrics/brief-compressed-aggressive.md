# Brief: The Compressed Plan — Parallel Channels From Week 1–2, Not Sequential Gates at Day 90

## The position

Crave should compress the corpus's default sequencing — founder-solo for 60–90 days, then creators, then paid — into a parallel launch: founder content from day 1, creator trial deals scouted in the same week (not after a quarter of proof), an early anchor bet committed by week 6 instead of day 90, Spark geo-amplification live from week 2 on any post that clears an engagement bar, and Apple Search Ads running from day zero because it is a different mechanism (search-intent capture, not audience acquisition) that the corpus's own "master one channel first" rule was never really about. The case for compression is structural, not impatience: the annual-with-trial SKU front-loads a full year of cash the moment a trial converts (business-model.md: "a paywall buys front-loaded cash, not stickiness"), which rewards getting more people into the funnel sooner over waiting to be sure; and the PrayScreen precedent shows the ASO first-mover window Crave is counting on — "only listing" for a query — closes to ~70 competing listings in 2–3 months (`superwall--20250928--prayscreen-120k-quick-exit.md`), which is almost exactly the window the patient plan spends sitting on its hands. This means accepting real dollars ($1.5–3k/mo, not zero) and real founder-hours risk in month 1, in exchange for a faster read on whether Austin has a repeat-use business inside it at all.

## The affirmative case

**The cash-flow logic is Crave's own, not borrowed.** Annual retains ~44% at 12 months vs. ~17.5% monthly (~2.5x) and "front-loads cash" (business-model.md); 1-year retention is "model-independent" — the paywall's whole value is realizing revenue NOW. There is no multi-month freemium engagement runway before monetization here, so the fastest lever available is simply more people entering the always-monetizing funnel sooner — that argues for compressing top-of-funnel effort, not pacing it.

**The ASO clock is a real, ticking constraint the patient plan ignores.** PrayScreen's exact experience — sole listing at launch, ~70 listings within 2–3 months (`superwall--20250928...`) — is the fact sheet's own cited precedent for why Crave should "own 'Austin food ranking'-type queries before clones arrive" (`ledger/04-launch-shape.md`). A 60–90-day founder-only quiet period is nearly the whole length of that closing window. Compressing distribution into weeks 1–6 is racing a clock the corpus itself measured, not aggression for its own sake.

**ASA is not "another channel" competing for the one-channel-first slot — it's a different mechanism, and it's free.** Ledger/11's own unresolved question asks this directly: does ASA "count as a different mechanism (search-intent capture, not audience acquisition) that can run in parallel from day one?" (`ledger/11-scaling-and-sequencing.md`, open question 4). Yes: ASA captures people already typing "best tacos austin," costs nothing beyond Apple's $100 signup credit, and every source that used it this way (Cal AI, Versy) ran it as a keyword-discovery lab alongside organic, never after it (`ledger/08-paid-amplification.md`, Conflicts). There's no reason to leave a free instrument idle for a quarter.

**Spark doesn't wait for a proven creative archive — it waits for one post.** The Sideshift doctrine triggers on engagement rate (≥7.5–8% → $20 test, scale to $50–100/day, kill <5% ER), not on tenure or portfolio size (`ledger/08`, consensus 6). If a founder or trial-creator post clears that bar in week 2, geo-targeting it at the Austin DMA — verified real (crave-fact-sheet.md: TikTok DMA/metro, Meta city+radius) — is instrument-grade spend, not the $7k–40k/day Cal AI scaling the corpus warns is premature. Waiting until month 4–5 (Cal AI's own arc) presumes an 18-month funded runway Crave doesn't have and doesn't need to import.

**Creators-in-month-1 is a compression of sequencing, not a rejection of founder-first.** The corpus's founder-first consensus is about _not delegating without personal literacy_ (`ledger/05-founder-led-content.md`, consensus 1) — it is not evidence that scouting and trialing creators must wait until founder content has run its full course. A trial-only deal (~$300–500/mo, 4–8 videos, per the anchor-creator-bet brief's own structure) is cheap enough to run _concurrent_ with founder posting, screened on the same gates (comment density, watch-through), so Brandon isn't handing off an unproven format — he's auditioning candidates on the same clock he's proving the format, instead of a clock that runs afterward.

**"No ad budget" is best read as "no cold-audience UA spend," not "zero dollars ever."** Blue Throne's Josh makes exactly this accounting point — creator retainers and Spark boosts are spend whether or not anyone calls it an "ad budget" (`ledger/08`, Conflicts). Reframing the constraint this way is honest about what the compressed plan actually is: still zero cold Meta/TikTok scaling, but real dollars on instruments (ASA credit, $20–100/day Spark tests, $300–2,000/mo creator retainers) that the corpus repeatedly treats as compatible with an organic-first philosophy, not a violation of it.

## Pre-empting the other side

**The corpus's best-triangulated claim is "0-to-1 needs ONE channel mastered before adding more"** (Blue Throne's rule, `ledger/11`, consensus 5) — and four parallel workstreams in month 1 looks like a direct violation. I concede the letter of it: Crave runs more simultaneous motions than the corpus's cleanest survivors did in their first quarter. My answer is that the rule is really about founder _creative attention_ staying on one format engine — which it does, since ASA and Spark add budget, not creative burden — but I can't claim this reframe is proven; it's this panel's interpretation, not the corpus's own finding.

**Both sibling p3 briefs (founder-plus-factory, anchor-creator-bet) sequence creators and the anchor deal AFTER a 60–90-day founder-solo gate, for a real reason: you can't vet a format you haven't run.** I concede the risk squarely — an anchor deal signed on unproven content could waste the retainer on the wrong creator or the wrong format. My mitigation (trial-only deals before the anchor commitment, same gates as founder content) reduces but does not eliminate this risk; it is a bet that concurrent scouting is safe, not a proof that it is.

**Founder-hours are the real constraint, and I will not pretend otherwise.** Month 1 still has to close Apple Developer enrollment, rebuild the RC pod, re-skin the paywall, and fix legal URLs (crave-fact-sheet.md, launch-blocking checklist) — none of which this plan compresses. Adding creator scouting/contracting on top is a genuine hours conflict; Jenny AI's 16-hour recruiting days assumed a full-time non-founder operator Brandon doesn't have (`ledger/11`, Noise). Honest resolution: ASA and Spark cost dollars and a few hours of setup, not creative hours, so they compress cheaply; creator scouting/anchor administration is the expensive line item, and if bandwidth runs out, it's the first thing cut back to the patient plan's pace — not the money-only instruments.

## Concrete implementation

**Week 0 (pre-launch):** File the ASA discovery campaign on the $100 credit against Austin-intent and competitor terms (best tacos austin, austin restaurants, Yelp/Beli brand terms) — a few hours of setup, no creative cost. Close launch blockers in parallel (Apple enrollment, legal URLs, paywall re-skin) — these gate everything else regardless of channel strategy.

**Week 1–2:** Founder posts demo-wow + ranking-controversy content daily. Simultaneously, DM 8–10 scouted Austin food creators for trial-only deals (~$300–500/mo, 4–8 videos on their own accounts) — screening, not a full roster commitment. Any post (founder or trial creator) clearing ≥8% engagement rate gets an immediate $20 Spark test geo-targeted to the Austin DMA; scale to $50–100/day while ER holds >5%, kill below it.

**Week 3–6:** Move ASA from discovery to exact-match on any keyword that spent and converted. Of the trial creators, whichever clears the same watch-through/comment-density gates as founder content becomes the anchor: stand up the "Crave ATX" zero-follower account at $500–1,500/mo + a modest revenue-indexed kicker — by week 6, not day 90.

**Week 6–12:** Expand to 3–5 creators if the Austin pool supports it. Blended paid-instrument spend caps at $1,500–3,000/mo across Spark + ASA — small next to Cal AI's later-stage numbers, real next to "zero."

**Kill gates:** Install→paid <4% for two consecutive weeks (Vahe band, `ledger/10`) halts all distribution scaling — freeze at ASA-discovery + $20 Spark tests only, redirect founder hours to onboarding/product. Organic share <20% sustained past week 8 (Blue Throne bands, `ledger/10`/`11`) is a UA-treadmill signal — cut Spark/creator spend back toward zero regardless of revenue, since amplification should ride real organic winners, not manufacture demand alone. A trial or anchor creator showing no comment-density/watch-through lift over founder-only content by week 6 means don't sign the retainer — fall back to the tiny-roster diffuse model.

**Scale gates:** Install→paid >10% and organic share sustained >35% even with paid layers running means the instruments are amplifying, not propping up, the funnel — raise Spark daily caps and consider accelerating a second city's dark-load (fact sheet: ~$600/city, decoupled from marketing effort).

## What would prove me wrong

If two consecutive weeks of parallel scouting produce zero creators willing to trial at these rates, or the first Spark tests can't clear 5% ER on anything (founder or creator content), that confirms Marcus Burke's niche-adset throttling warning even under paid amplification — the honest response is to collapse back to the patient, founder-solo-first sequencing the corpus actually recommends, not to keep spending into a channel that isn't working. If founder-hours visibly collide with the launch blockers — Apple enrollment or the paywall re-skin slips past week 4 because creator/Spark administration ate the time — that is direct proof the parallel plan cost more than it returned, and the fix is cutting creator-hours first, per my own stated priority. And if the anchor creator, once trialed, shows no lift over founder-only content by week 6, that is proof the early-anchor bet substituted speed for evidence the corpus says actually matters — commit depth to a proven face, not a fast one.
