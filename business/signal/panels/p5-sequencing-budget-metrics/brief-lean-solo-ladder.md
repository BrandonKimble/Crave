## The position

For a solo founder who is also the sole engineer, the only 0-6 month plan the evidence actually supports is the minimal-motion one: months 0-2 are founder-led content plus ASO/ASA hygiene and nothing else with a dollar sign on it; the first paid dollar is a Spark test behind an already-proven >=8% engagement-rate (ER) post, capped at instrument-grade spend; creators enter no earlier than month 3, and only if founder content has produced a repeatable signal; a campaign manager is not hired until the creator roster is large enough to need logistics, which on a single city's creator pool is a late event, not a month-3 one; and every week has a hard cap on growth hours because the same 40-something hours also have to ship product. The "real work" of these six months is not content volume, it's the instrumentation: the top-20%-engagement retention curve, install-to-paid rate, the annual trial funnel, and organic share of installs — because a hard-paywall, no-ad-budget, hasn't-launched product has exactly one asset (the Score) and exactly one open question (does it retain), and every dollar and hour spent on growth before that question is answered is spent blind.

## The affirmative case

The corpus's best-triangulated sequencing finding is that founder-led content precedes any creator program, often for months — five independent sources (StudyFetch, Jenny/Matt, Sideshift/Drew, Nomad Table's Jay at 7 months solo before the first creator hire, Dan Kwan's "founder should be the biggest influencer") converge, though the ledger flags real echo risk from a shared Cal AI origin story (`ledger/11-scaling-and-sequencing.md` consensus #1). Agency skepticism is the cleanest consensus in either section: six sources spanning Cal AI (agency capped at $5k/day, "never care as much"), Choi (agency half-life ~6 months), Kyle Fowler, Stronger, and two more independently land on "skip agencies, or only after a format is proven" (`ledger/08-paid-amplification.md` consensus #3, `ledger/11` consensus #2) — ruling out the fastest, most capital-intensive path a funded competitor might take.

On ASO/ASA, the evidence says free-to-cheap hygiene, never a growth plan: title/subtitle/keyword-field hierarchy and pre-build keyword validation against a popularity/difficulty threshold are genuine cross-source consensus (Arthur's tool, corroborated independently by Kyle Fowler's own 2019-2021 Sensor Tower research), and Apple's $100 free Search Ads credit is "the free validation lab every source agrees it is, never as a launch marketing plan" — Glow's own $100-credit-to-4-downloads result stands as proof it can fail completely (`ledger/09-aso-and-app-store.md` Crave transfer, Noise). Mechanical, nearly free, done in month 0, a signal generator rather than a channel.

The Spark gate fits Crave's structural constraint directly. The corpus's only mechanism that fixes one-city organic waste is Sideshift's doctrine — test any post clearing ~7.5-8% (elsewhere 10%) ER with $20, scale to $50-100/day while ER holds above 5%, kill below that — and it geo-targets, which national UGC economics otherwise can't do for a single-metro product (`ledger/08` consensus #6, Crave transfer). Layered onto the verified fact that TikTok Spark inherits DMA/metro/city targeting and Meta supports city+radius (`crave-fact-sheet.md`), this turns organic-grade creative into paid-grade Austin-only reach for "hundreds, not thousands, of dollars a month" (`ledger/08` Crave transfer) — spend several sources treat as compatible with "no ad budget" in spirit (Yev's $20/ad minimums, Roger Chen's cheap-validation ads, Ryan Thorp's $10-20/video kill-at-$5-10 tier; `ledger/08` consensus #5).

The hiring sequence is the section's single best-triangulated finding — three independent sources (Sideshift's 1-manager:10-creator ratio, Quittr's dedicated creator-manager-plus-VA hire once logistics demanded it, StudyFetch's marketing generalist as first non-founder hire) agree the operator, not the content talent, is the correct first growth hire, and only once there's a roster to manage (`ledger/11` consensus #3, Crave transfer: "likely well post-launch given a single city's creator pool"). That is the floor under "campaign manager much later."

Instrumentation-first rests on the highest-quality single source in the retention section: Blue Throne's Josh, a buyer auditing real M&A targets rather than narrating his own survivor story, frames the top-20%-by-engagement cohort's retention curve flattening — not DAU, not downloads — as the honest read on value, alongside organic-share bands (50%+ great / 35-50 good / <20% UA-treadmill) (`ledger/10-retention-metrics-revenue-engine.md` consensus, Crave transfer). With Vahe's no-trial install-to-paid framework (4-10% okay, >10% good, <4% a problem) — denominated exactly the way Crave's hard-paywall funnel now works — plus his trial-start (>15% good) / trial-to-paid (>30% good) bands, this is a complete, cheap, founder-hours-only measurement stack, buildable before a single Austin user exists (`ledger/10` numbers table).

## Pre-empting the other side

**"This is too slow — the corpus's own channels take months to read, and a solo founder can't afford to sit passively waiting."** Fair, and the corpus agrees on the timeline: SEO needs 3-6 months to distinguish failure from non-viability, Bloom's Jay cites a 90-day minimum for any video to go viral, Sideshift needs 1-2 months just to find "3 rock creators" (`ledger/10` consensus #5). I don't dispute the clock; I dispute that the response should be parallelizing channels rather than working the one channel harder within capped hours. Blue Throne's "0-to-1 needs one channel mastered" rule is explicit that this is a feature of the stage, not a founder failure to route around (`ledger/11` consensus #5).

**"The campaign-manager consensus argues for hiring sooner, precisely because Brandon is also the engineer."** The strongest objection, and I concede its core: `ledger/11` names this exact gap outright — "nobody models a solo technical founder simultaneously shipping product." No source resolves it for Crave; it has to be engineered, not borrowed. My answer is "automate the content instead of hiring": programmatic, data-driven ranked-dish posts drawn straight from the Score (Nick Weber's "content factory" framing) are lower-labor than the 16-hour-day creator recruiting `ledger/11`'s own Noise section flags as a bandwidth mismatch for Crave. If product velocity visibly stalls under the content load, that's a signal to pull a lightweight ops hire forward — see below.

**"Sideshift's 8% ER Spark threshold is one vendor's number, not a law."** Conceded directly — the ledger itself says Spark corroboration is thin (Vert Ventures secondhand, Ryan Thorp partial) and Drew sells the tooling being described, so "treat thresholds as starting grid, not law" (`ledger/08` consensus #6). The plan uses it as a starting gate to test and recalibrate against Crave's own ER data, not as a fixed rule.

## Concrete implementation

**Month 0 (pre-launch):** keyword-validation pass against Austin dish-intent phrases ("best tacos austin," "birria austin," "where to eat austin") using an Astro/Sensor Tower-class tool before finalizing App Store title/subtitle/keyword field, no duplicate keywords, Food & Drink category, Product Page Optimization queued for later. Apple's $100 ASA credit runs as a ~1-week broad discovery campaign, then exact-match on whatever converts (`ledger/09`). In parallel: build the four-metric instrumentation stack (top-20%-cohort retention curve, install-to-paid, trial-start/trial-to-paid, organic-share attribution question in onboarding) live from user #1, not retrofitted.

**Months 0-2:** founder personally produces Score-sourced content (ranked-dish posts, "best X in Austin" formats) — zero paid spend, zero creator contracts. Hard cap: growth work capped at roughly one day-equivalent per week (~15-20% of founder hours); the rest stays on product, because the moat is the pipeline, not marketing labor. No agency, no paid UA, no creator outreach in this window.

**First paid dollar (expected inside months 1-3):** any Austin-hooked founder post clearing ~8-10% ER gets a $20 Spark test geo-targeted to the Austin DMA/metro; scale to $50-100/day while ER holds above 5%, kill below it (`ledger/08`). Monthly paid ceiling: low hundreds of dollars, not a media budget.

**Month 3 gate:** creators enter only if founder content shows a repeatable signal — a format clearing >=8% ER more than once, or install-to-paid trending into Vahe's 4-10%+ band with organic share holding above 20%. If cleared: 1-3 micro-creator tests, Spark/partnership usage rights contracted upfront, not a program.

**Campaign manager:** deferred until the creator roster approaches Sideshift's ~10-creator logistics threshold — realistically month 6 or later, gated on revenue and roster size, not a calendar date.

**Kill/scale reads:** scale — top-20% cohort curve visibly flattening by day 90, organic share >35%, install-to-paid >10% (`ledger/10`). Kill-the-marketing-plan-and-fix-product, not spend-more — top-20% curve does not flatten and decays toward the Grindclock pattern (churn approximating organic inflow); no UGC volume should be thrown at that (`ledger/10` Crave transfer).

## What would prove me wrong

If, after a genuine 90-day founder-content window (the corpus's own minimum reading window), zero posts clear even a relaxed 5% ER _and_ ASO/ASA produce no measurable Austin-intent volume even on validated keywords, that would mean the organic-first premise has failed on its own terms — not that it needs more patience, but that Crave's content or keyword space is thinner than assumed, and the plan should escalate to Roger Chen's cheap-validation-ads-before-organic pattern despite the corpus's general preference against it. If product velocity visibly stalls because founder hours are being cannibalized by content production — the exact solo-technical-founder gap `ledger/11` flags as unmodeled by the entire corpus — that would argue for pulling a lightweight, part-time ops hire forward well before the 10-creator threshold, since protecting the engineering asset matters more than the hiring sequence borrowed from funded teams. And if organic share of installs falls under Blue Throne's 20% UA-treadmill line and stays there past month 3 with no recovery, that is evidence the "no ad budget" premise itself needs revisiting before, not after, the month-3 creator gate.
