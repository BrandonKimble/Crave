# Verdict: The lean ladder is the spine; budget buys depth inside phases, never earlier phases. Two compressions survive: ASA-as-instrument runs parallel from launch week, and Spark is gated on engagement rate, not calendar.

## The call

**The ladder is evidence-gated, not cash-gated — identical at both envelopes.** Month 0 (pre-launch): close the five launch blockers (Apple enrollment → real ASC products → RC key/pod → paywall re-skin → legal URLs → `enforce` flip, `crave-fact-sheet.md`), finish the Austin load + calibration per P2, run the ASO keyword-validation pass, build the metric stack live from user #1, and start founder content reps. Month 1 (launch): ASO-hygienic listing ships with launch; ASA $100-credit discovery week then exact-match; founder content on the P3 protocol (5–6 formats/2 weeks). Months 1–3: first Spark dollar fires whenever the first post clears ER ≥8% — calendar-independent — $20 test geo-targeted at the Austin DMA, scale $50–100/day while ER >5%, kill below (`ledger/08` consensus 6). Day 60–90: creator trials ($300–500/mo, top 1–2) only if founder content shows a repeatable signal, per P3. Day 90–150: anchor conversion per P3's gates. Month 6: roster expansion only after the anchor clears its own gate; first-hire evaluation.

**ASA: parallel from launch week — but it cannot start earlier.** Ledger/11's open question 4 resolves YES: ASA is search-intent _measurement_ ($100 credit, Versy's instrument pattern, `ledger/08` conflicts; `ledger/09` noise: "never as a launch marketing plan"), not a second channel competing with Blue Throne's one-channel rule. Cap it at ~$100–300/mo after the credit; a Glow-style zero-spend result is a volume fact, not a failure. The compressed brief's "Week 0 ASA" is factually impossible — ASA requires a live listing, which is blocked on the enrollment the plan hasn't closed.

**ASO keyword validation: month 0, pre-enrollment, before finalizing title/subtitle.** An Astro/Sensor Tower-class pull against Austin dish-intent terms ("best tacos austin," "birria austin") needs no developer account and gates listing decisions (`ledger/09` consensus). This — shipping the validated listing AT launch — is what answers the PrayScreen 2–3-month clone window; parallelizing creator spend does not.

**Envelopes.** At **~$1k/mo**: months 0–1 ≈ $100–300 (keyword tool + ASA after credit); months 2–3 ≈ Spark $200–300 + first trial $300–500; months 4–6 ≈ anchor at the $500 low end + Spark, dropping trials to fit. At **~$4k/mo**: the same gates on the same dates; the surplus buys the anchor at $1,500, 2 trial creators, Spark ceiling $1–1.5k/mo, a part-time editor/ops contractor if the hours tripwire fires, and the P2 dark bench ($2–4k one-time) once P2's preconditions are met. Blended paid never exceeds the envelope; no cold-audience UA at either level — restate "no ad budget" as "no cold-audience UA spend" (Blue Throne's accounting point, `ledger/08` conflicts).

**Founder hours: growth capped at ~10–12 h/wk through month 3 (~20% of a ~55h week), rising to ~15–20 h/wk months 4–6 only if gates pass.** Engineering/product/calibration keeps the majority always — the moat is the pipeline. Hard tripwire: if any launch blocker slips past week 4 because of growth admin, cut creator/Spark hours first (both briefs agree on this ordering).

**Kill/scale metrics (instrumented month 0, read from launch):**

- **Install→paid (blended, whole funnel):** 4–10% okay, >10% scale, **<4% for two consecutive weeks (min ~200 installs) = halt all distribution scaling**, freeze at ASA-discovery + $20 tests, founder hours to onboarding/paywall (Vahe, `ledger/10`).
- **Trial-start (annual leg):** >15% good, 8–11% fixable (Vahe).
- **Trial→paid:** >30% good; **<20% sustained = fix the paywall/onboarding before any spend scales**.
- **Renewal band:** Blue Throne's 40% good / 30% ok is the frame; do NOT anchor kill-criteria on Zach's anecdotal 30% — verify against RevenueCat's ~44% lifestyle benchmark first (`ledger/10` conflicts). Month-12 metric; inside 0–6, watch annual-mix share and month-2 monthly renewals as leading indicators.
- **Organic share of installs** (onboarding attribution question): 50%+ great / 35–50 good / **<20% sustained 4+ weeks = UA-treadmill → cut Spark/creator spend toward zero regardless of revenue**.
- **Austin-local view fraction:** per P3, anchor conversion needs ≥~50% Texas-plausible audience; for spend, if every ER-winner runs <20% Texas-plausible AND Spark DMA targeting can't recover install geography, the geo-dilution objection is confirmed — creator-identity becomes the only geo filter (P3 reversal 2).
- **Retention oracle:** top-20%-by-engagement cohort curve flattening by day 90 = scale; decay toward Grindclock (churn ≈ inflow) = **fix the product, not the marketing — no UGC volume gets thrown at it** (`ledger/10` Crave transfer).

**Multi-city: P5 defers entirely to the P2 verdict's trigger** (two consecutive flat/declining 2-week content cycles across ≥3 format variations while organic share ≥35%; overrides: non-Austin breakout, credible clone). P5 adds only the budget line: bench seeding is a $4k-envelope item; at $1k it waits until the trigger arms.

**First hire: an operator, never an agency, and not on a calendar date.** Campaign manager fires at ~8–10 contracted creators (Sideshift 1:10, `ledger/11` consensus 3) — realistically post-month-6. One earlier exception: if product velocity stalls ≥4 consecutive weeks under content load (the solo-technical-founder gap `ledger/11` names as unmodeled by the whole corpus), pull forward a part-time editor/VA at $500–1k/mo — $4k envelope only. Agencies: never (6-source consensus, `ledger/08`).

## Why

**The compressed brief's engine argument died on its own premise.** "Annual front-loads cash, so fill the funnel sooner" assumes the funnel converts — but install→paid, trial→paid, and retention are all unmeasured at launch, and the corpus's most reliable non-Superwall finding is that every channel needs 60–90+ days to read (`ledger/10` consensus 5). Spending three months of parallel dollars before any reading exists is spending blind, which the lean brief correctly makes the central point. **Its ASO-clock argument proves the wrong conclusion:** PrayScreen's closing window is about owning the _listing/keyword claim_, which the month-0 validation pass + launch-day listing fully answers at near-zero cost. **Its week-1 creator trials and week-6 anchor contradict canon** — P3 already adjudicated trials to day 60–90 and the anchor to day 90–150 on vetting grounds (you can't vet a format you haven't run; 2,000x view variance) — and its Week-0 ASA is mechanically impossible. **What it won:** ASA-as-parallel-instrument (a real mechanism distinction the lean brief conceded by running it in month 0 anyway) and the explicit "no ad budget = no cold-audience UA" accounting. **The lean brief carried** on the corpus's best-triangulated findings — founder-first (5 sources, echo-flagged but confirmed by P3), operator-before-creative hiring (3 sources), agency-never (6 sources), the Vahe/Blue Throne metric stack — plus the one insight neither ledger states outright: at gates this cheap, extra budget cannot buy speed, only depth, so the two envelopes share one calendar.

## Discarded as noise

- **Jenny AI's 16-hour-day month-1 creator blitz** — assumes a full-time non-founder operator (`ledger/11` noise); both briefs discarded it.
- **Cal AI-scale paid ($7k–40k/day), January arbitrage, sub-$1 CPM / 2–39¢ CPI benchmarks** — national-TAM physics, poison for a one-metro model (`ledger/08` noise).
- **Arthur's country/locale arbitrage and his headline numbers** — vendor-sold, no geo analog (`ledger/09` noise).
- **"Subscription apps need Meta ads"** — ads consultant on a paywall vendor's podcast (`ledger/08` noise).
- **Calendar-driven anchor commitment (week 6)** — speed substituted for the evidence P3 says actually matters.
- **DAU/view bragging as KPI** — four independent sources disown it against interest (`ledger/10` consensus 1).

## Reversal triggers

- **90-day organic failure:** after a genuine 90-day founder window, zero posts clear even 5% ER AND ASA shows no Austin-intent volume on validated keywords → the organic-first premise failed on its own terms; escalate to Roger Chen's cheap-validation-ads pattern (small, geo-fenced) rather than more patience.
- **Product velocity stalls** ≥4 weeks under content load → pull the part-time ops hire forward of the 10-creator threshold; protecting the engineering asset outranks the borrowed hiring sequence.
- **Organic share <20% sustained past month 3** → the no-cold-UA premise itself gets re-examined before, not after, the creator gate.
- **Keyword pull shows Austin dish-intent volume too thin to matter** → ASO drops to background hygiene; growth burden shifts fully to the P3 content machine; stop iterating the listing.
- **Spark DMA targeting leaks or CPMs spike at city scale** → Meta partnership ads (city+radius) become the entire instrument layer (`ledger/08` Crave transfer); if both fail, P2's bench earns +2–3 cities.
- **Install→paid >10% AND organic share >35% with paid layers running** → instruments are amplifying, not propping: raise Spark caps, accelerate the P2 bench flip evaluation.
