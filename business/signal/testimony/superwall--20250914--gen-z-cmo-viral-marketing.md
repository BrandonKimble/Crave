(claimed)
- Why the Cupid video went viral, per her: (1) it used a trending person, (2) the outrageous comment about her dead pet drove outrage engagement — "outrage and absurdity causes virality." (claimed/first-party analysis)

KPIs must start **qualitative** (video communicates brand identity, users take away XYZ), because "you can't set 'this particular video has to get a million likes' — no one can ever guarantee that, even a creator with a million followers." Quantitative KPIs only once you have data points. (opinion)
- Bad founder pitches to her: "come up with a viral format like yours" with no thought; good pitches specify target audience and why a format fits ("my users love Love Island, let's spin our version"). (opinion)

### Content strategy (organic)

- Her core strategy: "do the opposite of what people are doing right now" while keeping the guaranteed methods (hooks). Example: a **horizontal**, minimally edited, minute-30 talking video on Instagram got only **35,000 views** but converted **~200–250 followers** — which she calls "a very high conversion rate for Instagram" (~0.6–0.7% if the numbers hold). (claimed, first-party)
- Hinge→Twitter flywheel: real (not fabricated) Hinge conversations turned into tweets; teasing "the first AI social network" in her dating bio; famously converted a Hinge date into a user by walking him through onboarding in person. First tweet on a dormant 2016 account got **~20 likes** ("most people stay in 1-2-3 likes jail"). (first-party anecdote)
- Persona rule: "full send the persona" — one authentic trait played up to the max; half-committing reads as trying too hard. Online you're in a crowd of 100, so amplify; in person, one-to-one, be complete. (opinion)
- Day-by-day / challenge formats retain because people follow to see if you attain the goal. (opinion)
- The "calm meta" (authentic, un-edited, non-rage-bait) is the next retention style after loud vertical editing saturates. (prediction/speculation)
- Guerrilla-gifting trend: startups gifting donuts/doormats; recipients post about the kind act — social-proof-driven exposure. (secondhand observation)
- LinkedIn is unsaturated/undervalued, especially for students: "the highest value followers you can have are probably LinkedIn followers"; if people mock a platform's content, that means the bar is low and good content stands out. (opinion, echoed by host)
- Hamptons campaign mechanics: TV-show-style intern race; a LinkedIn post + job listing drew **~2,500 applications**; **12** intern slots on the show but **~150** people joined the "race"; social proof cascade — after 1–2 students posted, **100+ interns** posted LinkedIn banners+paragraphs within **two days**; a final challenge was invite-maxing and the winner invited **120 people in one day**. (claimed, first-party)
- Founders should build personal brands: "your personal brand stays with you forever, but the company will come and go." Caveat: big creators are good at building their OWN brand, which doesn't necessarily translate to building someone else's. (opinion)

## Contrarian positions

- Anti-trend contrarianism as strategy: post horizontal, calm, low-edit video on platforms saturated with loud vertical rage-bait; "do the opposite of what people are doing right now."
- Follower counts don't matter when hiring a marketer; storytelling potential does.
- Don't set quantitative KPIs for content people up front; qualitative-first, trust the creator, "let them cook" — against typical performance-marketing management orthodoxy.
- LinkedIn — the most-mocked platform — is the highest-value follower base.
- Outrage and absurdity cause virality, and participating in engineered outrage (dead-dog bait, with consent) is a rational win-win.
