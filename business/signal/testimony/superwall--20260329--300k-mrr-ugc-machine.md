## Arc

Dillian, after ~18 months of failed products and nearly no money, launched Halo AI (an AI photo editor on the Gemini API) as a last-shot consumer app — built in 2-3 weeks, live mid/end-October. After one creator TikTok returned ~6:1 ROAS, he emptied his bank account and took a ~$100k personal loan to fund a mass-UGC creator machine: 85 creators, one single video format, ~300 posts/day, 600M views in 60 days, 1.2B in 120 days, $300k MRR in 45 days. The episode exists to break down his UGC system, deal structure, and double-paywall monetization for the Superwall audience.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Default winning price: **$8.99/week** with a free trial ("we'll give you a notification when your trial ends"); no annual on the shown experiment. Weekly has been the consistent winner; yearly tested, dropped, being retested. (claimed)
- **Double-paywall onboarding**: a skippable ("non-gated") paywall during onboarding, then a **gated hard paywall at the moment of first action** (user uploads photo, types prompt, hits send → sign-in → paywall). Both paywalls **combined convert at 16.5%**, higher than either alone. (claimed, screenshot referenced)
- Theory for why: uncertain users skip, browse templates/examples in-app (dashboard "replicates the feeling" of the viral TikTok result), and convert when they're "right at the border" and blocked at the action. (speculation/first-party interpretation)
- It IS a hard paywall — core functionality (generation) is unusable without paying; browsing is free. (first-party description)
- Additional paywalls: exit offer, abandoned cart, trial-cancel paywall, "deduction abandon" [transcription uncertain — likely "onboarding abandon" or similar]. (claimed)
- Sign-in placed before paywall only to get users into the database; "could be either way." (claimed)
- Sponsor context: Superwall's free tool at paywallexperiments.com is trained on **422 profitable paywall experiments**. (host ad read)

### Trial & onboarding

- Trial mechanic: free trial on the $8.99/wk plan with a trial-end notification promise on the paywall itself.
- No re-engagement notifications/email yet for non-converters; "probably down the line." (claimed)

### UGC & creator deals

- Scale: **85 creators** now (10 → 25 → 85 over the growth period); **~300 posts/day**; posting on 4 platforms (TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, YouTube).
- **One single format only** — "One format, 1.2 billion views. There's no deviation of that."
- Views math: total views = creators × videos/creator × posts/video (≈ platforms) × views/post. Revenue = views × conversion rate. Conversion rate is **constant per format** (~0.3–0.5% of viewers "go down the video" [funnel]; illustrative example given: 1M-view video → ~500 downloads). (first-party framework, claimed numbers)
- Power law: **~5% of videos >100K views, ~1% >1M**. Getting >1M views/day at current volume. (claimed)
- Creator sourcing: job posts in Reddit groups (UGC Creators, UGC Brands) + "hundreds of Facebook groups, each with thousands of people" + paid platforms → **500-600+ applicants**, ~**30-50 applicants/day**.
- Screening: past UGC experience (ideally app/tech, not just e-com clothing ads) + US-based or US VPN (to create US-targeting accounts). Heuristics: native English speakers make better hooks; Gen Z creators for Gen Z audiences.
- **Paid trial: 5 videos × $20 base = $100**, ~5 days, run in Discord. Trial posts the same single format, 4 posts/video = **20 posts across 4 platforms** during trial.
- Trial pass criteria (explicitly NOT a view threshold — power law makes 5 videos too few to expect virality): (1) consistency (posting daily), (2) proactive communication, (3) demonstrated grasp of viral structure (hook → build-up → payoff). "A lot of people don't understand… and won't learn" — cut those. (first-party)
- **Uncapped posting** once hired; some creators post **20 videos/day → ~80 posts/day** across platforms. Platform tolerance: YouTube and Instagram "don't care" about volume; **TikTok requires ramp-up** (3/day → 5 → 10, warming the account) but no cap at scale; Facebook unknown ("still really new"). (claimed)
- Viral replication loop: when a video goes viral (e.g., 10M views), an automation pushes it to every creator's Discord channel and **all ~80 creators recreate it same-day**, each recreation pulling 2-3M+ views → view spikes. Creators are also power-law (a few drive most views), but replication works if the specific viral element is preserved. (first-party)
- **OpenClaw agent as creator coach**: trained on all >1M-view videos (downloaded, transcribed via Gemini, stored as local text files); creators query it daily for scripts/ideas; hooked to TikTok trend data (trending keywords + hashtags via provider **Scrape Creators**) to fit trends to scripts. Hashtags don't move virality; sounds do; trends (e.g., Olympics) feed topics. (first-party)
- Community as retention mechanism: all creators in one Discord, private channel each, plus wins/ideas/feedback channels, late-night voice chats; camaraderie + gamification ("repost my video, I want to hit the milestone") drives consistency more than pay alone. Contrasts with "post a video, get paid" programs that get no compounded learning. (first-party)

### Deal structures (see also section below)

- Four structures considered: (1) fixed retainer, (2) CPM ($1/1000 views with a cap, e.g., $1,000 or 1M views), (3) mixed base + lower CPM, (4) **base + view milestones** — the one standardized on.
- Milestones: **20K / 100K / 500K / 1M views → bonuses of $60 / $200 / $500 / $800**, on top of **$20 base per video**. Averages ~**$1 per 1000 views but effectively cheaper**, because: (a) between-milestone views are free ("triangles"), (b) mega-viral videos (5-10M) drive CPM down, (c) **only ONE of the 4 platform posts needs to hit the milestone — bonuses don't stack across platforms** (a video with 2M on each of 4 platforms = 8M views pays a single $800 bonus), (d) residual views after payout are effectively capped.
- Contract experiment: 30 creators offered a choice of CPM (slightly higher expected pay, explicitly disclosed) vs. base+milestone; **29 of 30 chose base+milestone**. Read: creators value the guaranteed $20/video base over higher expected CPM returns; milestones also gamify. Also operationally simpler than CPM true-ups. (first-party experiment, claimed)

## Deal structures

- Base pay: **$20 per video** (a "video" = one edit, posted to 4 platforms).
- Milestone bonuses per video: **20K views → +$60; 100K → +$200; 500K → +$500; 1M → +$800** [note: transcript lists "60… 200, 500 and then 800"; pairing with milestones inferred from order — the 500K→$500 pairing is the natural reading but not stated one-by-one].
- Bonuses non-stacking across platforms: only one post of the four needs to cross a threshold; hitting on two platforms does NOT pay twice.
- Effective cost ~$1/1000 views on paper, less in practice (free between-milestone views, 1M cap on mega-virals, single-platform trigger).
- Trial deal: $100 total ($20 × 5 videos) for a ~5-day paid trial.
- CPM alternative offered in contracts: ~$1 per 1,000 views with a cap (~$1,000 / ~1M views); 1 of 30 creators took it.
- Uncapped posting volume; no exclusivity or retainer terms discussed.

## Contrarian positions

- **One format only, forever** — against the standard advice to run many content angles/tests simultaneously; "no deviation" for 1.2B views.
- **Creators prefer guaranteed base + milestones over higher-expected-value CPM** (29/30) — against the assumption that rational creators maximize expected payout; certainty + gamification win.
- **Don't judge trial creators on views** — power law makes 5 videos statistically meaningless; judge structure comprehension, consistency, communication instead.
- **Soft-skippable paywall BEFORE the hard gate outperforms a single hard paywall** (combined 16.5%) — against the pure hard-paywall-at-onboarding-end orthodoxy.
- **Uncapped posting volume** (20+/day/creator) doesn't trigger meaningful platform penalties on YouTube/Instagram — against widespread shadow-ban fear (TikTok excepted, needs ramp).
- Weekly $8.99 beats yearly for this app — against the annual-plan-first conventional wisdom.
- Zero-margin, grow-at-all-costs reinvestment while bootstrapped on a personal loan — against bootstrapper profit-first norms.

## Crave transfer

The load-bearing caveat: Halo AI is a single-session AI-wow entertainment app with global TAM, viral-demo-native output, and self-admitted weak retention — the near-opposite of Crave's local, repeat-use utility, so the headline numbers (0.3-0.5% view→funnel conversion, 6:1 ROAS, 300k MRR pace, $8.99/wk pricing) should NOT be treated as attainable benchmarks; the speaker himself says entertainment virality "is not lasting" and that durable value lives in health/wealth/relationships-style retention — which is closer to Crave's actual bet. What DOES transfer: the mechanical UGC playbook is category-agnostic and cheap enough for Crave's zero-ad-budget launch — sourcing via UGC Facebook/Reddit groups, $20/video base + non-stacking view milestones (~$1 CPM effective), a $100 five-video paid trial judged on structure not views, one proven format replicated by all creators, and a Discord community as the retention layer; Austin-local food UGC is a natively viral vertical, and the "views × constant per-format conversion" measurement discipline is exactly how to know within weeks whether a format pays. The double-paywall finding (skippable paywall → browse → hard gate at first action, combined 16.5%) is the most directly testable claim against Crave's current hard-paywall-at-onboarding-end design — letting users browse the Austin map/rankings before gating the action could plausibly convert the uncertain middle, though 16.5% is one app's unaudited number on an impulse-priced weekly SKU. Weekly pricing is likely a category artifact (impulse entertainment) and shouldn't dislodge Crave's $7.99/mo / $39.99/yr structure. Discount everything ~2x for survivorship and the podcast's incentive stack (Superwall sells paywall tooling; the guest is marketing himself), but the deal-structure numbers are concrete enough to copy as a starting grid.
