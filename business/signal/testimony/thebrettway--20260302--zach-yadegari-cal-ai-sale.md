## Arc

Zach Yadegari, 18, co-founded Cal AI (photo-based calorie tracking), scaled it from launch to a claimed ~$50M ARR in about 1.5 years, and sold 100% of the company to competitor MyFitnessPal (price undisclosed, no retained equity). This is a follow-up interview — 1.5 years earlier he was at ~$1M/mo — recorded the day before the acquisition announcement. The video is largely a playbook recap: influencer/UGC to ~$2M/mo, then cracking paid ads + an affiliate creator program to $5.7M/mo.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Price point: **$30/year** annual subscription. Transcript repeatedly says "$30" for yearly revenue too — "$30 in that year" almost certainly means **$30M revenue in 2025** (transcription dropped "million"); flagged as auto-caption ambiguity but the $30M reading is confirmed by "zero to 30 million in ARR" later. (claimed)
- Unit economics framing: "trying to run ads 20 bucks, make 30 bucks" — target profitable on first purchase, immediately, because bootstrapped/cash-constrained. (claimed)
- Industry annual-subscription **retention ~30%** for health & fitness apps (i.e., ~70% don't renew); he declined to share Cal AI's own number. Attributed to apps that "require a lot of discipline." (secondhand/industry claim)
- Year-2 renewals now layering on top of new-customer revenue ("now we have renewals coming in since we're a year later"). (claimed)

### Trial & onboarding

- **3-day free trial**; ad pixel optimized for **trial start**, not purchase, because Apple only allows high-quality signal in the **first 24 hours** — a conversion event 3 days later trains Facebook's algorithm too slowly. They back-model: known average trial→paid conversion rate × cost-per-trial-start = profitability estimate. (first-party mechanic, claimed numbers)

### UGC & creator deals

- Phase 1 (to ~$1–2M/mo): all influencer posting, fitness niche. Ceiling hit because big fitness influencers **share the same audiences** — diminishing "bang for the buck." Then broadened to non-fitness influencers. (claimed)
- Influencers "definitely account for a few million every month" even after paid ads scaled — channel never died. (claimed)
- **Mr Beast sponsorship: $500,000** for placement in a behind-the-scenes video of a viral weight-loss challenge (contestant ~200 lb, had to lose ~100 lb for a prize pool). Zach appeared in the video. Deal sourced via a friend on the Beast team ("Nagesh" — name uncertain). Beast team **never invoiced**; Zach proactively reached out months later to pay. (first-party)
- Beast ad was **slightly unprofitable on direct conversions** but paid off in brand authority — "helped us sign many new deals going forward," net probably profitable. (claimed, unverifiable)
- Beast attribution modeling: assume ~**1 in 10** viewers who convert actually click the description link; ~**1 in 8** use promo codes; triangulate with day-of revenue spike vs. average day; accept the number if independent methods converge. (first-party method)
- **Affiliate creator program**: any creator can make videos run as ads, paid a **percentage of revenue** (% not stated); "some creators making a lot of money." Managed on **Tribe**; communication via a WhatsApp group. This — not paid ads alone — is credited for the jump from $3M to $5.7M/mo. (first-party)
- **Creator-only app build**: a second version of the app for influencers to screen-record — big Cal AI logo on every page, fake "perfect streak" (~30 days) — so ads look pretty and the brand is legible. Compared to McDonald's burger-styling. (first-party)

### Paid ads

- Agency phase: hired an agency initially; **capped at $5K/day spend**, couldn't scale; blames over-complicated campaign structures. General stance: agencies "never care as much as someone on the team." Moved in-house, churned through a few hires, lost "a lot of money" learning. (first-party)
- Influencer-style subtle creatives (3-sec app cameo in a 30-sec morning-routine video) capped at ~**$10K/day, maybe $15K**. Direct-sell creatives (whole video about the app, pitch within ~5 seconds) broke through to ~**$40K/day** spend. (first-party)
- Winning creatives now are mostly **not talking-head**: visual demo (gym footage + scanning food) with a hook caption like "how I cut X pounds in a month." (first-party)
- Campaign structure: **one scale campaign (CBO) + one testing campaign**, kept deliberately simple. **No interest targeting, no lookalikes** — "you target with creative"; even retargeting is done in the copy ("You've probably seen our ads before"). Cites Meta's **Andromeda** algorithm update as why creative volume beats settings. (first-party)
- Attribution "sauce": **App Store custom product pages** per ad, uploaded as the ad's destination link in Facebook — Apple reports revenue per custom page, giving clean per-ad revenue. Multiply by ~**+30%** for people who see the ad but search the App Store manually. Breaks down at high ad counts (too many pages to manage). (first-party)
- MMP: **AppsFlyer** ("you have to use one for apps") — but "supposed to give you clear data… never really does"; a team member hand-models actuals. Incrementality tests (turn a channel off a few days, compare) are part of the toolkit. Apple privacy rules make even performance-ad attribution hard; no tool covers all touch points. (first-party)
- Advice to founders: learn the **20% that accounts for 80%** (talk to founder friends, free content), budget **$50–$100/day** of experiment spend, then hire someone who really knows it. (first-party opinion)
- Channel hierarchy claim: for subscription/pay-upfront models, performance ads (TikTok/Facebook) "will always take you the furthest" because of user volume — though not necessarily highest ROI; social apps are a completely different game. (opinion)
- Organic → paid flywheel: massive prior influencer awareness made direct-response ads convert better — "paid ads is where you liquidate" warmed-up audiences (host's phrasing, Zach agrees). (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing

- Zero-budget path he prescribes: identify problem → know the marketing plan **before** building ("I always knew from the start… what marketing strategies I would take") → make videos yourself (1 in 10 goes viral, "makes you $1,000") → reinvest revenue into UGC creators → ads take you "from a million to 5 million, or 10K to 100K, depending on scale." (opinion + first-party pattern)
- Timeline: launch → sale ≈ **1.5 years**; $1M/mo at 17; $3M/mo when starting college; **$5.7M in January [2026]**; ~$50M ARR at sale; 2025 full-year revenue $30M. (claimed)
- Sale: MyFitnessPal bought **100%**, apps remain separate, Zach staying temporarily for transition, **no retained equity**; cash/stock structure not disclosed; deal came from a long relationship, not a bank process. (first-party, terms withheld)

### Retention & product

- Design excellence framed as relentless iteration ("standard of excellence… wouldn't stop iterating"), not accident. (claimed)

- Founder time now mostly on talent: hiring, vision-setting. When he stepped away for a month at college start, morale and numbers dipped; returned and they recovered — vision-setting is "the most important part" post-PMF. (claimed)

Complementary skills + a designated final decision-maker per domain ("zones of genius") framed as essential. (first-party)

## Contrarian positions

- **No audience targeting at all** — no interests, no lookalikes, retargeting done inside the creative copy; "the algorithm is so good… you target with creative."
- **Optimize the pixel for trial start, not purchase**, and back-model profitability — against the "optimize for the money event" default, forced by Apple's 24-hour signal window.
- **Influencer content ≠ ad creative**: subtle native integrations that work organically actively fail as paid creatives; ads must sell directly within 5 seconds. Most winning ads have no talking person at all.
- **Agencies are structurally misaligned**; learn 80/20 yourself specifically so you can hire well, not to run it forever.
- Morning-routine guru habits (journaling/meditation) explicitly did **not** drive his success — he stopped them before Cal AI took off.
- Marketing-first idea selection: never build anything without knowing the distribution plan first.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is sequencing and mechanics, not the numbers: know the content/distribution plan before launch, self-made videos → reinvest into creators → paid ads only once organic awareness exists, and the custom-product-page attribution trick (directly usable and cheap for a solo founder). The "creator version of the app" idea — a screen-record-friendly build with the brand legible and data looking its best — transfers cleanly to Crave demo content, and "trial-start pixel + back-modeled conversion" is directly relevant to Crave's annual-only trial. But be skeptical of the growth math: Cal AI is a global-TAM, visually instant AI-wow utility at $30/yr with a 3-day trial — Crave is single-city, repeat-use local discovery at a higher effective price with a hard paywall, so viral UGC economics ("1 in 10 videos goes viral") and $40K/day Meta scaling don't map; Austin-geofenced ad spend and Austin-local creators are a different, thinner market. The ~30% annual retention benchmark is a useful sanity floor for LTV modeling, but everything here is unaudited founder self-report told the day before an acquisition PR blitz — classic survivor testimony with an incentive to make the playbook look inevitable.
