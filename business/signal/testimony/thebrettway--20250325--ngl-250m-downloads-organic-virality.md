## Arc

Hunter Isaacson, 25, is the designer/co-founder behind NGL, an anonymous Q&A app riding Instagram's story-link feature that claims 250M downloads since 2022 with effectively zero marketing spend, plus Wink (~60–70M downloads) built with the former Musical.ly president who recruited him at 21. The episode is a founder-story interview mining his organic-virality playbook, then pivots to his current bootstrapped bet, Bags, a social crypto trading app. NGL now runs "on autopilot" as passive cash flow while he goes all-in on Bags.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- NGL monetizes via a **weekly subscription in every country**, priced by country GDP: roughly **$1/wk vs $5/wk vs $8/wk** from small countries to the US. Rationale: "in some countries $10 a week is like a salary… I didn't want to price out parts of the world." Claims this GDP-tiered pricing + full localization is a major reason it went viral globally (huge in Asia, Middle East, North Africa). (claimed)
- Premium features: hints about who sent a message (sender location, phone type — kids triangulate with notebooks), plus link-viewer counts. Deliberately simple; "nobody really got mad about anything" tried under premium. (claimed)
- Monetization/pricing existed **from day one** (they were testing different prices from launch, GDP tiers came later). (claimed)
- Bags: takes the "industry standard" **1% fee** on buys/sells only (none on send/receive) and **passes back the majority of fees** to active/followed traders and referrers. (first-party claim about own product)

### Trial & onboarding

- Viral-era NGL onboarding was **one step**: type your Instagram username. "No login, no authentication, no phone number, nothing" — only needed to pull the profile pic for the story asset. (claimed)
- Design law: every page has **one central action** — one big obvious button; branching flows avoided; one main onboarding path straight to the core metric (for NGL: share the link + post a few replies). "When you make people start to doubt what to do… they go back to TikTok." (anecdote/philosophy)
- Make the button **pulse/wiggle/glow** — "a lot of people don't do that last 5% polish" and that polish is what gets people to finish the flow. Grayed-out button until the field is typed = self-evident gating. (anecdote)
- Loading moments should be **anticipation/priming** moments ("getting this ready for you, ETA one minute") followed by over-delivery + confetti/delight. (anecdote)
- "People want things to work the way they assume they should work" — meet perception, not logical perfection; if it matches expectation the user thinks the product is amazing. (anecdote)
- Bags onboarding: sign up in **5 seconds** (any email/OAuth), fund wallet with Apple Pay, buy a memecoin within **60 seconds**. Key retention insight: users who **fund their account** show high retention and high session time, so the whole growth battle is compressing time-to-funding ("get to that magic moment faster"). (first-party claim)

### UGC & creator deals

- The NGL ignition event: an influencer paid **$50** to post the link on her story → a follower in New Zealand who was popular in high school posted it → activated her whole school → New Zealand/Australia regional pop. (claimed)
- Total lifetime marketing spend: **~$5–7k** in influencer tests (total expenses ~$10k including a few thousand on a never-used domain, "Ask.fun"). All 250M downloads after ignition: "no marketing, zero dollars, 100% organic." (claimed)
- The breakout TikTok push: **5–10 TikToks total**; one hit **1M views** on a **CPM deal (~$3/1000 views ⇒ they owed her ~$3k)** they couldn't afford (both founders had day jobs), so they took the video down — "it didn't matter because it already activated." Later paid her "a lot more." (claimed; the $3k figure is stated, CPM rate inferred from 1M views/$3k)
- Growth threshold observed: from **~1,000 downloads/day** to **1.5M downloads/day within a few days** (~**90,000 downloads/hour**) once critical mass hit. The "**1,000 users in one day**" threshold was the key: at that point the story permeated the entire Instagram friend graph of those users and extended outward. (claimed)
- Host pitches his content-rewards model: post e.g. **$5,000**, pay **$1 per 1,000 views**, set content requirements (must show app, discuss a trade, affiliate link in bio); hundreds of UGC creators submit; pay only after content is created/edited/posted. Guest is receptive ("that's beautiful") for Bags. (host's product pitch, incentive-laden)
- Bags referral: every user has an auto **ref link**; post it anywhere and you earn a **percentage of your referees' trade fees** indefinitely ("have whale friends, send them your link"). Group-chat links double as ref links. (first-party)

### Content strategy (organic)

- Core doctrine: **organic virality through design** — the viral loop IS the product. NGL loop: see friend's story → tap link → send anonymous message (experience the receiving end) → big "get your own messages" button → post your own link. First contact always carries a friend's implicit endorsement ("if your friends are doing it, it's socially acceptable"). (claimed)
- Replies were the retention/spread lever: users posting **multiple public replies** signaled social acceptability and increased their link's usage; the "perfect user" = posts a link + shares a few replies. They explicitly did NOT optimize app screen time — "it just wasn't an important metric for us." (claimed)
- School-by-school seeding tactic: activate one random high school; if the product is real, 5 users in a school "eats the whole school," and one cross-school friendship eats the next school. "If you can prove that once at a small scale it'll work globally." Prior proof at Wink: pay-99c-or-share-on-Snapchat gem mechanic produced "millions of organic downloads." (claimed)
- **Android was the unlock**: NZ/AU virality stalled because the hot region was Android-heavy and they were iOS-only; friend built the Android app in **3 weeks**; expansion resumed. (claimed)
- Naming: renamed from "Ask.fun" to **NGL** pre-virality because the name itself explains the product to the demographic in one beat ("oh, it's my NGL link… I'm not going to lie"). Colors red/orange chosen to pop next to the Instagram icon; "people like tapping red buttons." (anecdote)
- NGL still does ~**100M downloads/year** with no marketing; churned users get re-acquired by the still-running loop (friend posts → redownload). Strong retained cohorts claimed but no numbers. (claimed)

### Paid ads

- "I've actually never really done paid" — beyond the ~$5–7k seeding tests, zero paid acquisition across 250M downloads. Open to paid for Bags (a revenue-per-user product) in a way he wasn't for NGL. (claimed)

### ASO & app store

- "The app store is a pure meritocracy… same sandbox, same tools, no advantages." Only tip: don't submit until the app is mostly ready, to avoid drawn-out review cycles. (anecdote)
- Apple payout gotcha: **payouts delayed ~60 days**; during hypergrowth they racked up software/API bills (billions of requests), had providers shut them off, and **took out a loan** to cover bills before Apple paid. "First three to six months afterwards was pretty hard." (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing

- NGL: idea Oct 2021 (Instagram opened story links to everyone, previously 10k-follower gated) → built in **3–4 weeks** to v1 → **6 months of essentially zero traction** (and no clones) → NZ/AU spark Apr–May 2022 → Android in 3 weeks → 5–10 TikToks → detonation. (claimed)
- He watched Firebase geo graphs to see the spread stall (NZ-only, not permeating) and diagnosed the Android gap — instrument where users are, fix the mechanical blocker. (claimed)
- Build cadence for a simple app: ~**1 week design** in Figma → engineers scope/module-by-module → founder tries to break the build → QA list → few cycles → ship. NGL was his **8th app**. (claimed)
- Idea validation "in those days" = pure intuition that the mechanic would go viral, grounded in a prior firsthand proof of the same channel mechanic (Wink's Snapchat share). No formal validation. (claimed)
- Bags counter-example: launched Nov [2024], then months of heads-down performance work (99.9% of transactions land in a couple seconds) BEFORE starting marketing — "I started marketing it once we fixed enough of the bugs." Product foundation first, growth features layered later; "very opposite my other apps." (first-party)
- Studio-vs-all-in: studios are for throwing simple consumer bets at the wall, then doubling down on the hit; he went all-in on Bags because he loved the problem/market before knowing the product. Hasn't sold NGL: ~2.5 years of significant revenue, passive, cash-flowing — "why sell out?" (claimed)

### Retention & product

- NGL is share-driven, not session-driven: "the product itself is more about people sharing than about people staying on the app." Seasonal churn is fine because the loop re-acquires churned users. (claimed)
- Bags retention hinge: funded accounts = high retention + high average session time. (first-party)
- AI moderation filters bullying/harassment/hate speech; claims they "increased usage of the [Instagram] link sticker significantly for a cohort of users"; no contact ever from Instagram/Meta. (claimed)

### Team, tools & cost structure

- Aggregate: "my companies have done over **$50 million in revenue** over the last few years"; NGL "number one in over 150 countries." Wink **60–70M downloads**. (claimed, unaudited)
- Recipe team = 3 skill sets: product/design (him), technical lead/CTO, growth (influencer outreach/videos) — can be 1–3 people. Splits equity "way more evenly than most people" — the person who wakes up at 3am when everything's crashing needs real equity. (anecdote)
- Stack to launch an app: Figma, App Store Connect, TestFlight, Firebase or AWS, Cursor/coding environment, Xcode, Mixpanel or Amplitude. NGL is Swift-native ("animations are the best on Swift"); Bags is React Native cross-platform. (claimed)
- Bags: **300k+ registered profiles**, active traders "a lot less"; bootstrapped entirely from NGL cash flow; ideation summer 2023, fully locked in since early 2024; built on Solana; embedded non-custodial wallet. (first-party)

### Other

- His education: 2 years as sole designer at a mobile-app holding company under the ex-president of Musical.ly (who led the ByteDance acquisition, "a billion dollars"); they'd print app screens onto whiteboards and reverse-engineer flows. "That job was my college." (claimed)
- Zoom University had a **7:1 girl-to-guy ratio**; couple hundred thousand downloads, top 10 social. First app (Leader) failed — overengineered, killed by COVID. (claimed)

## Deal structures

- Influencer story post: **$50 flat** (the one that ignited NZ). (claimed)
- Breakout TikTok: **CPM deal**, 1M views ⇒ **~$3,000 owed** (≈$3 CPM); video voluntarily taken down for lack of funds, virality persisted; creator later paid "a lot more" retroactively. (claimed)
- Total influencer testing budget: **$5–7k** (~$10k total company expenses pre-virality). (claimed)
- Host's content-rewards template (his product, not NGL's history): fund a pool (e.g. **$5,000**), pay **$1/1,000 views** post-hoc, with content requirements + affiliate-link-in-bio mandate. (host pitch)
- Bags: perpetual **rev-share of trade fees to referrer** via auto ref links; "majority" of the 1% fee passed back to users/referrers/copied traders. (first-party)

## Contrarian positions

- **Zero paid acquisition at scale** — 250M downloads on ~$5–7k of tests; rejects the paid-UA treadmill entirely for viral-mechanic products, while conceding paid may fit products (like Bags) where users aren't inherently sharing.
- **Screen time is not a metric worth optimizing** for a share-loop product — directly against engagement-time orthodoxy.
- **Don't validate; design for virality and ship in 3–4 weeks** — no surveys, no landing pages; the only validation is a small-scale replication (one school) of the loop.
- **Six months of zero traction is survivable and doesn't mean the thesis is wrong** — they changed nothing fundamental; the mechanic just hadn't caught its spark yet.
- **GDP-tiered pricing everywhere** rather than uniform premium pricing — deliberately leaving US-margin on the table to keep the global loop unbroken.
- **Split equity nearly evenly** — against standard founder-heavy cap tables.
- **App Store as pure meritocracy** — dismisses ASO gamesmanship as a lever.

## Crave transfer

Almost none of the growth mechanics transfer directly: NGL is a K-factor social product whose distribution IS the product (every use emits an Instagram story to hundreds of viewers), while Crave is a paid utility whose usage is invisible to non-users — Crave has no equivalent "post a link, friends must tap it" loop, and the 1,000-users-in-a-day friend-graph detonation math simply doesn't apply to a hard-paywalled discovery tool. The transferable pieces are narrower: (1) his onboarding doctrine — one central action per screen, one unbranched path to the core moment, last-5%-polish on the primary button — maps cleanly onto Crave's onboarding-to-paywall funnel; (2) the Bags insight that a single "funding/commitment moment" predicts retention supports Crave's pay-now gate framing, though Bags shows it as correlation among survivors, not proof that forcing the moment earlier lifts LTV; (3) the school-by-school density tactic is structurally analogous to Crave's one-city launch — prove saturation in Austin before widening, and instrument geography (his Firebase-graph diagnosis) to see where spread stalls. Heavy discounts warranted: this is a single unaudited survivor account (8th app, 7 prior misses he glosses over), the "zero marketing" framing conveniently excludes the paid ignition that made everything happen, and his weekly $1–8 impulse pricing for teens says nothing about a $7.99/mo card-required adult utility. The $50-influencer / ~$3-CPM data points are 2022-era teen-social rates, not 2026 food-content rates.
