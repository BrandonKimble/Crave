## Arc

Caleb Dean built Runafy, a "ranked competitive running" social app (the "LiftOff of running"), validated it with copied Instagram Reels formats and a ChatGPT-built Stripe landing page before writing the app, used Apple pre-orders to seed 3,000 day-one downloads, and sold it to an app-studio buyer (deal brokered via a PE firm) after just 26 days of revenue at a ~5x ARR valuation on an ESTIMATED $3K MRR. The episode is his full playbook: validate distribution before building, market during development, and engineer the launch.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Pre-launch "early adopter" offer: $5 one-time via a Stripe link on a ChatGPT-generated HTML landing page — ~90 people paid ($5 buyers were promised a LIFETIME subscription, which he had to manually fulfill at launch). Screenshot showed "80 successful transactions... I think is like $400 US dollars." (claimed, screenshot shown)
- Launch email included a referral/discount code for the subscription at half price; "the majority of our revenue [on launch day] was probably from those discount codes." (claimed)
- Subscription with 7-day trials; at acquisition, ~"20, 30" 7-day trials were "set to trigger," used to extrapolate MRR. (claimed)
- Paruresis app claimed "30 plus percent conversion rate" — attributed purely to niche desperation + ASO intent. (claimed, via his own tweet)

### Trial & onboarding

- V1 onboarding was assembled by copying LiftOff plus Cal AI, Quitter, Life Reset, Clean Eights ("why try and reinvent the wheel"). (claimed)
- First onboarding "did well, but the conversion rate wasn't that high"; post-launch rework repositioned from "cool app that has ranks" to outcome-driven ("these ranks will help you achieve this") — aligning onboarding with the competitive-runner marketing persona. No before/after numbers given. (claimed)
- Full app UX designed in Figma in ~3 days of ~15-hour days before development, so the developer "can come in and just crush it." (claimed)

### Content strategy (organic)

- ~90% of distribution was organic: Instagram primarily, a little TikTok. (claimed)
- Copied LiftOff's exact Reels format (rank/medal-times video); his copy "got 2 million views"; icons ChatGPT-generated. The base content took 30 minutes to make, scheduled via a scheduling tool. (claimed)
- Posted 9 videos/day on Instagram "cuz Instagram really doesn't punish you for that"; ~50 videos in ~2 weeks during validation → 2,000 waitlist emails + 90 x $5 payments before the app existed. (claimed; host restates "posted 50 videos in a week" — timeframe inconsistent, 1 vs 2 weeks)
- View economics: average 5–10K views per video, "one in 10 videos would go above 500K"; ~5M views in the first month (intro says 5M; Caleb later says "5 views" — clearly a transcription garble of 5M). (claimed)
- TikTok contrast: one video hit ~700K views, but increasing post quantity cratered views "to like five" — TikTok punishes volume, Instagram doesn't → doubled down on Instagram. (claimed)
- CTO built an internal tool in one day that generates 10,000 format variations in ~1 minute (varying times/captions); a couple of captions consistently performed. (claimed)
- Track-and-field-event videos went most viral early — read as signal that the competitive segment resonated. (claimed)
- Instagram Stories polls on ~2,000 highly engaged followers used as product research (e.g., track in-app vs watch vs Strava). (claimed)
- Agency-era lesson: "quality nearly always beats quantity" for creator content — one video hitting 6M in the right audience changes a business; but his agency model (10 creators × 30–60 videos/mo = 300–600 videos/mo, 3–4 revisions each) drowned him in revisions and didn't scale. (claimed)
- Twitter launch video (Rotato demo, tactic copied from Blake Anderson): ~50K views; his "sold my company" quote-tweet got ~586K views; estimates 40–50K views in first couple days flowed from it. He had ~200 followers when the acquisition-relevant posting happened. (claimed, screenshots shown)

### ASO & app store

- Apple PRE-ORDER as launch weapon (the centerpiece claim): ship a bare-bones approvable V1 (two tabs, no onboarding), get approved, list as pre-order while marketing during development. Pre-orders auto-download at launch AND Apple emails every pre-orderer. Pre-order live only ~2 weeks → 3,000 pre-orders → 3,000 day-one installs; leaderboard filled to its 1,000-user limit within an hour. (claimed)
- Pre-order gotcha: Apple forces a fixed release date you must keep pushing back; he forgot once and the buggy two-tab V1 auto-launched to ~1,000 users, generating ~20 complaint emails; he reverted it to pre-order. (claimed)
- Paruresis app: majority of users/revenue came via ASO on condition-name searches (paruresis / "shy bladder"). (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing

- Sequence: idea → validate demand with copied content format + Stripe link ($5/waitlist) → 2K emails, 90 payments → recruit team via Instagram story + 10X audience inbound → 3-day Figma build of whole app → CTO "grinded 14 hours a day for a month and a half" → bare V1 approval → 2-week pre-order while still marketing → launch (email blast, Reddit, Twitter demo video, personal messages to every early adopter) → acquisition DM ~1–2 weeks post-launch → close at ~26–28 days. (claimed)
- Waitlist email performance at launch: ~32% went to spam, ~20% open rate ("apparently decent for an email that's not warmed up") — his argument that "waitlists are stupid" vs pre-order's Apple-delivered install+email. (claimed)
- Acquisition numbers: 26 (or 27) days of revenue; estimated $3K MRR from actuals + pending trials; 4.5–5x ARR multiple; host summarizes "100k 180k valuation" (exact price never stated — uncertain). Deal terms: Caleb retained 30% equity, 6-month earnout of additional cash, plus a cash bonus not tied to equity; buyer (app studio, deal signed off by a PE firm) brings dev team, distribution team, and reinvestment capital. Due diligence took "a couple days" (App Store Connect access, checking users weren't bought); six contracts negotiated; funds via escrow. (claimed, DM screenshots shown)
- Status at buyer contact: "two to 3K rev... two to 3K downloads, 50 to 100 downloads a day." (claimed)
- Ideation checklist: enter a space only where 1–3 apps do ≥$100K/mo (LiftOff at $5K/mo would NOT have been enough validation); don't clone directly — adjacent-ify (ranked gym → ranked running); avoid one-app monopoly niches; discount VC-funded comps (Reframe: $500K–1M/mo but raised $24M — "70k downloads, 400k revenue" on Sensor Tower may hide "$50K spend for 10K MRR"; they play a 10-year game). (claimed/secondhand)
- LiftOff validation bar: doing $200–300K/mo at validation time, >$100K/mo sustained for ≥12 months; later "300k downloads, 700k revenue per month." (secondhand)

### Retention & product

- Day-one leaderboard seeding framed as solving THE cold-start problem for a social app; host adds that synchronized launch (everyone arriving the same day) likely helps long-term retention vs trickled arrivals. (claimed/speculation)
- Only engagement metric he had at sale: ~7 sessions per device on average, "I really didn't have much benchmark for that though." (claimed)
- 50–80 early adopters became a feedback/bug-testing corps; he got ~20 users' WhatsApp numbers, sent TestFlight builds personally. Biggest product decision from users: tracking data evenly split Garmin/Apple Watch/Strava → built ALL integrations plus own GPS tracking. (claimed)
- Audience was ~99% male, unintentionally. Next app targets women in health/fitness because he found a demand gap. (claimed)

### Team, tools & cost structure

- Two initial developers for 2–3 weeks (didn't work out); third dev became CTO, worked 14 hr/day for ~1.5 months. Caleb did all design + decisions. Team recruited free via Instagram story + 10X stream audience. (claimed)
- Tools: FlutterFlow (first app, no-code), ChatGPT (landing page, icons), Figma (full app design), Rotato (demo video), a post-scheduling tool, internal variation-generator tool. Essentially zero paid acquisition mentioned. (claimed)
- Backstory: got hired as 10X CMO by cold-pursuing Blake Anderson (portfolio Notion doc answering his "bench 225 / build an app / 1M views in a day" bar — subbed marathon-with-broken-leg for the bench), guessed his email, hired within 48 hours, 12-hour days. (claimed)
- First app (paruresis) lifetime revenue $5–10K. (claimed)

## Deal structures

- Runafy acquisition: ~$3K estimated MRR × 4.5–5x ARR (host: "100k 180k valuation" — exact figure uncertain); seller retains 30% equity; 6-month cash earnout; separate cash bonus; escrow-settled; six contracts; days-long diligence.
- Pre-launch early-adopter deal: $5 one-time → lifetime subscription (~90 takers).
- Launch waitlist deal: referral/discount code for ~50% off the subscription; drove the majority of launch-day revenue.
- No creator/UGC/affiliate deals with stated terms (his old agency work and the 50-influencer CPM/RPM sheet for 10X are mentioned but no terms given).

## Contrarian positions

- "Waitlists are stupid" — Apple pre-order beats an email waitlist because installs are automatic and Apple sends the launch email (vs his 32% spam / 20% open rates).
- Post 9x/day on Instagram — volume is not punished there (explicitly opposite on TikTok).
- Sell at ~4 weeks despite believing in a $100K/mo path — "10% of a watermelon beats 80% of a grape," de-risk the first win, use capital + brand as the moat for the next app.
- Don't build until distribution is validated with real money ($5 Stripe payments), not just signups.
- Copy a proven app's format and concept shamelessly into an adjacent vertical; don't reinvent onboarding or content formats.
- Discount Sensor Tower revenue as validation when the comp is VC-funded.

## Crave transfer

The transferable pieces are the pre-build distribution validation (copied short-form format + $5 Stripe commitment beats a waitlist for signal) and the Apple pre-order launch — the latter is unusually apt for Crave: a city-local app with a hard paywall benefits enormously from concentrating Austin installs on one day, and Apple's own launch email partially substitutes for having no ad budget. The Instagram-volume playbook (9 posts/day, templatized variations) transfers only if Crave finds a comparably meme-able format (ranked-dish/top-10-Austin lists are a plausible analog to his medal-times videos); his was a globally viral competitive-fitness format, while Crave's audience is geo-fenced, so 5M-view economics won't replicate. Do NOT transfer the valuation/exit math or the "it worked in 26 days" timeline: this is a survivor story on estimated MRR, told on a podcast whose host sells paywall tooling, and the app category (social/gamified, network-effect, male-fitness demo) is a mismatch for a paid utility. His half-price launch discount driving "majority of launch-day revenue" is a caution for Crave's pay-now hard paywall — his data suggests launch-day conversion leaned hard on a discount lever Crave currently doesn't plan to offer.
