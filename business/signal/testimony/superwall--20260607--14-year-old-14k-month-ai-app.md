## Arc

Evan Yadegari, a 14-year-old (now 15, ninth grade) and younger brother of Cal AI founder Zach Yadegari, scaled Locked — a gamified self-improvement/task app for Gen Z — to a claimed $14k+/month almost entirely through manual influencer outreach at scale. The video is a Superwall-podcast playbook episode: how he tested channels, the DM outreach machine, four creator deal structures, and his new product 10X, which the episode partly promotes.

## Deal structures

Four structures pitched (all first-party claimed terms):

1. **Standard flat**: ~$500 for 1 video or ~$1,200 for 4 videos — only for creators with consistently huge views.
2. **CPM deal**: $1–2 per 1,000 views, always with a cap (e.g., capped at $500) so a viral outlier doesn't blow the budget; CPM must stay under your RPM ($3–4 for Locked).
3. **Minimum-view clause** (his favorite, most successful for Locked): e.g., $1,000/post with a 500,000-minimum-view clause — if the first video misses, the creator keeps posting until videos collectively hit the benchmark. Sized at $1–2 effective CPM: pay $800 → set the clause at 400–800K views. Alternative: proportional payout ($400 deal / 400K clause / creator stops at 50K views → pay $50). Anchoring the full number motivates creators.
4. **Bonus deal**: e.g., $250 base + $250 more if the video hits 250,000 views; mixable with the other three.

## Contrarian positions

- Follower counts "don't matter at all" — engagement quality (comment realness, emoji ratio) and per-video views are the whole filter.
- Manual DMs at industrial scale (5–10k creators, 500/day across multiple bought-follower verified accounts) beats automation/agencies — pure volume + a two-word hook ("paid promo?").
- Never let the creator link-attribute; accept spike-based attribution rather than fight Instagram's limits.
- Minimum-view clauses shift virality risk onto the creator — the opposite of the standard flat-fee sponsorship most creators expect.
- Always escalate DMs to a call before negotiating.

## Crave transfer

The deal-structure math is the most transferable asset: price creator deals at a CPM below your measured RPM, prefer minimum-view clauses or capped CPM to shift risk, and vet by comment quality not follower count — all mechanics Crave can use for Austin food creators. But the headline numbers do NOT transfer: Locked is a Gen Z lifestyle app with a $7/week impulse plan, ~10% download→sub conversion, and a globally unlimited audience; Crave is a $7.99–$39.99 hard-paywall local utility whose addressable views are only Austin-relevant, so Locked's $3–4 RPM and $1–2 CPM targets are the wrong anchors — Crave must measure its own RPM before signing any CPM-indexed deal, and geo-targeted food creators will command higher effective CPMs on far smaller (but better-fit) audiences. The mass-DM machine (bought followers, verified burner accounts) is ToS-gray and reputationally risky for a brand courting local creators. All figures are self-reported by a founder currently selling a platform (10X) that this exact playbook advertises; treat everything as directional, and note the transcript's own cold-open contradicts the body on which channel won (Reddit vs influencers).
