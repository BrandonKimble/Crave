## Arc

Interview with Yev (uncertain), head of growth at Sizzle AI, an edtech/learning app, previously an operator of 10+ faceless viral YouTube-shorts channels claiming a billion-plus cumulative views. The video exists to teach his core playbook: mass-produce organic creative variations, put platform-minimum ad dollars behind every one to surface outliers fast ("directional patterns" over statistical significance), then use paid ads as a data firehose to optimize the in-app funnel (onboarding, paywall) within days instead of months. Host runs a Superwall-sponsored community.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Paywall placement is variable and tested: "paywall related steps whether it's in the onboarding or after product usage." (claimed)
- Paywall split tests: free-vs-paid plan comparison layouts, more vs less detail, social-proof placement — big variations first, then "micro variations." (claimed)
- Social proof test had mixed results: engagement up, purchases down in some cells; purchases up only in certain demographics/pathways; judged neutral once assisted conversions were counted. (claimed)
- Whether a user gets a free trial or not depends on country: ad targeting is split by country because "this fits into are they going to a free trial or not." (claimed)

### Trial & onboarding

- Core method: identify the single biggest funnel drop-off (>50% at one step) and fix it → "2x to 3x growth moments," vs 10% gains from ordinary feature optimization; achievable "within a week." (claimed)
- Buys tens of thousands of installs via ads specifically to power onboarding/paywall experiments, deliberately accepting a lenient $200 cost-per-purchase for that traffic tier while other campaigns must hit $100 (break-even-ish) or $50 (aggressive/profitable). Three explicit CPP targets: $50 / $100 / $200. (claimed)
- Baseline funnel: ~1% conversion from install all the way to purchase. (claimed)
- Rapid iteration: 3-way and 4-way in-app experiments resolved "within days"; cost per purchase "goes from $500 to $300" as flows are optimized. (claimed)
- Tracks "assisted conversions" — screens that don't convert directly but assist later purchase — as the tiebreaker when purchase metrics are neutral. (claimed)
- Deliberately balances purchases against engagement ("sometimes engagement goes way down… find that hybrid"). (claimed)

### Content strategy (organic)

- It took "between 20 and 30 organic pieces of content" (multiple creators, multiple hook strategies) to find one clear outlier worth replicating. (claimed, first-party example)
- Featured example TikTok: 79,000 likes; as a paid ad it produced "in the ballpark of hundreds of purchases" — not thousands. (claimed)
- Winning-video anatomy: shocking non-verbal hook (facial expression, no talking) → middle = product demo that filters for relevant audience → conclusion that is a pattern-interrupt, often no explicit CTA (the paid-ad link carries attribution); comment section does the "what app is this" work. (claimed)
- Virality/conversion paradox: the most viral content converts worse ("viral for the wrong reasons"); best converters are the "middle virality" ones — dial down clickbait. Explicitly framed: "if a video does go viral, probably means it didn't convert as well." (claimed)
- Creator sourcing: marketplaces like JoinBrands, plus "Side Shift" [name uncertain, garbled] and one platform bleeped in captions, plus their own Discord; also in-house designers/contractors. (claimed)
- TikTok algorithm rewards posting volume itself — "just a lot of posting there" can trigger the algorithm. (claimed)
- Creators with their own followings reach the algorithm differently; same hook format run in a creator's native style can go viral differently. (claimed)
- One extra second of watch time (from an expression/tagline) is the algorithm signal that compounds distribution. (speculation/mechanistic claim)
- His faceless-channel background: fake-text-message videos, SpongeBob, "president head" comedy, oddly-satisfying/crushing content; one channel at 26M views in last 28 days; cumulative "hundreds of millions… over a billion views"; 10+ channels; RPMs low depending on viewer country ("revenue is not high"). (claimed, dashboard shown)
- Cross-niche transfer as a strategy: applying formats from unrelated niches into the learning niche produces virality. (claimed)

### Paid ads

- Central playbook: paid = amplifier and testing engine for organic, not standalone acquisition. Put platform-minimum spend behind EVERY organic piece; 20 creatives × a few dollars each; most get 0–2 clicks, the outlier gets 5–10; keep funding it to confirm or falsify. "Directional patterns," explicitly NOT statistically significant. (claimed)
- Creative is the 10x lever ("the right creative will give you the 10 time difference"); targeting/optimization tweaks are the 10% levers, for later. (claimed)
- Platform minimums cited: ~$5–$20 depending on platform; TikTok minimum $20/ad (can kill at ~$5 spent); Facebook allows lower amounts and easier duplication — run 30–40 creatives there, "as little as a dollar behind each one"; results readable within hours (top-of-funnel clicks only). (claimed)
- Expect 4–5 of 30–40 videos to clearly outperform; 2x/5x/10x spread between winners and the rest. (claimed)
- Targeting split: TikTok works better for US targeting; Facebook better for global. iOS materially better than Android ("iOS is much better"). Keep audience targeting broad — "the algorithm is really good"; segment mainly by device and country because countries differ in LTV/MRR goals and trial flow. (claimed)
- LTV/churn inputs: churn between 3 and 7 months depending on audience/country; per-purchase value ~$30 or ~$70 "or higher" depending on product part, country, device. (claimed)
- Scaling a winner = go deeper first (10 headline variations on the identical creative/targeting — "one word makes a difference"), then horizontal (countries/audiences = "different psychologies and value propositions"); "hundreds of different ads at the ad group level," thousands of ads total; revisit weekly to confirm patterns hold. (claimed)
- Attribution correction stack: real purchases ≈ ~2x what the ad platform reports; lift studies (turn all ads off, watch sales fall, turn back on); ≥10% of purchases arrive later than the 1-day window; another 10–20% via other attribution methods (SKAN vs reported); another 10–20% spillover visible only in App Store Connect (see ad → search → install). (claimed)
- Some in-app journeys convert 10x higher than others (users who start with a particular product vs "roaming"). (claimed)
- $5,000-budget answer for an unknown MVP: spread across as many hypotheses as possible — up to 100 creative variations with ~$1–minimum each — rather than concentrating; "you're going to get zero clicks for almost all of them" and one will get ~20. Alternative: buy or rent an existing channel/audience for "a few hundred [to] $3,000" — a creative there has "a hundred times more likely chance of success." (claimed/speculation)
- Condense the veteran's 500-videos-over-a-year into a week of testing. (claimed)

## Contrarian positions

- Ignore statistical significance early: $1–$20 micro-boosts on dozens of creatives, read "directional patterns" from 5–10 clicks — explicitly "does not follow best practices of data science" (from a self-described former data scientist).
- Most viral ≠ best converting: deliberately choose the middle-virality creatives and reduce clickbait; conventional advice chases maximum virality.
- Don't pick organic vs paid — the alpha is the transition layer: paid micro-amplification as an organic testing instrument, and paid installs as fuel for in-app experiment velocity (buying data at a loss, $200 CPP tier, on purpose).
- Keep ad targeting broad; the algorithm out-targets you — segment only on country/device (because they change LTV and trial flow), not interest.
- With $5k, dilute across ~100 bets instead of concentrating on a few polished ads.
- Assisted conversions as a decision tiebreaker rather than last-click purchase metrics alone.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is the cheap-outlier-search discipline: 20–40 organic creative variations with platform-minimum boosts ($20/ad TikTok, ~$1 Facebook) is executable on Crave's near-zero budget and matches the "no ad budget, organic-first" constraint — treat tiny paid boosts as a testing instrument, not an acquisition channel. The middle-virality-converts-better finding and the winning-video anatomy (non-verbal hook → product demo filters the audience → no hard CTA) map well to food content, which is natively viral on TikTok. Do NOT transfer the funnel-optimization half: it presupposes buying tens of thousands of installs at $200 CPP to power weekly experiments — Sizzle has funding and a global audience; Crave is bootstrapped, single-city (Austin geo-fencing makes broad-targeting advice partially moot — Crave MUST geo-target, contradicting his "stay broad" rule), and its hard $7.99/$39.99 paywall would make his CPP math brutal at Crave's LTV until retention is proven. Also discount the first-1,000-users section entirely: his honest answer was "founder connections and PR," which is survivorship of an ex-Facebook-VP network, not a playbook. Numbers are all self-reported dashboard walk-throughs with no revenue disclosed; treat magnitudes (2x/3x from fixing a >50% drop-off, ~2x attribution undercount) as priors, not facts.
