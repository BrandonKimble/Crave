## Arc

Yiannis is an ML researcher who moved into growth marketing post-COVID, first posting 50–100 TikToks manually himself in French, then scaling a "reposter fleet" model: dozens of warmed-up TikTok accounts run by hired posters, with ALL creative produced centrally (programmatically where possible), never delegated to creators. He now runs this for client apps at claimed nine-figure annual view volume. The episode is a walkthrough of his format-research → mass-production → account-fleet system, interleaved with an extended demo/promotion of SpyTok (an outlier-post research tool the host also sponsors).

## Claims

### Content strategy (organic)

- Claimed scale: "Last year, consolidated, maybe we did 300 million views"; currently "opening 50 to 100 accounts per week, making anywhere from 10 to 15 million views per month" (restated later as "10 to 50 million views per per month" — the range is inconsistent within the transcript; flag as uncertain). This year only ~50M views so far because he's been "holding on a couple apps." (claimed)
- Downloads from last year's 300M views: "I want to say 3 million to 5 million downloads." Attribution explicitly soft — clients run hybrid (paid + organic) and "won't communicate the hybrid results"; one client "pushed... 250 million views one post" (garbled passage — number uncertain, may be $250k spend or 250M views). (claimed, self-flagged as unattributable)
- Core model: "reposters" — hired people who provide warmed-up accounts and post centrally-produced creative from their phones; contrast with "ambassadors" (creators who own accounts and make their own content). Host estimates cost "what, like $20" per reposter post; guest does not correct him. (claimed/secondhand)
- Reposter advantages claimed: spin up new accounts quickly, "approximately the same success rate every time," no dependence on one creator's account (shadowban risk, single-account scale ceiling). (claimed)
- Creative process must stay in-house: founders who delegate creative to UGC teams end up with "seven to 10 people" who are actually creative out of a larger hire pool. "At some point someone has to do the research... We're not artists." (claimed/anecdote)
- Founder should post personally first — "post themselves first... go through the motions" before managing any organic team; "there is no way you're just going to delegate to someone that is not part of your core team and make it a success." (claimed)
- Format research method: separate FORMAT from CONTENT. A "gem" = a format seen viral cross-account/cross-post, "between five and 10 or 20 posts that went viral with the same shot-by-shot type of post." One-off virality is not a recipe. (claimed)
- Outlier metric (SpyTok): posts at "above 200x the median views of the account" — controls for famous-face accounts that go viral on identity, not format. Ignore accounts doing consistent 1M views; hunt the 10k/10k/10k/1M/10k account. (claimed)
- Scalability filter: only pick formats you can mass-produce (slideshows, text overlays, programmatic screen-recordings). Skit/street-interview formats (Cheater Buster) are "very difficult formats to reproduce" — too many variables (actors, setup). His early dating-app work: programmatic Tinder-clone iMessage conversations, screen-recorded by computer, pre-LLM. (first-party process description, claimed results)
- Sounds are load-bearing: TikTok is "sounds-based first"; he curates sounds per angle; "a good 20% or 30 even" of good sounds have persisted for years as spin-offs. (claimed)
- Duration norms by niche: quick 7–9 seconds; 9–15 seconds with build-up when the app mechanic needs explanation; ~30 seconds for full-skit/prank narrative; longer still for talking heads. Health/finance require longer "expensive" content — short bait-and-switch in finance reaches the wrong audience even if viral. (claimed)
- Comment-bait method: pure-engagement viral post (e.g. "trying to save 10K by June... give me your most unhinged side hustles" — 10M views, 1M likes, 5.4K comments) doesn't convert itself, but (a) app can be named in comments/replies, (b) the follow-up posts ride the "ripple effect" to the now-targeted audience with solution-oriented content. Host connects to Tabs Chocolate / "Oliver" precedent. (claimed/secondhand)
- Conversion is a separate layer from virality — his key story: scratch-map walking app; identical creative with text overlay "post-breakup activity" got millions of views, ZERO downloads; changing the overlay to "procrastinating for my exams" (timed to US exam season) "made the app jump in the App Store in several countries." Difference = present-tense intent vs. merely relatable theme. (first-party anecdote, no numbers on the jump)
- View→rank efficiency varies wildly (SpyTok correlation demo): "Bed Outs" French app — posts of 600K/100K/300K/200K views produced only a #117 productivity ranking then vanished; Menace — a 16M-view post produced essentially "nothing" (~170 rank); prank apps get huge views for little movement; whereas some apps jump the App Store off 100K–200K-view outlier posts; "I know people who do a million views on a post and jump top 100." Best case: high-conversion formats where 1M views ≈ what others need 5M for. (secondhand/claimed, shown on tool)
- Early organic movement shows in non-US English-speaking App Stores first — US is "the Super Bowl," hardest to move; multi-country simultaneous rank movement = signature of TikTok organic vs. paid. (claimed)
- Ambassadors (when used): "very strong briefs" with bounded leeway — ~80% of posts must follow the structure, 20% free exploration; adapt content to the creator's persona. Hybrid reposter+ambassador model used "for bigger clients." (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing

- Reverse the build order: validate the format BEFORE building the app. "I wouldn't build my app without trying to go viral first with a high converting format... start with I know this format is viral, I know this format converts." He's doing this now for a claims-app competitor, running research off the incumbent "Claim" profile before the app exists. (first-party, in progress — no outcome yet)
- Pick your niche from what already converts: in finance, only "a couple angles" both go viral and move App Store ranks (claim/settlement finders, budgeting); fit your idea into a proven angle rather than pioneering a new one. Cake-recipe analogy: change the flavor, keep the recipe's structure. (claimed)

### Paid ads

- Channel-choice framing: organic removes dollars but adds founder time/skill cost; paid ads buy speed. "If you have a budget... do a bit of paid ads and figure out if your product is good and converts." Organic suits "impulse type of installs" and novelty-factor products; he's seen founders with low tickets overspend on ads AND founders who wrongly chose reposters without creative-management skills. (claimed/anecdote)
- Host's contrast: Facebook ads winner at 2x ROAS decays to ~20% net margin at 10x spend; an organic hit can be multiplied across reposters at ~$20/post with "no diminishing return... right away." (host speculation/secondhand)

### ASO & app store

- App-rank movement is his ground-truth conversion signal for new apps (rank appearance timed against viral posts); doesn't work for big apps with blended channels ("mixed marketing problem"). (claimed method)

## Contrarian positions

- Never outsource creative to influencers/UGC creators — the opposite of standard "let creators be creative" advice; brief to 80% fixed structure even when using ambassadors.
- Build the audience/format validation BEFORE building the app (reverse launch order).
- Views are nearly worthless as a KPI — he repeatedly shows multi-million-view posts with zero rank movement; intent-in-the-moment wording ("procrastinating for exams" vs "post-breakup") is the conversion lever, not CTAs or app demos per se.
- Virality is an engineering/research discipline ("we're not artists"), reproducible via format decomposition, not creative genius.
- Organic scales better than paid at the margin (no immediate diminishing returns on a proven format) — inverts the usual "paid is the scalable channel" framing.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is the method, not the numbers: format-vs-content decomposition, outlier-vs-account-median research, intent-timed hooks, and the "validate a converting format before/at launch" sequencing are all executable by a solo founder at near-zero cost, and food content is a proven high-engagement TikTok niche. The scratch-map lesson maps directly: "best pasta in Austin ranked by data" (present-tense dining intent) should beat generically relatable food content. But be skeptical of the headline scale: 300M views / 3–5M downloads is self-reported, unattributable by his own admission, and his model favors impulse-install, mass-market, geography-agnostic apps — Crave is single-city, repeat-use utility behind a hard $7.99/mo paywall, so raw view volume in the wrong geography is worthless; TikTok's for-you page can't be geo-fenced to Austin, which undercuts the entire reposter-fleet volume play. The multi-account reposter fleet also carries platform-TOS/ban risk he never mentions and needs ops capacity a solo founder lacks. Take: do the research loop and 1–3 owned accounts with Austin-specific intent hooks; skip the fleet.
