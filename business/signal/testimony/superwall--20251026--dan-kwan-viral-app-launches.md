## Arc

Dan Kwan is a college-influencer-turned-app-marketer who cold-starts consumer apps via viral short-form content: Conch AI (AI homework/detection-bypass tool, ~$2.2M revenue, sold), Arise (anime fitness, ~$50k/mo), and Shepherd (Bible-study Tamagotchi, $75k MRR in 14 days). The interview is a tactics download on his playbook — riding "momentum waves," building/finding a "cult," Frankensteining viral clips, founder-led scrappy UGC — hosted on Superwall's channel as content marketing.

## Claims

### Content strategy (organic)

- Conch lifetime: ~220M views ("around like 220 something million views"), ~$2.2M revenue, "most of that was all organic." (claimed)
- Conch hit ~$1M ARR in ~30 days; did $500k in one month. (claimed; opening montage)
- One Conch video → ~$500k revenue attributed to "a single viral video" (host intro); Dan predicted "at least 10 million views" 30 min after posting. Another cited Conch video: 4M views ("ChatGPT just broke university"); one referenced at 30M views. (claimed)
- Shepherd: ~7 videos → ~20M views in two weeks → 100k+ downloads → $75k MRR in 14 days, "nothing was optimized," app "broke a ton." (claimed)
- Shepherd's most viral videos were on Instagram, on the app's own account (not creator accounts); "much easier to grow an Instagram account from scratch" (his claim, hedged "arguable either way"). (claimed)
- "Frankensteining": find already-viral videos related to your subject, stitch them together — used for both Conch and Shepherd (e.g., podcast clips of Bryce Crawford [name uncertain, auto-caption], Christian-space influencer; hooks sourced by searching the phrase "Bible reading"). (first-party method description)
- UGC realism pillar: lean into one extreme — either obviously-an-ad/high-cringe or so-native-it-feels-like-a-Snap-story; lower quality/frame rate = more realistic; Snap-native filming works; "Snap-style captions" were meta for a while, less now. "Marketing alpha ladder": trends start on Snapchat → Instagram/TikTok → marketers adopt. (opinion/method)
- Us-vs-them positioning: "anything that's going viral online is often outraging one group" — lean into whichever side has the larger/louder emotion. Conch = students vs busy-work/academia; Shepherd = "God-first lamb over the AI-first owl" riding Duolingo-getting-canceled momentum. (method)
- Winning-format discovery for a technical founder: just post about your product on TikTok + Instagram, gather data points; stitch videos found via phrases loosely related to your problem/community; convert responsive influencers into full-time UGC creators running brand-only accounts. Three frameworks = founder-posts, stitching/community conversation, dedicated UGC accounts. (method)
- "The founder should be the biggest influencer" of their app, at least for cold starts; "if you're a founder and you're scared to do any sort of founder-led marketing, then you shouldn't be building your app." Gen Z/Gen A "goes off of vibes," not production quality. (opinion)
- Arise cold start: carousels tapping existing fan demand ("we need the system in real life" posts by Solo Leveling fans), timed to the Solo Leveling season 2 finale — "riding a momentum wave." Every project he's done "stemmed from some sort of momentum wave and an existing cult." (claimed/method)
- Movement math: "building the cult or finding the cult — definitely better to build the cult in terms of LTV." (opinion)
- Current-wave speculation (Oct 2025): freedom-of-speech apps, debate apps, and the Christian revival as live movements; AI-detection-bypass startups "still doing billions of views." (speculation/secondhand)

### Paid ads

- Conch tested paid, "didn't like the margins," dropped it — paid became a passive side stream, organic carried the business. (claimed)
- Rule: "don't touch paid until you've cracked at least five winning formats" organically — otherwise low ROI on your time. (opinion)
- On competitor anime-fitness apps: "most of them were running ads, which... does not generate much revenue and it's probably the worst way to monetize your app" — note this is ad-MONETIZATION (in-app ads as the revenue model), not ad-buying. (opinion)

### UGC & creator deals

## Contrarian positions

- Don't touch paid ads until you have ≥5 proven organic formats (vs. the common "test paid early for signal" advice).
- Ad-supported monetization is "probably the worst way to monetize your app."
- Lower production quality can outperform — deliberately lean into low-fi/Snap-native cringe rather than polish.
- Founder-led content is near-mandatory: if you won't do it, "you shouldn't be building your app."
- Mid-blitz, they voluntarily halted a working viral engine (Shepherd) because hacky UGC conflicted with brand depth and K-factor was the bigger play — against "never stop what's working."
- Instagram (not TikTok) was the highest-virality surface for Shepherd, and it's "easier to grow an Instagram account from scratch."

## Crave transfer

The unit economics here mostly don't transfer: all three apps are impulse, identity/outrage-wave products (cheating on essays, anime power fantasy, faith revival) with global TAM, where one viral video converts a worldwide audience — Crave is a single-city utility whose viral reach is ~99% wasted outside Austin, so the $1-2 CPM meme-page math and "one video → $500k" stories are category- and geo-mismatched, and everything is self-reported by a survivor on a sponsor's channel. What does transfer: the "find the existing cult + momentum wave" frame maps cleanly to Austin food communities (r/austinfood, local food TikTok/IG — a geo-dense cult is arguably BETTER for Crave than a diffuse one); Frankensteining/stitching already-viral local food content and starting arguments ("Austin's most overrated restaurant, per 40k Reddit comments") is a cheap, testable playbook; Instagram-first for a from-scratch account is worth testing; and "founder as biggest influencer" plus "don't touch paid before 5 winning organic formats" fit a no-ad-budget solo launch exactly. His conversion emphasis (long onboarding, no ad monetization, optimize the funnel before scaling traffic) is directionally aligned with Crave's hard paywall, but note none of his apps demonstrably ran a card-required pay-now gate, so his numbers say nothing about that specific mechanic.
