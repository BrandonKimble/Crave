## Claims

### Retention & product

- Day-30 retention arc (claimed, first-party): 0.8% (date-planning MVP "Sparks") → 4.2% (Flame AI ChatGPT wrapper) → ~30% → ~50% (daily-question core + carrot/stick). ~8 months of continuous iteration to get there.
- Benchmark context (secondhand, speaker says "not exactly sure"): X ~30% day-30, Instagram ~35%, TikTok "barely touches roughly 40%" day-30.
- Retention mechanism = BJ Fogg habit framework: every retention event needs a trigger, a daily habit, and carrot + stick. Carrot notification in the morning ("come answer your question"), stick notification later in the day ("you'll lose your streak, your partner will be sad").
- The emotional lever was partner-directed, not self-directed: "You losing the streak was not linked to you losing the streak. It was linked to you making your partner sad." Claims this is why it out-retained solo-use apps.
- Missing-out loop: you cannot see your partner's answer until you answer — a mutual-reveal mechanic that drives both sides back daily.
- Widgets called the discovery-too-late lever: big home/lock-screen widget (e.g. distance-between-partners for long-distance couples) as a standing recall surface; credits Couplejoy as first with the lock-screen widget and copied it, as did most competitors. Top three retention tools cited: daily-question habit loop, notifications, widgets.
- Date-planning app analogy for low retention: like Airbnb, "you book a place to stay, but unless you're traveling next time, you won't come back" — one-shot utility ≠ daily habit.
- Product built FOR TikTok: flows, positioning, loaders and AI animations designed to be visually pleasing on camera (cites Cal AI's scanning feature as the pattern). New app's home screen deliberately obvious: decoder / replier / opener.

### Content strategy (organic)

- Total claimed results: 50M views, 150k users, 100% organic, across roughly 20-30 TikTok accounts for Flame.
- Learning method: 4 hours/day scrolling TikTok for 2 months to internalize the algorithm, hooks, language.
- VSC framework (Viral, Scalable, Convertible):
  - V: content counts as provably viral if it got >100,000 views on an account with <5,000 followers, and is trending across multiple accounts.
  - S: must be recreatable at scale by AI/ops — pushable across 5-10 accounts, multiple times a day; a one-off talking-head video fails this test.
  - C: check the COMMENTS — if people aren't asking "what is the app / where can I get this," it's view-bait, not converting content. He admits he discovered C last and wasted much of the 50M views on non-converting formats.
- View-count diagnostic thresholds (first-party heuristics): <300 views = account problem (shadow-flag/ban), not content; 300-1,000 = content problem, iterate hooks/formats; 1,000-5,000 = keep same format, vary copy until it crosses 100k.
- Format conversion ladder (claimed, views→download): memes & slideshows 0.05-0.2%; reaction + app clip 0.3-0.5% (also stated "0.2 to 0.5"); direct app-demo-as-the-video 0.5-1%. He would never again post his 340k-view meme (also cited as "over 300,000") because the format doesn't convert.
- Watch time > 3-second hook: TikTok favors total watch time more than the initial hook; hook matters but watch time drives views.
- Optimization metric depends on format: app-featuring content → optimize LIKES (like ratio predicts conversion; million-view videos with low like-ratio didn't convert); question slideshows → optimize SHARES + SAVES (mirrors the in-app action of sharing a question with your partner).
- Volume math: ~200 pieces of content/day; at 10 downloads per piece that's "2,000 downloads a week" even if nothing goes viral (his arithmetic — as stated it's inconsistent, 200/day × 10 would be 2,000/day; flagged as garbled/loose).
- AI content pipeline: 10-page "playbooks" (one full working day each to build) fed to AI — Section A product/brand context + target audience; Section B every winning script found in the category (do-not-repeat references); Section C master prompt (system role: "you are a viral TikTok question slideshow copy engine for a couples app called Flame" + output spec incl. slide structure and Nano Banana image prompt). Team generates hundreds of videos/day across accounts.
- Feedback flywheel: track performance on day 2 in a deliberately manual ("ugly") Excel/Google sheet — every piece of content, account status, content type, posts/day, views, likes, daily; 1-hour daily team meeting; winners get folded back into the master prompt ("like a cursor MD file... a TikTok MD file"). Manual tracking is intentional: tools make you lazy; the learning is in why things DON'T work. He edits the master prompt by hand, not via AI, to avoid hallucinated drift.
- AI tools used: Sora extensively, influencer.ai, Nano Banana / Nano Banana Pro (best for realistic lifelike vertical images); VO (Veo, uncertain) consistently flagged as AI content — avoided.
- New-app format being tested (1-2 days old): pull real Reddit "what do I reply to this?" screenshots, generate the reply in-product, show the app doing it — expected 0.5-1% conversion because it's a pure product demo.
- Don't share winning formats/accounts publicly: a format dies once everyone copies it.

### TikTok farming infrastructure (Team, tools & cost structure)

- Scale: couple dozen accounts ("not two, not three"); ~20-30 accounts for Flame; team of 3-4 people total during the grind; team currently holds 10 numbered iPhones (mostly iPhone 8s, smallest/cheapest), 3-4 TikTok accounts per phone.
- Account setup SOP: full factory reset, no leftover fingerprints, fresh Apple ID per phone, paid IP/proxy client per phone to present US IPs from abroad.
- Costly mistake (first-party): reused ONE Apple ID across phones to avoid re-buying the proxy client → TikTok fingerprints Apple IDs and linked all accounts; one flag cascaded to all. Cost "a week of our work time... 40 hours for three people, 120 hours."
- Relocating a Europe-created account to push US traffic "costs a lot" — his safest account stays non-US.
- Account triage: unhealthy account → try recovery → if it doesn't come back, ditch it and restart the setup process; many accounts banned/zero-view during the learning curve.

## Contrarian positions

- Views are an "ego metric": he explicitly disowns his own 340k-view meme; conversion (comments asking "what app is this") is the only test that matters.
- Track content stats MANUALLY in an ugly spreadsheet; analytics tools make you lazy and the gold is in analyzing failures, not wins.
- Total watch time beats the sacred 3-second hook as the ranking driver.
- Don't invent formats — systematically copy, adapt, and scale formats already proven viral on small accounts ("we are operational people, not creative people").
- Build the product's visuals FOR TikTok (loaders, animations) rather than treating content as downstream of product.
- Never share working formats/accounts publicly because copying kills them (while simultaneously selling a playbook — tension he doesn't resolve).
- Distribution before fundraising: a great retention curve without a distribution story gets zero term sheets.

## Crave transfer

The strongest transferable claim is the format-conversion ladder — memes/slideshows 0.05-0.2% vs. straight product demo 0.5-1% — plus the comments-as-conversion-test: for Crave, "here's the actual ranked map of Austin's best birria" demo content is the analog, and listicle-style food slideshows would be the low-converting meme tier.
