## Arc

Cedric is a college student who built Pepai, a peptide/GLP-1 tracking app, starting late January (launched end of February), and claims $100k revenue in its first 70 days. His core move: every successful GLP app was running paid ads only, so he did the opposite — signed essentially every peptide creator in the niche to monthly retainers + affiliate codes before touching ads, then ported the organically-winning videos into Meta ads. The video exists as a Superwall podcast case study to showcase the playbook (and Superwall's paywall tooling).

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Current pricing: **$40/year or $10/month**; founder is "working on making our prices even cheaper if possible." (claimed)
- Paywall is a simple scrollable feature list with yearly and monthly options; constantly price-testing. (claimed)
- Willingness-to-pay logic: users injecting drugs and spending heavily on them find "$30 to $40... for the year... a no-brainer" for cleanliness/safety. (speculation/founder rationale)

### Trial & onboarding

- **3-day free trial on the yearly plan only**; pre-paywall slides frame it as "try Pepai for free so that there's not this big commitment." (claimed)
- **Age-branched onboarding**: ~50–60% of users ("over 50%... more like 60%") are over age 25. Under-25 users are less likely to purchase and more likely to abandon a long onboarding, so under-25s get a very short onboarding; over-25s get a long onboarding emphasizing nutrition/water/sleep tracking plus a personalized nutrition plan (height/weight inputs → recommended calories/protein). Framed as fixing "the onboarding mistake that was killing a meaningful share of his signups before they ever hit the paywall." (claimed)
- Onboarding sells behaviors AROUND the core tracker (diet plan, nutrition) rather than the tracker itself; founder concedes "there's a little bit of convincing them into needing this app."
- Paid-user demographics: mostly 25–40, "young... with some disposable income who are biohacking." (claimed)

### UGC & creator deals

- Strategy thesis: successful GLP apps did ads only, none did influencer marketing — so he "went and found pretty much every peptide creator in the space" and "got every single one of them to post about Pepai." Modeled the system on Cal AI etc. ("Calai, uh Twitter" — second app name garbled/uncertain; likely a different app). (claimed)
- **~100 creators** posting; multiple posts go out every day. (claimed)
- Outreach machine: **100+ DMs/day, 1,000+ outreach messages/week** across TikTok/Instagram/every platform; **4 dedicated outreach hires + 2 marketing managers** (friends he trained) + himself. First DM opens with "paid promo affiliate deal?" to boost open interest. Flow: DM → number → WhatsApp/iMessage → call immediately → deal → contract → code + tracked link → marketing guide (Google Doc of best-performing videos) → creative freedom. Founder personally takes calls with bigger creators. (claimed)
- Attribution: creator codes at signup + AppsFlyer OneLinks ("Appsfly"/"Appsfire" in transcript — AppsFlyer, name garbled) in bio tracking click → download → subscription, iOS/Android routed automatically. (claimed)
- **Link-in-bio residual effect**: creators drive downloads even without posting — one creator's link stayed in bio with **no posts for 2 weeks and still produced "a ton of downloads."** People click creators' bio links from unrelated videos (creators promote their own paid communities/Discords at ~$10–50/mo, per host's paraphrase) and find the app there. (claimed; host's $10/20/50 community pricing is speculation)
- Best-performing format: **slideshows** (informational tips + app CTA on final slide), outperforming talking-head UGC. Example top creator "Ethan": slides like "prioritize protein, water and electrolytes," "work out 3–5x/week," CTA "Pepai makes it easier to track." Comedy creator "Moose": 19-second joke videos, app mention at end, one got **400,000 views**; a brand-account repost got 7,000 views. (claimed)
- Quality gate: first video from each new creator is reviewed in a group chat before posting; some get sent back ("this doesn't really show any value to the app").
- Vetting: engagement (especially comments) over followers; caught creators with **bought views — "120,000 views in an hour and no signups and no comments."** (claimed)
- Relationship-heavy management: texts "every single one of our creators almost every day"; gives creators lifetime app access before deals; creators respond to their audiences' Pepai questions and promote on TikTok Live unpaid. Feature launches are announced to the whole creator roster with the pitch that promoting them yields more affiliate commission.

### Content strategy (organic)

- Influencer marketing is "by far" the #1 channel, ahead of ads. (claimed)
- Videos are mostly informational peptide/GLP content with the app mentioned at the end, not hard shills; creators get creative freedom ("you know your audience the best") guided only by a doc of winners.
- Founder's own early self-made videos "didn't really do much" — formats came from the creators.

### Paid ads

- Ads only started in month 3, deliberately delayed "until we had winners" from organic. Winning TikTok/IG creator videos ported to Meta = "skyrocketing." (claimed)
- Scripted UGC copying other GLP apps' ad formats **failed** ("we lost some money on that"); only proven organic winners worked as ads.
- **Cost per download ~$2.50** vs. a rule-of-thumb ceiling of **$5** ("if your cost per download is under $5, you're usually doing pretty good" — secondhand rule). One AI-generated (ChatGPT, "two minutes") static ad gets **under $2/install**. (claimed)
- Spend: **~$400/day Meta + ~$150/day Apple Search Ads**; goal of **50–100 campaigns running by next month**. (claimed)
- Best ad is a cluttered 8-element static graphic the founder didn't make and doesn't understand ("I think it's way too cluttered... but it's performing extremely well") — made by his ads guy. Static ads outperform expectations; also running video ads from creator winners (posted from own account, not partnership/spark ads).
- **TikTok Spark ads keep getting rejected** (peptide content reads as medical advice); Meta "doesn't care as much" and is where the big GLP apps run. (claimed)
- Marketing spend vs revenue by month: **month 1 spent $10–15k, made $30k; month 2 spent ~$20–25k, made $50k; month 3 spent $15–20k, made $70k so far** (~2x return each month; founder first said $20–30k for month 1 then corrected down — treat ranges as loose). One big new signing cost **over $20,000** (unnamed "really, really big fitness influencer" who was previously the #1 channel for one other app). (claimed)

### ASO & app store

- Ranked **~#250 in Health & Fitness**; believes ads+influencers can push top 100, where "a couple" GLP apps sit doing only Meta ads. (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing

- Timeline: started building late January, launched end of February, $100k by ~70 days post-launch. (claimed)
- Category timing: existing peptide calculator apps had been out for years with "only a couple reviews" and no marketing; claims his app "was a big influence on this trend" (self-crediting — uncertain). GLP trackers already did well; pure-peptide space was empty.
- Bootstrap: no personal capital; raised from a fraternity-adjacent acquaintance with dropshipping money after one pitch call; **investment went 100% to marketing.** Built the app himself first; recruited 2 developers from a university founders club ~1 month in for equity ("a percentage of the company").

### Retention & product

- App: dose tracking, next-dose reminders, **injection-site history** (key safety pain point — don't repeat sites), medication level, cycles (on/off week scheduling — top user request), vial-runout reorder alerts, syringe-units **dose calculator** (lawyer-vetted; "drove a ton of traffic once we were able to add that"), calorie/macro/water tracking, AI meal scan, weight via Apple Health, workout tracker, AI chat box (no medical advice; answers e.g. "can I travel with my GLP?").
- Everything is user-inputted, no medical advice — deliberate legal positioning after public "sounds like a lawsuit" criticism from an earlier Starter Story video.
- Ship cadence: **2–3 updates per week**, claims most of any peptide/GLP app; features killed when unused (AI body scan, blood-work log removed; achievements simplified to streaks).
- Feedback loop: in-app feedback, emails, reviews, IG/TikTok DMs, and walkthrough calls with creators (who are domain experts); new features are fed back to creators as marketing beats.
- Contrarian product stance: rejects "keep it simple" advice — serious use case justifies feature depth.

### Team, tools & cost structure

- Team at month ~4: founder/CEO, **2 developers** (equity, full-time), **2 marketing managers** (trained friends, run creator pipeline end-to-end), **4 outreach-only hires**, a CFO, a product guy, an ads guy. Hiring more marketing managers + another developer in next 1–2 months.
- Tools: AppsFlyer OneLinks (attribution), WhatsApp (creator comms), Google Doc marketing guide, ChatGPT (static ad creative).
- Founder background: self-taught; ran a Fortnite esports team in high school (hundreds of thousands of followers, 30–50 players, clip-compilation YouTube videos getting hundreds of thousands of views) — source of his creator-management instincts. Studied Superwall/app-founder interviews before launch.

## Deal structures

- Standard opener: **1-month trial deal — 4 videos + 4 story posts + link in bio for the month** (one video + one story per week, incl. TikTok stories).
- Rate anchored to views not followers: **~5,000 views/video ≈ $200/month**; scaled up on signup performance; bigger creators up to **$4,000/month**; renegotiated down if unprofitable ("willing to keep working with you but at this lower price"). Good first month → immediate **3-month deal**.
- **Affiliate on top for almost every creator: ~20% commission on subscription sales, ONE-TIME per sale (not recurring monthly)** — credited with creators posting more than contracted.
- No formal view bonuses; discretionary "we make sure to take care of them" for outperformance.
- One outlier signing: **$20,000+** for a major fitness influencer (exclusive-ish; previously one app's #1 channel), posts start next month — acknowledged as a risk.
- Also referenced (from the episode intro): a flash-sale tactic borrowed from e-commerce produced his single biggest revenue day — **details never actually elaborated in the body of the conversation** (flag: teased but not substantiated).

## Contrarian positions

- Do the opposite of category incumbents: the GLP winners were ads-only, so he went influencer-first and delayed all paid spend until organic produced proven creatives.
- Slideshows beat talking-head UGC across his creators.
- Link-in-bio + explicit discount codes convert, against the current UGC fashion of subtle CTAs/no links (works because niche creators carry real trust).
- No view bonuses / no CPM structures — flat retainers + one-time 20% affiliate + genuine relationships beat mechanical performance incentives.
- Reject "keep the app simple": for a serious, high-stakes use case, add depth users ask for and cut what's unused.
- Full creative freedom to creators (guide of winners only, no scripts) — scripted videos failed.
- An objectively "too cluttered" static ad is his best performer — trust the numbers over design taste.

## Crave transfer

The most transferable material is the deal math, not the growth curve: view-anchored retainers ($200/mo at ~5k views, 4 posts + 4 stories + persistent link), one-time ~20% affiliate on top, 1-month trial → 3-month renewal, and OneLink/code attribution are a concrete, cheap template for Austin food creators, whose niche-trust dynamics resemble peptide creators more than generic UGC farms. The link-in-bio residual effect should transfer well — a food creator's bio link earns clicks from every video, matching Crave's repeat-utility positioning. Heavy discounts apply elsewhere: Pepai rode an exploding, near-empty category with anxiety-driven daily-need utility (injection safety), spent $10–25k/month from an outside investor, and this is a single self-reported survivor story on a channel that monetizes the playbook; Crave's local-discovery category is neither empty nor trend-surging, and bootstrapped Crave has no comparable marketing budget. His pricing ($40/yr, 3-day trial on annual only, monthly with no trial) is strikingly close to Crave's $39.99/yr structure — mild convergent validation, not proof. The age-branched onboarding-length insight (impatient younger users abandon before the paywall) is cheap to test and worth stealing.
