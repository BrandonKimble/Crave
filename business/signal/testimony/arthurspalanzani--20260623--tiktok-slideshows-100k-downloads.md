## Arc

Arthur (AppSprint) interviews a Spain-based, marketing-background solo founder ("Alberto") who grew a simple budget-tracking app to 100k downloads and ~$8k/mo across two apps using only organic TikTok slideshows (photo carousels) — no paid ads, no video content, no automation. The episode is a playbook interview: how he picks replicable content formats, engineers "wow-moment" screenshots, runs 6-7 accounts per phone, why he started in Spain instead of the US, and why he prices below market.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Switched to a **hard paywall** on advice from a successful founder acquaintance; before that he was making ~**$20/day** — after the switch "the sales comes crazy." (claimed)
- Current prices: **€4/mo in Spain, $5/mo in the US, $35/yr**. Started with lower prices and tried several. (claimed)
- Frames himself as deliberately below market: typical apps in his view charge **€6-10/mo**. (claimed/speculation)
- Rationale for low price: users download a finance app **to save money**, so asking them for more money is a hard sell — price to the psychology of the niche. Also benchmarked by downloading "tons of apps" in the same niche and pricing relative to competitors. (claimed reasoning)
- Host: "You're probably the guy with the lowest subscriptions I've ever met, but you're also making a lot of money."

### Content strategy (organic)

- Scale claims: **2-5 million TikTok views/month across all accounts** (guest says "between two and five million"; host rounds to "5 million a month"); **100,000 downloads** on the main app hit "a few days ago"; **$8,000/mo across his two main apps**. All organic — **no paid ads, no video content, only slideshows/carousels**. (claimed)
- Core format thesis: pick content **you can post 3x/day**; UGC or produced video is impossible at that cadence for a solo founder, slideshows are not. "You need a content that you can do upload to TikTok three times a day."
- Replication doctrine: when a format goes viral cheaply, "that's the video that you have to replicate over and over and over and over again" — his account is "literally the same format again and again" (host's observation).
- Viral hit rate: roughly **1 in every 6 posts goes viral in Spain** (host's number, guest doesn't dispute); guest later says in Spain **~1 in 10 videos goes viral** vs **~1 in 30-35 in the US** via the VPN phone. (claimed; the 1-in-6 vs 1-in-10 discrepancy is unresolved in the transcript)
- New-account testing protocol: post **5-6 different formats in the first two weeks**; if nothing goes viral, "maybe you have to change your app." If one video clears **~15k views**, that format is a viral candidate. (claimed heuristic)
- Ad-vs-organic balance: "You have to find a way... that the user feel that the user found your app and not in the reverse" — content reads as a genuine tip/discovery, often **using the app without naming it**, so viewers ask for the app in comments.
- **Design the app for the content**: build at least one **"very dramatic" screenshot** — a wow-moment screen (his: pastel-colored, very beautiful finance screens for a female audience) — and think "this screen is the screenshot for my TikTok" while building. His #1 tip to new builders: "build an app thinking of the content that you're going to do," otherwise you get "a very good app but a forgotten one with no downloads."
- Host's adjacent tip: **widgets** are very easy to market on social; his own best apps' wow moments were home-screen widgets, not in-app screens. (host first-party anecdote, no numbers)
- Music/hashtags/description are second-order: match the emotion/audience (no reggaeton on a sad video; girl-targeted hashtags for a girl-targeted app) but "do not spend too much time" — no magic trending song or hashtag. (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing (market selection)

- **Started and still earns mostly in Spain, not the US** — contrarian to the US-obsession he and the host see among founders. (claimed)
- Recommendation: launch organic content in **your own country on your own phone first** (higher viral rate, less frustrating), find the working format there, **then** buy a second phone + always-on VPN to open English-language markets (US, Canada, Australia — "way more value... more purchasing power"). (claimed)
- Believes TikTok detects/penalizes VPN posting — his VPN phone virality is ~3x worse (1/10 Spain vs 1/30-35 US). (speculation on mechanism, claimed on the rates)

### Team, tools & cost structure

- Solo, marketing-background founder; built the app with an AI/no-code tool (captioned "Rock" — name uncertain). Building was the hard part for him, marketing the easy part — inverse of the typical technical founder.
- Multi-account operation: **6-7 TikTok accounts on his main Spain phone** (posts on 5 of them) + separate VPN phone for English markets. Dismisses the "only 2 accounts per phone" folk rule. (claimed)
- Anti-automation stance: does **not** recommend posting via API/automation — "TikTok catch that" it's automatic. His semi-automation: app screenshots prepared in a helper app he built for himself, then **text pasted by hand inside TikTok's native editor using TikTok's native fonts**, deliberately spending time in TikTok's edit zone because "TikTok find that more attractive." (claimed)

## Contrarian positions

- **Slideshows over video/UGC** at a moment when (per the host) everyone on X is talking AI video and UGC — chosen for 3x/day sustainability by one person.
- **Spain-first, not US-first**: home-market organic beats forcing the high-LTV US market early.
- **Price LOW in a save-money niche** (~half the €6-10 norm he cites) rather than maximizing ARPU — while still running a hard paywall.
- **Repetition over novelty**: reposting near-identical formats endlessly instead of fearing repetition.
- **Manual posting over automation/APIs**, and deliberately using TikTok's native editor/fonts to court the algorithm.
- **Build the app around a marketable screenshot**, not features.

## Crave transfer

The strongest transfers: (1) the hard-paywall-unlocked-revenue anecdote is directionally consistent with Crave's already-chosen hard paywall, though $20/day→"crazy" is an anecdote, not a rate; (2) "design at least one dramatic, screenshot-able wow screen" maps directly — Crave's map with ranked-dish pins/scores for a recognizable Austin street could be the slideshow-native asset; (3) home-market-first organic (post as an Austin local, "apps I use in Austin" framing) mirrors his Spain-first logic and fits a one-city launch; (4) the testing protocol (5-6 formats in 2 weeks, ~15k views = signal, 3x/day sustainable-format requirement) is a cheap, concrete experiment design. Do NOT transfer his pricing logic: a €4/mo budget tracker for a global female audience is a different category and psychology than a $7.99/mo local utility with dense proprietary data — his "users want to save money so charge less" rationale doesn't apply, and his $8k/mo is across two apps at unknown margins with zero verification. Also note the geographic mismatch: his whole engine is nationwide/global reach; Crave's audience is one metro, so a "viral" slideshow that reaches mostly non-Austin viewers converts to nothing — format ideas transfer, but Crave needs local-signal content (Austin food spots) where views and addressable users overlap, and hit-rate math (1-in-6/1-in-10) will not.
