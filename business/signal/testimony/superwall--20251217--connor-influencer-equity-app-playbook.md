## Arc

Connor is a 23-year-old CS-trained solo dev who says he makes over $1M/year across 4-5 mostly-private apps. His flagship, Payout (class-action lawsuit discovery), does a claimed $45k/mo almost entirely from influencer distribution. His core inversion: instead of building an app and then paying influencers to promote it, he gives an influencer equity FIRST and builds the app specifically for that influencer's audience — presenting ~5 Figma mockups and letting the influencer pick. The video exists as Superwall-podcast content marketing showcasing tactical consumer-app growth.

## Claims

### UGC & creator deals

- Core playbook: partner with an influencer via EQUITY (not payment), then design the app around their audience. He made ~5 Figma mockups of different app ideas and asked partner Casper "which one of these do you think resonates the most with your audience." (claimed, first-party account)
- Equity partners are more motivated: "if they have equity, they're going to be more motivated and the videos will do better." Also removes per-post negotiation — "post as much as you want... you're both in it together." (claimed/speculation)
- Casper (influencer partner on Payout) has driven "probably around 35,000 clicks to the website, more honestly" — link clicks only, not counting organic installs from the videos. (claimed)
- Best video: 288,000 views on TikTok/IG; same reel got 206,000 views on YouTube; a weaker one got 45,000 views. ~9,400 comments on the best video, but roughly half are the creator's replies, so ~4,000 people got the ManyChat app link. (claimed, read off-screen)
- Outreach: EMAIL, never DM — "there's absolutely no reason to try to DM these larger creators... they get like a million notifications a month." One influencer replied to an April email ~8 months later and the deal is now moving forward. Unmanaged influencers (personal Gmail, no agency) respond at much better rates. YouTube's "view email address" (behind a CAPTCHA) is an underrated way to find creator emails; the CAPTCHA deters bot spam so manual outreach stands out. (claimed)
- It's a volume game — most won't respond, "but if you partner with a creator you're pretty much instantly going to win." (claimed/speculation)
- Influencer selection: look for niche "expert" creators (the mountain-bike example has ~100k IG followers yet drives "tens of thousands of downloads"); assess audience depth via COMMENT QUALITY (specific requests like "do square wheels" vs shallow "wow amazing"; fans asking about family/wedding = deep parasocial audience that converts). (claimed heuristic)
- Payout influencer profile: "life-hacky finance savings" creators (Costco hacks, Prime Day savings, side hustles) — not classic finance content. Casper sources them from his own network. (claimed)
- Secondhand: friend's app Ride Pal launched WITH an Instagram-influencer equity partner: 10,000+ downloads day one, tens of thousands of active users, large share of downloads from pre-launch influencer partners. (secondhand)
- Graduation path: once you have capital, STOP giving equity and pay flat fees instead. His current rates: ~$1,500/video for influencers with 500k–1M followers (a "good rate" via his partner's connections); he'd go to "a couple thousand"; others he knows pay $4,000–5,000/video for creators with 2–3M followers. (claimed, concrete rate card)
- Expect brand-deal videos to underperform the creator's normal views; he prices that in and still prefers "hard integration" (really selling it) over subtle mention. (claimed)
- Dev differentiation to win influencer deals: a portfolio/track record ("here's an influencer I built an app with, here's some numbers") compounds; also raw confidence — "you know it's going to do well and go with that attitude." Many influencers just want an app at all. (claimed)
- For the neglected posture app he'd give away "60–70%" of it to an influencer to run distribution, if that yields even ~$10k/mo vs zero effort from him. (claimed hypothetical)

### Content strategy (organic)

- Cross-post everything: TikTok, Instagram, YouTube Shorts, Facebook video (Facebook = "might as well," auto-cross-post from IG Reels; not a main revenue driver). Instagram outperforms TikTok; YouTube about the same as TikTok but harder to attribute (no ManyChat CTA there); download traffic visibly spiked the day the YouTube upload went live. (claimed)
- CTA mechanic: ManyChat "comment 'claim' and I'll send you the app" + link-in-bio. Attribution via App Store CUSTOM PRODUCT PAGES per link — revenue splits "surprisingly even" between ManyChat-link installs and untracked organic search installs on post days. (claimed, tactically specific)
- Winning video format (Payout): scripted skit — "can I really make $1,000 from your lawsuit?" / "up to $5,000" — walks through the app on-screen, ends with the comment-claim CTA. Connor admits it reads as "a pretty straightforward ad" and didn't personally resonate with him, but it converts for that audience. (claimed)
- Second admired format (Ride Pal video): storytelling founder-hook — stranded on a trail, incumbent app (Trailforks) was outdated, "what if I just made a better ride tracking app" — long problem-space intro, then features. Host+guest both call it a model hybrid of hook + competitor comparison + founder story. (opinion)
- Format research: find formats already working — scroll TikTok/the App Store for trends (host's sponsored plug: SpyTok automates finding viral app-promo TikToks).

### Launch & sequencing

- Ideation framework: "there is an app already out there that works for ANY influencer." Ask the influencer what apps are already sending them brand-deal requests — build one of those. Or scan the App Store top charts + TikTok trends in the niche; you don't need a novel idea unless you're building "a billion dollar app like Strava." (claimed heuristic)
- Niche sizing: avoid super-broad (competing with Kim Kardashian-scale UA budgets in beauty/fashion) and super-niche ("an app that teaches dogs handstands"); aim middle-then-slightly-more-niche. His target band: apps doing $30k–a couple hundred k$/month, deliberately NOT $10M/mo yet — match execution risk to experience. (claimed)
- His own ladder: app 1 (social map, $0) → app 2 ("couple thousand/mo") → next ("10–20k/mo") → Payout ($45k/mo claimed). Not planned; emergent from repeatedly picking "the highest leverage thing." (claimed)
- Anti-sunk-cost: sell or give away stagnating apps. He'd sell the posture app (~$4–5k lifetime revenue) for $10k without hesitation; owners who won't sell dormant 2-year-untouched apps are hoarding. (claimed)
- Bootstrapping over VC: SF/YC founders with no track record should first build something that covers rent and teaches funnels/negotiation, then go big. (opinion)

## Deal structures

- Equity-for-distribution: influencer gets equity in a new app built FOR their audience; percentage for Casper/Payout never stated. Structures vary "depending on their size and how complicated the app is."
- Hypothetical posture-app deal: give an influencer with "a couple million followers" 60–70% equity, reskin the app around them (their photos as the exercise figures), they own distribution.
- Flat-fee rate card (post-capital phase): ~$1,500/video @ 500k–1M followers (discounted via connections); up to "a couple thousand"; market comps $4,000–5,000/video @ 2–3M followers.
- Pre-partnership influencer economics (Casper before the deal): paid-per-post brand deals + affiliate commission link-in-bio — unstable "hamster wheel" income, which is why equity pitches land.

## Contrarian positions

- Build the app AFTER securing the influencer, designed for their specific audience — inverts build-then-promote.
- Give real equity (even 60–70%) to influencers; rejects the classic "never give equity to a promoter, you'll outgrow them" startup advice — explicitly reframed as "building a business, not a unicorn."
- Cold EMAIL beats DMs for large creators; months-later replies are normal and fine.
- Don't invent a new app idea — clone what's already earning in the niche/on the charts.
- Prefer "hard integration" ads over subtle mentions, accepting lower views for higher conversion.
- iOS-only is rational (Android ≤15% of revenue in his experience).
- Deliberately cap ambition ($30k–few-hundred-k/mo apps) to match skill level; bootstrap before ever raising.
- No planning, no calendar — pure short-horizon leverage-maximization.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is the DISTRIBUTION-FIRST posture, not the product playbook: Crave already exists and is a dense, defensible local-data product — the opposite of Connor's thin, 2-week, audience-first tools — so "build the app for the influencer" doesn't apply, but "secure creator distribution before/at launch" does. The strongest concrete imports: cold-email (never DM) Austin food creators, prefer unmanaged niche creators with high comment-quality audiences over follower count; use ManyChat comment-CTAs + App Store custom product pages per creator for attribution; and the rate anchors ($1,500/video @ 500k–1M, $4–5k @ 2–3M) as negotiation baselines. An equity or rev-share deal with one anchor Austin food creator is worth serious consideration given Crave's $0 ad budget — Connor's argument that equity changes creator motivation and posting volume is his most credible claim, though note it comes from a survivor whose one public data point (Payout) is unverified. Category mismatch caution: Payout is a "free money" hook with near-universal appeal and instant gratification; Crave is paid-upfront, single-city, repeat-use utility — expect materially worse comment-to-install and install-to-paid conversion than the numbers implied here, and his silence on paywalls/pricing means the transcript offers zero evidence about Crave's hardest funnel step.
