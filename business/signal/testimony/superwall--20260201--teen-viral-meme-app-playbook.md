## Arc

Rafael Kramer started making no-code meme mobile games at 13 during the pandemic, hit >$100k profit in a single month at 14 off a DaBaby-car-meme game (~1.5-2M downloads in a month, ads-only), founded and sold an influencer-game studio at 17 (incl. a Skibidi Toilet game that did 2-3M downloads in ~2 weeks), then pivoted in 2025 to consumer apps (debloat/looksmaxing exit, AI relationship coach exit). The video exists to showcase "distribution sense" — finding a trend early, building fast, and marketing on TikTok — and to tee up his next "big swing" in therapy/wellness, plus the host's Superwall/Consumer Club funnel.

## Claims

### Content strategy (organic)

- Core playbook: find a meme/trend the day it pops, build a game overnight ("pull an allnighter"), ship to the App Store, then post TikTok dev-logs about building it. Ran this ~50 times. (claimed)
- The DaBaby game: promoted from his own TikTok personal brand with "a couple hundred thousand followers"; ~9 total videos about the game, each ~100k–1M views, one at 4M views (shown on screen). ~300–400k downloads directly from the first few videos; total 1.5–2M downloads in ~a month. (claimed; view counts shown)
- K-factor > TikTok after ignition: middle/high-schoolers sharing at lunch + chart placement did most of the volume. Hit #2 overall on the App Store (his recollection, "I believe"), #1 in racing/games. Chart visibility itself drove curiosity downloads. (claimed, hedged)
- Dev-log format mechanics: AB-tested hooks in early videos ("see what words I would have to say, what frames I would have to show"); the load-bearing factor is the video being about something currently trending, so the algorithm pushes it; audience comments ideas ("add this") and he adds them — participatory loop. (claimed)
- A game is a meme made interactive: "a trend is kind of like 2D… if you can actually interact with it… people will kind of automatically share that itself." Rationale for game-over-content. (claimed)
- Faceless UGC slideshow pages for DBL AI: ~8 accounts, paid on a CPM basis, "not a lot at all" — "basically free installs compared to what we paid them"; creators incentivized to figure out views themselves. (claimed, no rates given)

- New looksmaxing app strategy: partner with operators of hyper-realistic AI influencer TikTok accounts (before/after transformations that are fully AI-generated; some accounts exposed, others still believed real) — DM'd them the day their page launched; also "training a bunch of people to make the same type of videos." ### UGC & creator deals

- Influencer-game studio model: 50/50 splits with influencers on co-created games — "you really have to make them feel like it's their game." Weekly update calls, character updates, involve them in ideas even if not implemented. (claimed)
- Skibidi Toilet creator (DaFuq!?Boom!) reached at ~5M subs (now >50M): ONE YouTube Shorts promo got >50M views and drove 2-3M downloads across iOS+Android in ~2 weeks. (claimed)
- Outreach: tried relationship managers cold-emailing 50-100 emails/day; what actually works is Instagram-profile social proof — mutuals with influencers, decent posts, blue check, "positioning yourself as one of them." Current DM response rate "insane" — DM'd 3 influencers one night, woke to 2 replies. (claimed)
- Vetting > follower count: one partner with 5M followers / 2M views-per-video totally flopped a game. Check the COMMENTS for an actual community (people talking to each other, not flame emojis); check whether the content has an inside joke/universe you can extend into a game/app. Skibidi worked because the audience was invested in a video-only universe they could finally enter. (claimed + one first-person counterexample)
- Warns of influencers charging $5,000 per post whose promos flop to ~zero downloads. (claimed, generic)
- Don't script influencer collabs — pick influencers who can make viral videos, incentivize with ownership, let them figure out views ("they know their audience the best"). Scripts/guidelines are for paid promos only. (claimed)

## Crave transfer

Almost nothing here transfers mechanically: every success is a single-session, zero-retention, trend-surfing product monetized by ads or insecurity paywalls — the exact opposite of Crave's repeat-use local utility with a hard $7.99/mo paywall — and the headline numbers are unverified teenage recollections filtered through a sponsor-driven podcast. The durable, transferable ideas are the distribution-vetting heuristics: judge creators by comment-section community density (do Austin foodies actually answer each other under a creator's posts?) rather than follower counts; give local food creators genuine ownership/involvement so promotion feels like their project (a per-city creator rev-share or retainer with proven-fit micro-creators maps to his "3x ROAS retainer" pattern better than one-off $5k posts); and use comment sections ("where should I eat in Austin" threads) as a live demand gauge, which Crave already does at the data layer via Reddit. His CPM-paid faceless-slideshow farm is worth a cheap test for Crave (dish-ranking listicles are natural slideshow content), but expect his "couple cents per install" arbitrage windows to be irrelevant — those existed for novelty AI-wow apps and die in days. Ignore the paywall psychology (exaggerated-rating sunk cost) and AI-influencer deception outright; both are trust-destroying for a credibility-based product whose entire value proposition is objective data.
