## Arc

Arthur Spalanzani is a French solo app builder with a 5-app portfolio; this video is a follow-up on his second/latest app, a Bible app ("Versy") built in a claimed 3-day process shown in prior videos. The app hit ~$2,443 revenue in 28 days and he attributes the growth to a repeatable ASO playbook — keyword selection by country, localization, screenshot optimization, and Apple Search Ads — which he walks through while demoing his own ASO tool. The video exists both to teach ASO to his audience ("stuck between zero and $5,000/month") and to funnel viewers to his tool and community.

## Claims

### ASO & app store

- Core thesis: ASO is the foundational acquisition layer — "once your ASO base is done, you can then add meta ads, TikTok ads or organic content"; "a lot of developers are just missing out on big opportunity right there." (claimed/opinion)
- Keyword placement hierarchy: most important keywords in app **title** (and at the beginning of the title), second-best in **subtitle**, remainder in **keyword field**. (claimed best-practice)
- Never repeat a keyword across fields — "the App Store will just take the worst one and rank you on this." His tool flags duplicates (example: "Versy appears both in title and subtitle"). (claimed)
- Keywords are country-specific; even between two English-speaking countries stats differ materially. (claimed)
- Difficulty threshold: "What you're trying to look is difficulty under 50, otherwise it's dead. You won't be able to rank without ads or external traffic." (claimed rule-of-thumb, tool-specific score)
- US is "super hard to rank" for Bible keywords (high popularity AND high difficulty); he found **Brazil** as the biggest low-difficulty market for Bible apps — hence "lots of trials in Brazil… it's part of my strategy." (first-party for his app)
- Singular-only keywords: don't target plurals ("prayers") — the App Store auto-pairs the plural form; target "prayer". (claimed)
- Keep only very specific keywords, not broad ones even with great stats — mismatch drives bad reviews which damage ASO. (claimed)
- Reviews influence ASO: good average rating in a country improves ranking there. (claimed)
- Localization multiplies traffic: create locales (translate title, subtitle, AND all screenshots). He shows English, Spanish, Portuguese, Romanian, Polish locales. (first-party that he does it; claimed that it drives traffic)
- **US matrix trick**: besides the US locale, **nine other locales** feed US rankings; adding English keywords to e.g. the Russian (Russia) locale boosts US ranking. Using _different_ English keywords there widens targeting — e.g. "widget" in the Russian locale's _title_ has bigger US impact than "widget" in the US _subtitle_. (claimed; well-known ASO technique, but exact mechanics unverified here)
- Screenshots feed ASO: "Apple will extract the different keywords that are on your screenshots"; words used in top apps' screenshots are usually high-traffic. (claimed — the keyword-extraction-from-screenshots mechanism is disputed in the ASO community; treat as uncertain)
- Screenshot conversion benchmark: App Store analytics conversion rate under **10%** means your screenshots need work. (claimed rule-of-thumb)
- A/B test icon and screenshots via Apple's Product Page Optimization — "you never [are] trying to guess… only your users will tell you if that works." (claimed practice)
- Keyword changes take **1–2 weeks** to show impact; refresh/re-check keywords at least **once a week** because popularity/difficulty scores move. "ASO is just a marathon." (claimed)
- His tool's per-keyword data: popularity, difficulty, top-5 daily downloads estimates, top-5 MRR estimates ("sometimes you can find really good keywords… that have literally no money") — revenue-per-keyword matters more than download vanity metrics ("not… 20,000 downloads, but no revenue"). (tool demo/claimed)

### Launch & sequencing

- **App Store launch boost**: done "perfectly," the first **3 days** after publishing can yield ~**300 downloads** and **15–30 trials** — "but nowadays, it's harder and harder to get it," and you can do everything right and still miss it. (claimed)
- Start with only **2–3 locales**, not 10–20; launch first, then iterate based on observed traffic. Example: unexpected trials from **Korea** → added Korean locale afterward. (first-party)

### Paid ads (Apple Search Ads)

- Apple Search Ads = "a way to cheat your rankings": ranked 25th on "Bible" in the US organically, ads put you at position 1. (claimed)
- **$100 free credits** on Apple Search Ads signup. (claimed, standard offer)
- Campaign structure: (1) **discovery campaign** on broad keywords for ~**1 week**, then (2) **exact campaign** betting on the best performers. (first-party practice)
- Troubleshooting: campaigns take **24–48 hours** to start spending; if not spending, raise target CPA by **10–20% per day** until it spends. (claimed)
- Ads conversion rate: his is currently **70%** ("quite good"); under **50%** means either wrong keywords or bad screenshots (usually screenshots). (first-party number, claimed thresholds)
- Ads double as an experimentation channel for finding keywords and iterating screenshots. (claimed)

## Contrarian positions

- Revenue-per-keyword over download volume: explicitly dismisses download counts as vanity metrics; picks keywords by the MRR of apps ranking on them.
- Deliberately targets a low-competition _country_ (Brazil) rather than fighting the US head-on — most indie advice defaults to US-first.
- Anti-scale-at-launch: launch with 2–3 locales and iterate, against the "set up everything before launch" instinct; frames ASO as explicitly slow (weeks per iteration) versus growth-hack framing.
- Singular-only keyword targeting (plurals auto-paired) — a specific mechanical claim many beginners get backwards.
- Treats Apple Search Ads primarily as a keyword/screenshot experimentation instrument, not a scaling channel.

## Crave transfer

The mechanical ASO hygiene transfers cleanly and cheaply to Crave: title/subtitle/keyword-field hierarchy, no keyword repetition, PPO A/B tests on icon/screenshots, the sub-10% conversion red flag, and Apple Search Ads' $100 credit as a keyword-discovery instrument for Austin-intent terms ("Austin restaurants", "best food Austin", "where to eat"). The strategic core of his playbook, however, does NOT transfer: his edge is arbitraging low-difficulty _countries_ via localization, which is meaningless for a single-city app whose addressable keyword pool is geo-anchored and where "Brazil trials" would be worthless traffic; Crave's ASO ceiling is capped by Austin-specific search volume, so ASO can only be a background layer, not the growth engine it was for a globally localizable Bible app. Scale and model also mismatch — a 3-day lifestyle app with a soft trial funnel at ~$2.4k/mo says nothing about converting a hard, card-required paywall on a data-density local utility. Heavy incentive distortion throughout: the video is a demo for his own ASO tool and community funnel, the revenue figure is the marketing hook, and the tantalizing Instagram claim ("hundreds of thousands of views/day") is pure unsubstantiated teaser. Take the checklists, ignore the ceiling implied.
