## Arc

Three founders aged 17/19/22 built Quittr, a quit-porn app, and claim $250k revenue in the last 30 days roughly 4-5 months after launch, having started with $3k of the marketer's own money ($3k → $37k first month). The video is a podcast breakdown of their playbook: long psychology-driven onboarding into a hard paywall, plus CPM-priced influencer retainers in adjacent niches (religion, fitness, self-improvement), whose videos are then recycled as paid ads. The host uses it partly to pitch his own creator-payments product.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Subscription: $12.99/mo or ~$45/yr; "a lot of people buy a yearly plan," giving upfront cash to "reinvest that into more marketing... it's easy to scale." (claimed)
- HARD paywall, no freemium. They tested free/premium: "the activity on the app dropped significantly. No one used the app when it was free, everyone used it when it was a hard paywall." (first-party test, no numbers)
- Payment framed as commitment device: paying = "I'm doing this to quit"; users with hard paywall "rate our app higher because it's finally working... it was just their mentality."
- Counterfactual claim: "if we removed our onboarding and also removed our hard paywall we wouldn't have had 99% of the revenue... maybe have 10K in revenue" instead of $250k/mo. (speculation, first-party)
- ~15% of downloads convert to paid ("pretty much like 15% of people that download the app convert, so 85% don't"). (claimed)
- Superwall used for paywall A/B tests "on the fly" — pricing, designs, results monitoring. Superwall took their paywall as a public template (without asking). (claimed)

### Trial & onboarding

- Onboarding = the product's revenue engine: 12-page quiz → symptom self-selection ("low drive," etc.) → testimonials page → side-effects-of-porn + benefits-of-quitting page → App Store review ask (pre-use; "select few just click five stars... adds up" — feeds ASO) → personalized "plan card" → hard paywall with name personalization: "hey Alex, it's time to invest in yourself."
- Mixpanel step completion claimed at "99, 99, 98%" per step — "no one's falling off." (claimed, screenshot referenced)
- Deliberately LONG onboarding: "keeping it long because you want to filter out all those people that aren't serious." Sunk-cost framing: gamblers "chasing something back for all the time spent in the casino."
- Never shows features pre-paywall — sells outcome/end-state only; features are learned via marketing content and App Store screenshots.
- Onboarding built by screenshotting every step of top-grossing apps ("making 5 million, 6 million") into Figma and ripping the pattern into their niche. Explicit hypothesis built "off of what Blake and Zach did with Cal [AI] / Umax" (names garbled in captions).
- Onboarding ≡ funnels/landing pages/VSLs — same sales-psychology principles (Grant Cardone analogy).

### UGC & creator deals

- Started with $3k total; turned it into ~$37-40k first month. First deal: paid a religious creator ~$50 for a "consulting call," pitched him on it → "first thousand [dollar] day." Wasted ~$150 on calls that went nowhere. (first-party)
- Internal pricing rule: $2-3 CPM ($2-$3 per 1,000 views) sets the retainer, but the CPM is never disclosed to the creator — pitched as a flat retainer with a view guarantee (e.g., "$1,000 for half a million views," negotiate to $1,250).
- Standard structure: 4 videos/month minimum, cross-posted TikTok + Instagram (4 videos = 8 posts), view guarantee (e.g. 500k views/mo for $1,500), 20% upfront / 80% after contract completion (originally timed to when Apple paid them), keep-posting-until-you-hit-views clause, pro-rata payout if they can't (700k of 1M views → $700 of $1,000).
- Usage rights in every contract: recycle creator videos as paid ads "for as long as our contract with them" — "probably your lowest cost, best performing ad." Some creators charge a premium for usage rights; "generally worth it."
- Viral case: creator "Jeremiah" (~2M followers, posts 5-6x/day, makes content for many apps): $3 per 1,000 views, 1M views/mo guaranteed, plus tiered view bonuses ("couple hundred bucks per million views"); a video hit 9.9M views → $40k revenue in one day, $100k in ~65 hours. Ad-hoc bonus "three to five grand," retainer being bumped next month. (first-party numbers, revenue attribution claimed)
- Scale needed was small: 2-3 influencers to first $100k in 90 days; ~5-6 on retainer in the $250k month (which also included paid ads + the viral hit).
- Top-end creator cost: some creators on ~$8k/month with no view guarantee "because of who they are" and ad-usage value; those get hands-on video direction.
- Killed a ~$10k contract because the creator would only integrate the app 1-2 minutes into the video — refunded and walked. Requirement: SHOW the app in every video; multi-million-view videos with the app only in the description brought "only $1-2k" (caption ambiguous "like, $2,000").
- Affiliate/rev-share doesn't work on iOS: Apple attribution is broken ("you can't know" which creator drove a sale; most people search the App Store organically) — tried ~$2 CPM performance pay; building "view rewards" (retroactive per-1,000-views payment on submitted videos) instead, more for UGC-style creators than influencers-with-agents.
- Outreach machine: Pakistani VA (Omar, found on Fiverr ~4-5 yrs prior) sources creators into Google Sheets/Notion and DMs from the app's VERIFIED account (verification bumps inbox placement — claimed). First words of the DM: "Paid promo," then social proof ("100,000+ users"), keep it short. Never name a price in text — only on a call ("you give them too much space [in messages]... on a call it's very easy to say yes"). Volume game: "first 100 people probably won't respond"; "send DMs to 2,000 influencers... and the next 2,000 change it up."
- Niche selection: creators NOT in anti-porn — religion ("the devil's coming after you... here's a tool called Quittr"), fitness/aggro self-improvement ("low testosterone, no gains"), semen-retention self-help; message tuned to each audience's value system. Ego filter: only creators who can deliver 500k-1M views/mo; small creators won't accept honest CPM math.
- Creator ops tooling: "Groy" [uncertain name, possibly Grow/Growy] platform manages onboarding, contracts, payouts, and view-guarantee tracking; agree.com for contracts; Zapier email automation on contract signature; hired a creator manager.

### Content strategy (organic)

- Product has near-zero native virality ("you're not going to share this product with your friends") — tried refer-a-friend ($5) early, few downloads, dropped it. Taboo/personal category = distribution must be pushed, not shared.
- Alex went viral early doing free Reddit + Twitter marketing while Connor built the influencer engine. Twitter meme-marketing formats (e.g. "5 reasons porn affects your brain" brain-scan images → "download Quittr"; planned troll tweet "Quittr in talks to purchase Pornhub and shut it down").
- Organic (in-house faceless/UGC content) explicitly NOT yet tapped; friends claimed doing "a couple hundred grand a month off just organic." (secondhand)
- Next phase = brand awareness / virality stunts: purple as anti-orange category color, IRL stunts, podcast plugs (Theo Von, ~$50k example framed as awareness risk), church/diocese partnerships.

### Paid ads

- Current: ~$40k/month spend at "three or four ROAS." (claimed, vague)
- Sequencing: influencers first ("the way you can bootstrap"), then paid ads fueled by the influencer videos (usage rights), then organic/brand.

### ASO & app store

- In-onboarding review ask (before use) drives rating volume → ASO. Claimed #1 ranking for "quit porn" keywords; ASO = their only organic acquisition channel.
- Naming: wanted "NoFap" (the movement's name, for SEO) but it's trademarked → "Quittr."

### Retention & product

- Product: streak timer (relapse self-reported, resets; 90 days framed as breakthrough), community feed (the claimed biggest moat — "the community stays forever"), ~20+ tools: panic button (routes to community/journaling; vibration-as-conditioning), breathing exercises, growing-tree visualization (loss aversion), recovery progress bars (not data-backed — "high level for now"), articles/resources page, AI therapist "Melius" [name uncertain] — the "AI" in the title; described as one of the more viral features.
- $100 bounty to users whose feature suggestions get implemented; cancellation flow only collects a feedback reason (no guilt/down-sell yet — Duolingo-style guilt emails to the 85% non-converters explicitly on the roadmap).
- Churn: "I honestly don't think we've looked into it much"; retention claimed "above average for the industry" at 30/60/90/120-day without numbers; "we sell mostly yearly plans so retention doesn't matter, once they pay us we got their money — we just reinvest it." (first-party admission)
- Future authority play: 6-month studies with neurologists demanded by big anti-porn YouTubers as a partnership condition; high-ticket offers and 1:1 support seen as the next tier.
- Stack & cost structure: Swift + ChatGPT-written code ("a lot of it") + Firebase + Figma; Mixpanel; Superwall; VA(s) + a creator manager; no other team. Equity was informal for the first ~45 days.
- Design: all-black "premium product" look, DM Sans (Google font), multiples-of-4 sizing/padding rule; "if it looks scrappy... no one's going to pay."

### Launch & sequencing

- Timeline: idea late Aug/early Sep 2024, product already built by Alex "last week" when pitched; $3k → $37k month one → $100k in first 90 days → $250k in month ~4-5 (single month, biggest ever; explicitly NOT stated as steady-state MRR).
- Market pick: giant taboo pain point, weak competition (Brainbuddy stagnant 14 yrs; Covenant Eyes targets 35+; nothing for Gen Z), "one of the largest conversion rates in the industry" claimed.
- Meta-advice: don't procrastinate, first apps failed, copy what top-grossing apps A/B-tested for you, "stop reinventing the wheel."

## Deal structures

- Standard influencer retainer: internal $2-3 CPM valuation → flat monthly fee with view guarantee; 4 videos/mo posted to both TikTok + IG (8 posts); 20% upfront, 80% on completion; must keep posting until the view guarantee is hit, else pro-rata payout; usage rights for paid ads included (sometimes at a premium).
- Jeremiah deal: $3/1,000 views, 1M guaranteed views/mo, tiered bonuses (~couple hundred $ per million views) + discretionary $3-5k viral bonus.
- Premium creators: ~$8k/mo, no view guarantee, heavy creative control by Quittr.
- Planned "view rewards": retroactive ~$3.50 per 1,000 views on submitted videos (UGC-style creators).
- Early user referral: $5 per referred friend — tried and abandoned.
- Feature-bounty: $100 to users for implemented suggestions.
- First-ever deal: ~$50 paid "consulting call" as the outreach foot-in-the-door.

## Contrarian positions

- Hard paywall with ZERO product access beats freemium/trial — they claim free access killed engagement AND revenue; payment itself is the therapeutic commitment device. Directly against "let users experience value first."
- Never show the product before the paywall; sell only the outcome. Features are "not important."
- Ask for the App Store review BEFORE the user has used the app.
- LONGER onboarding converts better — length is a qualification filter, not friction to minimize.
- Retention doesn't matter (for now) if you sell annual plans and recycle cash into acquisition.
- Views don't matter unless the app is shown in the video; walked away from a $10k contract over integration placement.
- Don't disclose your CPM math to creators; negotiate only by phone; lead every cold DM with "paid promo."

## Crave transfer

The single most transferable claim is the paywall one — and it's also the most category-confounded: Quittr's hard paywall works partly because paying IS the intervention (commitment device for an addiction), a mechanism food discovery does not have; Crave's hard paywall must be justified by demonstrated utility instead, so the "never show the product, sell the outcome, long qualification onboarding" pattern transfers only in weakened form (Crave should probably show the map/data richness, the opposite of Quittr's hide-the-features rule). The influencer mechanics transfer well and are unusually concrete: $2-3 CPM internal pricing, view guarantees with 20% upfront, mandatory in-video app demo, contractual ad-usage rights, "paid promo"-first DMs from a verified account — Austin food creators are a denser, cheaper, more geo-matched pool than national fitness/religion creators, and Crave's zero-ad-budget launch mirrors their $3k bootstrap. Discount heavily: all numbers are self-reported by 4-month survivors during their best month, retention is admittedly unmeasured, the yearly-cash-recycling flywheel assumes churn doesn't bite later, and their ~15% download→paid conversion rides a desperation-level pain point Crave doesn't have. Their "no native virality in a private-need app" finding INVERTS for Crave — food discovery is inherently shareable, so Crave should invest in the share loops Quittr rationally skipped. The ASO note (in-onboarding review ask, #1 on the category keyword) transfers cheaply and is worth testing for "austin food"-class queries, within App Store policy limits.
