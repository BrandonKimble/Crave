## Arc

The guest is a serial consumer-app builder in the Christian/faith niche who has built and sold four apps. Flagship story: PrayScreen — a clone of screen-time blockers (One Sec/Opal/ScreenZen) reskinned for Christians ("pray to unlock your apps") — launched Dec 2024, hit $120k ARR in ~6 months on TikTok UGC + Meta ads, and was acquired within a week of him tweeting it was for sale. The video exists as Superwall podcast content and doubles as a live sales pitch for his next flip (Toxic Traits) and his builder community.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- PrayScreen's install-to-paid conversion "wasn't anything outstanding"; he uses ~2.5% as the illustrative figure. Framing: at 2.5% conversion "you're basically telling 97.5 people to [f---] off." (claimed)
- Because D30 retention was strong, they pivoted to a free tier monetized with ads instead of relying on the paywall: ~5 cents/user/day in ad revenue; ROI on install cost in ~14–15 days; math stated by host: 5c/day × 30 days ≈ $1.50/user against ~50c CPI, on the 60% of users who would otherwise be "completely lost revenue." (claimed, first-party)
- Ad load: 4 ads/day per user (one per prayer — "they have to watch your ad... to do a prayer"). (claimed)
- Advice he gives struggling founders on conversion: if you only show one paywall, "just show another one" — i.e., multiple paywall placements before optimizing anything else. (claimed/advice)

### UGC & creator deals

- Ran 10–12 TikTok accounts for PrayScreen, managed by an agency (agency cost not disclosed). (claimed)
- UGC doctrine: optimize for comments/engagement; deliberately "comment farming" via shock factor — content framed as the opposite of religion to bait "why would you do this?" comments, which later flipped to "I really needed this app." (claimed)
- One video drove ~70% of spend AND ~70% of installs out of 20–30 videos in the same angle; he still can't explain why that one worked. Started organic, then repurposed the same creative as the ad. (claimed)
- Song choice is strategic: "super Christian songs" on TikToks generated their own engagement comments. (claimed)
- Video anatomy: (1) splash screen first (brands the video, skips showing onboarding — show it "half a second"), (2) the core action (the prayer screen), (3) the congratulations/end-result page. Splash-page polish is an explicit growth investment. (claimed)
- Rage-bait formula (Toxic Traits): show an AI verdict on a deliberately AMBIGUOUS screenshot ("you demand respect but you haven't done anything to earn it") so commenters argue both sides — "a masterclass in rage bait." (claimed)
- Content emotion rule: pick the ONE emotion you want the viewer to feel after the video. (claimed/advice)

### Paid ads

Host's intro claims this produced "two cent app installs" (figure appears only in the intro, not in the guest's own account — uncertain). (claimed/secondhand framing)

### Retention & product

- PrayScreen: users praying 4 times/day; 60% day-30 retention "of daily praying four times a day." (claimed — extraordinary figure, no dashboard shown)
- Product thesis: don't invent; find what's already working (One Sec at a claimed $100k/mo for "breathe for 3 seconds") and clone it for an underserved niche you know (Christians). (claimed)
- The blocker's "objectively terrible UX" (forced pause) becomes a retention asset when the pause is replaced with a meaningful ritual (prayer) — users are IN the app, not just interrupted by it. (host+guest synthesis)

### ASO & app store

## Contrarian positions

- Ads > subscriptions when retention is high and conversion is mediocre: he walked away from paywall-led monetization on a consumer app — on a podcast run by a paywall company — because 5c/day × high D30 retention beat 2.5% subscription conversion.
- Build to sell fast, not to hold: optimize for a quick 6-figure exit at below-top price rather than scaling MRR; "MRR flexing" ignores that 12 × MRR × multiple is cash up front.
- Deliberately don't exhaust growth levers (no localization) — the opposite of standard "maximize before exit" advice — because untried levers are what the buyer is buying.
- Cloning is the strategy, not a compromise: copy a proven mechanic into an underserved identity niche instead of inventing.
- A worse UX (forced friction) can be the product, if the friction is replaced with meaning.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is the niche-first-mover + UGC anatomy material: the "splash screen → core action → payoff screen" video structure, the one-emotion rule, comment-bait framing, and the observation that ONE video out of 20–30 can carry 70% of installs — all directly applicable to Crave's organic-only Austin launch, as is the "only listing for the query" ASO window (Crave should own "Austin food ranking"-type queries before clones arrive). The retention math does NOT transfer: PrayScreen is a 4x/day habit ritual with (claimed) 60% D30 retention; Crave is episodic decision-support, so his ads-over-paywall pivot argues the OPPOSITE for Crave — with lower natural frequency, the hard paywall is the right call, and his "show another paywall" quip is the only paywall-relevant advice here. His 39–80c CPIs are a religious-identity-niche Meta artifact and irrelevant to a no-ad-budget launch. Discount everything by heavy survivorship and salesmanship: all numbers are self-reported by someone mid-pitch (selling an app and a community on-air), the 2.5% conversion figure was explicitly illustrative, and the "2 cent installs" line exists only in the host's hype intro. Finally, his build-to-flip philosophy (leave levers untouched, sell at 3 months) is directly opposed to Crave's data-density moat thesis — useful only as a reminder that city-by-city expansion is Crave's own version of "untried levers" if an exit ever matters.
