## Arc

Mauro co-founded Symmetry, a fitness app for Spanish-speaking Gen Z, roughly a year before this interview. After personal-brand YouTube traffic and influencer marketing underdelivered, the team went all-in on an in-house UGC creator program — now ~50 trained creators claimed to have produced nearly 1 billion views, 2 million downloads in 9 months, and $200k/month revenue with zero paid user acquisition. The episode is a playbook interview on Arthur's Tap and Swipe podcast (which exists partly to promote his ASO tool).

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Symmetry uses SOFT paywalls, explicitly not hard paywalls; no prices or trial mechanics disclosed. (claimed)
- Core thesis: "people think they can make more money with a hard paywall and a long onboarding. That's true if you have a shitty product, but they are not thinking big enough. When you have a really good product, that's when you make a lot of money." Cites Tinder, Duolingo, Spotify as the model. (opinion)
- Explicitly rejects dark patterns (hard-to-cancel, trial traps) as small thinking; acknowledges the product-led route is "very hard" and takes years of micro-improvements. (opinion)
- Doesn't optimize onboarding-completion or trial-start rates; primary metric is retention/product quality. (claimed, per host's framing which Mauro accepts)

### UGC & creator deals

- ~50 UGC creators on the roster; nearly 1 billion total views; 2M downloads in 9 months; $200k/mo revenue; zero ad spend on user acquisition. (claimed — headline numbers, round)
- Power-law concentration: "The top five creators, only top five, outperform the whole other 45 creators." A-players hit virality far more frequently; they're "very very very hard to find" and cost more, but worth it. (first-party claimed)
- Bottom-45 are kept because (a) they still generate some views/revenue, (b) they're bets on future improvement, and (c) pay-per-results means idle creators cost ~$0: "if they are in the team, we basically don't have any costs by having them." Non-improving bottom performers get kicked out, and they know it. (claimed)
- Recruiting funnel: initially hired from their own personal-brand audiences; found that hiring app USERS as creators "works really good"; now also running paid ads to recruit creators (the only paid ads they run). Funnel is tracked step-by-step "the same way a B2B SaaS company tracks a lead." (claimed)
- Selection: prior social-media following is irrelevant ("we don't care at all"); they screen for intelligence + determination via an unpaid video challenge series, then a trial period, then team admission + heavy training (most hires have no social media experience). (claimed)
- Most founder energy should go into FINDING creators, not making videos. (opinion)

### Deal structures (pay model — see section below)

- CPM-based pay that is recalculated every month from that month's app revenue/output; claims it's novel ("I haven't seen any other app do it"). Guarantees Symmetry's margin (can't overpay) while creators have NO earnings cap. (first-party claimed)
- Top creators "make 10, 20k per month constantly." (claimed — also a recruiting pitch)
- Evolution: at launch they paid creators "nearly everything the app made"; over time they cut the CPM to fund developers/PMs/UX designers, but simultaneously raised ARPU so creator take-home didn't visibly drop. (claimed)
- Uncapped pay is deliberate: a per-video/per-reel cap would demotivate and reduce output. (opinion)

### Content strategy (organic)

- Sequence: personal brands (some downloads, not recurring/enough volume) → influencer marketing (tested, "wasn't really good") → UGC (hit, doubled down). Hormozi "more, better, new" framing: do more of what works until it stops. (claimed)
- Format process = "exploration and exploitation" (his heuristic-algorithms analogy): exploit a working format hard, but allocate resources to scanning Twitter/TikTok trends for new formats; test at volume — "next week going to do 100 videos in this format and test it." No secret, "it's just hard, and you have to make a lot of volume." (claimed/opinion)
- Rejects pure brand-flooding (Clippers-style awareness plays): brand comes from product quality, so flood social media with videos that CONVERT — "you can also flood social media with videos that convert… why wouldn't you do that?" (opinion; a reversal of his earlier bullishness on brand)
- Starter advice: upload TikToks yourself first to learn the algorithm ("you cannot manage people expecting them to go viral if you don't know how to go viral"); copy other apps' formats and replicate them; validate onboarding/paywall/feedback with your own output; then delegate (even to friends for side money) and scale with urgency. (opinion)

### Paid ads

- Zero paid ads for user acquisition — deliberate. Reason: team already had social-media skill (smaller learning curve to UGC); paid ads is "a completely different skill." (claimed/opinion)
- Exception: paid ads ARE used to recruit UGC creators. (claimed)

### Launch & sequencing (market choice)

- Spain-first, Spanish Gen Z gym-goer ICP — chosen because the app was built for that ICP and their prior channels were Spanish. (claimed)
- Economics of non-US markets: ARPU is lower in Spain/Mexico than the US, but video-production and download costs are also lower, so "profit margins… are very similar to English-speaking countries" — you just need more volume. (claimed, no numbers)
- Market depth is a limiting belief: kept making videos in Spain, kept getting downloads; "the market will not get saturated"; still "plenty of room in Spain." 2026 goal: #1 health & fitness app (in Spain) "without any question." (claimed/speculation)
- Scale plan $1M→$10M ARR: no magic feature; hundreds of A/B tests + double down on the in-app social network (invite friends → referral effect) in Spain/Spanish-speaking countries; once provably #1 there, possibly raise VC to replicate in other markets. (speculation/plan)
- Mentality claims: current revenue $200k/mo (~$2.4M/yr run rate); framed goals as "zero to 1 million ARR" then "10 million ARR" — "we are just going to work until it happens. We know it's going to happen. We just don't know when." (opinion)

### Retention & product

- Marketing-first for most founders: "in 99% of the cases, just market" — downloads validate PMF (retention, churn, refunds, paywall/onboarding conversion); but once you have downloads and a weak product (their own situation early on), pivot to product. "Marketing exists to fund the construction of a good product." (opinion)
- Feedback machinery: Discord community linked from settings (skews to power users); user interviews useful but user SURVEYS more useful — "you can really get a lot of data with like only like 50 surveys" (cites The Mom Test); survey incentive = free pro version for answering ("a big gift, but we are also doing a big ask"); public feature-request wall in settings with upvote/downvote (pattern detection, e.g. missing bench-press exercise). Recommends quantitative + qualitative combined. (claimed)

### Team, tools & cost structure

- Creator payroll is fully variable (pay-per-results CPM), preserving margin by construction. Margin freed by CPM cuts funds developers, PMs, UX designers. (claimed)
- Attribution tooling is admittedly weak: "That's so difficult, I cannot answer that question with like a good answer." Best methods: correlate view spikes with download spikes per viral video, then track those cohorts to pro conversion; in-app "where did you hear about us" survey segmented to TikTok (excluding family/friends). Spike-correlation is also a RED detector: peak of views with no peak of downloads = format not converting. (first-party claimed)

### ASO & app store

- Only via the host's ad reads: App Tweak claims keywords, competitor study, rank tracking by country, metadata editing, price localization, Apple Search Ads management, and (with RevenueCat/Superwall connected) per-keyword trials/subscriptions/revenue/ROAS. No Symmetry ASO claims made. (advertisement)

## Deal structures

- Symmetry↔creator pay: monthly-recalculated CPM (rate derived from that month's app output/revenue), uncapped upside, pay-per-results only (no retainer/base implied — idle creators cost ~nothing). Top creators claimed at $10k–$20k/month "constantly." Historical arc: near-100% revenue pass-through at start → CPM ratcheted down over time, offset by ARPU increases. Unpaid challenge-video test + trial period as the entry gate. No flat fees, exclusivity terms, or bonus tiers disclosed.

## Contrarian positions

- Anti-hard-paywall, anti-onboarding-optimization, anti-dark-patterns — directly against the dominant indie-app X/Twitter playbook (which the host explicitly invokes); retention/product is the metric, not trial-start rate.
- Spanish-speaking markets over the US as a deliberate competitive advantage (lower ARPU offset by lower content/download costs → similar margins).
- Zero paid UA at $200k/mo scale; refuses even the common "spark-ads-on-viral-organic" hybrid.
- Prior follower count is irrelevant when hiring creators; train no-experience people selected for intelligence/determination.
- Monthly revenue-indexed CPM (vs. flat fee, rev-share, or milestone tiers) as the creator pay mechanism.
- Keep 45 underperforming creators rather than hunt only A-players (optionality at zero marginal cost).

## Crave transfer

The most transferable mechanics are the creator-program design, not the headline outcome: pay-per-results/revenue-indexed CPM with no cap (margin-safe by construction, ideal for a bootstrapped budget), hiring your own USERS as creators, unpaid challenge-based screening over follower counts, the top-5-of-50 power law, exploration/exploitation format testing at 100-videos-per-format volume, and view-spike-to-download-spike attribution — all workable for Austin food creators. His anti-hard-paywall thesis directly contradicts Crave's chosen model but shouldn't be over-weighted: Symmetry is a daily-habit fitness+social app for price-sensitive Spanish Gen Z where network effects and long retention curves reward soft monetization, whereas Crave is a paid utility with local data density and no social graph — and Mauro concedes hard paywalls DO make more money absent his scale ambitions ("that's true if you have a shitty product… a good way to start"). The Spain-first logic actually supports Crave's one-city launch: a "small" market is deeper than limiting beliefs suggest, and dominance in one geography is the fundraising/expansion proof point. Discount all magnitudes: single survivor, round self-reported numbers, no verification, and a host selling ASO tooling; fitness UGC (transformation content) is also inherently more viral than restaurant-discovery content, so expect far lower view ceilings per creator.
