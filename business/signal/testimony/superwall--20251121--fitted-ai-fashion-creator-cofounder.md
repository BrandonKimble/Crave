## Arc

Reed Moncada started Fitted 8 years ago as an on-demand college laundry service he scaled to 18 cities "without owning a single washing machine," then pivoted it into an AI closet/outfit app. He recruited a small fashion creator (Max Gomez) as a content co-founder before the product existed, rode ~500M lifetime views and 600k+ downloads off essentially one TikTok account, made ~$150k in subscriptions — and is now removing his hard paywall, chasing market share, and launching a crypto token. The video exists as Superwall podcast content marketing.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- App has made "about 150k from subscriptions" lifetime (claimed). No price points, conversion rates, or subscriber counts given.
- Fitted currently has a HARD paywall, and Reed says it is "hamstringing user growth"; next two updates make the app "entirely free" with "different kind of paywalled features" (freemium pivot). Framed as: stop "fighting over MRR," win market share instead. (claimed strategy, not yet shipped)
- Explicit rationale for going free: "we're not turning away more users that get turned off by a paywall." Note: he says this while at only $150k lifetime revenue on 600k downloads (~$0.25/download) and admits churn is high — the paywall may not be the actual problem.
- Host mid-roll: Superwall's tool aggregates "422 profitable paywall experiments" (paywallexperiments.com); Consumer Club founders build apps "at a median of about a million dollars ARR." (host ad copy, claimed)
- Long-term monetization thesis: mask FitCoin purchases "behind an in-app paywall that consumers are really used to" to "subconsciously onboard" users into crypto. (speculation/strategy)

### UGC & creator deals

- Core playbook (host's summary + Reed's confirmation): find a creator with 1k–50k followers who has viral potential but "isn't expensive yet," pay $20 per video to test, then graduate to equity + rev share as a co-founder — "crawl, walk, run."
- Reed found Max via six friends sending him Max's viral TikTok (an outfit generator Max built for himself in class); reached out on "every single platform imaginable," guessed his email — before Reed had a product.
- Crawl phase: sub-account "Fitted by Max" under the Fitted brand; $20/video, "noncommittal." Max's early videos got 20–30k views with NO product — Reed treated that as demand signal.
- Walk/run: when Max graduated with nothing lined up and went all-in, Reed gave "very meaningful equity" (vested) + a rev share defined as "a percentage of any of the revenue generated on the app" — explicitly to incentivize going viral. Equity ratchets up over time as effort increases ("I'm putting in more work, I want more equity"). No percentages disclosed.
- ~95% of Fitted's distribution came from Max alone; a few other accounts plus cross-posting (TikTok main account repost + Instagram Reels) made up the rest. (claimed)
- Max is far more than an influencer: day-zero team member who lives in the comments/DMs, drives the product roadmap from audience feedback, sits in design meetings. Reed frames this as the fix for the usual failure of influencer marketing (marketers not embedded in product).
- Creator-selection theory: 50–100k-follower creators "know they're an influencer," know their worth, want big payouts/equity; the 1k–50k band is good at content but cheap. "Founder-market fit" matters — Max's favorite thing was getting dressed in the morning.
- Breathwrk (friend's app) ran the same influencer-led playbook: one core creator, iterate to a formula, account grew 0→~3M TikTok followers. (secondhand)
- Believes "there is an app for every creator" and floats a studio model: find creators good at going viral, build an app around them, let them set direction. (speculation)

### Content strategy (organic)

- Headline funnel: 500M+ lifetime views → 600,000+ downloads, "basically one account plus a couple of other creators." Downloads acquired at 1¢–15¢ each (see Paid ads — the mechanic is organic-first, then ad spend on winners).
- Cadence: a video goes viral "about once a month" over the past 1.5 years; playbook = keep finding new content formulas, iterate, cross-post everywhere, put ad dollars only behind proven hits.
- Launch sequence: raised ~$10k "from a homie," flew the team to a Palm Springs apartment for a week; day one a video hit 2M views; waitlist captured 100,000 signups; mobile-app release a few weeks later + one mega-viral video = "a couple hundred thousand downloads off the rip."
- Biggest videos: 136M views on one (host reads on-screen; Reed elsewhere says 135M), replicated format at 60M views. The 135M video's value prop was dead simple: "an outfit delivered to you daily."
- First-ever viral taste (laundry-era, 2020/2021 — he gives both years): an investor posted "the simplest video," a screen-recording site walkthrough, hit ~600k views; brought inbound investors and brand partners but didn't scale for a service business.
- The first big Fitted video was just "how to use the app" — "no gimmick, no sauce" — landing on Max's pre-built, hyper-engaged audience of 50–60k followers; a "couple thousand people" were already excited at launch.
- Content philosophy: hook in a split second; never "download this app" — instead "follow along in our journey"; two buckets = trend/meta-riding vs evergreen; diversify channels for dry algorithm periods; team is "chronically online."
- Feature validation via TikTok BEFORE building: teased features to gauge engagement (e.g., "stalking my boyfriend's closet" video on the Fitted by Sydney account — feature was web-only, video hit, so it's shipping in the next mobile update). Reverse-engineers product from what makes people stop scrolling ("flex your net worth" / "neck worth" angle).

### Paid ads

- Only paid mechanic described: put ad dollars behind organic videos that already went viral ("have really good organic videos, and then you put ads into them"). This is the stated engine behind the 1¢–15¢ per download claim. No spend totals, CPMs, or platform breakdown given.

### Launch & sequencing

- Distribution BEFORE product: paid a creator to post before any product existed; web app first (it "just didn't convert" / "didn't resonate"); paid $1,400 for a 15-minute call with Nikita Bier, whose advice was build the mobile app (transcript stumbles "the 400 $1,400" — $1,400/15-min is the coherent reading, flag as slightly uncertain); six "grueling" months to ship mobile; then viral video → couple hundred thousand downloads.
- Origin: Fitted was a laundry service for 8 years' lore, scaled to 18 cities asset-light before the pivot.
- Current posture: deliberately NOT scaling acquisition yet — "getting it to a million users is not hard"; the constraint is churn/stickiness. Won't call in celebrity favors ("not ready for Kim Kardashian... not ready for Drake") until the app is "in its most dialed spot."

### Retention & product

- Explicit admissions: "I haven't found product market fit yet," "my churn is high," "my app isn't sticky enough," revenue chart is viral-spike-then-decay rather than up-and-to-the-right. Top of funnel is "cracked"; product is not.
- Fixes in flight: free app, frictionless closet-building (receipt-scraping tool, selfie-add, quick add, TaskRabbit humans), better outfit recommendations, AI tools ("talk to your closet"), weather integrations, widgets, shareability ("share your most expensive pieces to Instagram/Twitter").
- Data-moat thesis: 2M+ pieces uploaded; "hundreds of millions of dollars worth of clothes" on platform; knows brands/colors/style/spending habits — positions Fitted as a future "AI resale marketplace." (claimed counts, speculative valuation)

## Contrarian positions

- Distribution before product: validate demand with paid creator content for a product that doesn't exist, then build what the audience responds to; feature roadmap is decided by TikTok engagement tests, inverting build-then-market orthodoxy.
- Hire the creator as a co-founder with equity/rev-share instead of running influencer campaigns — and give them real product/design power, not just posting duties.
- Deliberately abandoning the hard paywall and subscription MRR to chase market share and a data moat — the opposite of the host's (Superwall's) paywall-optimization gospel, ironically.
- Deliberately throttling growth until retention is fixed, despite having near-free acquisition available.
- Crypto token as consumer-app monetization ("mask Fitcoin behind an in-app paywall") — far outside consensus app-growth advice.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is the creator-deal ladder: $20/video tests with 1k–50k-follower creators on a company-owned sub-account, graduating winners to rev-share then equity — that's cheap, testable, and fits Crave's zero-ad-budget Austin launch (a local Austin food creator is the exact analog of Max, and "founder/creator-market fit" — someone whose favorite thing is finding the best dish in town — is a real filter). But heavily discount the outcome numbers: fashion outfit content is universally relatable single-video spectacle with a global audience; a one-city dish-ranking utility caps viral ceiling and makes 500M views / 1–15¢ installs a category-mismatched fantasy, and Reed is a survivor telling his own story with no third-party verification. Most importantly, his paywall lesson runs OPPOSITE to a naive reading: his hard paywall + weak retention produced only ~$0.25 revenue per download and he's now ripping the paywall out — but Crave's thesis is repeat-use utility with real retention, which is precisely the condition Reed says he lacks; his freemium pivot is a symptom of no PMF, not evidence hard paywalls fail for retentive products. Ignore the token material entirely; note the host's Superwall incentive means paywall-related framing on this channel always bends toward "optimize the paywall."
