## Arc

Ryan Thorp built Reflectly (2017, "AI journaling before AI was a thing"), scaled it on a Facebook-ads/micro-influencer-UGC arbitrage 2018-2020, then pivoted to a roll-up: acquired 30-35 apps 2020-2025 (habit tracking, fasting, money tracking, calendars, meditation) and grew them with an industrialized TikTok creator + paid-ads factory — an in-house "university" of ~250 creators feeding a three-tier ad-scaling system with extreme January seasonality. He sold most of the portfolio and now builds Flo, AI "employees" for app operations. The video exists to package his playbook for the Superwall audience.

## Claims

### Paid ads

- Three-tier campaign structure (claimed, core playbook): Level 1 = every video as its own ad set, $10-$20 test each; kill after $5-$10 spend if clearly failing, or judge after 1-2 days / $20-$40 spend. Level 2 = 4-5 surviving videos per ad set at $100-$200, "maybe even $500." Level 3 = 1-2 evergreen winners that "take 90% of your ad spend." Same video can run at all three levels simultaneously. "Most of them… run out of steam after $100."
- Seasonal arbitrage (claimed): spent ~75% of annual ad budget in the first month of the year; CPI in early January is roughly half ("your average price to acquire a download… is half… maybe more") vs rest of year. Rest of year = creative testing to bank winners for next January.
- Day-by-day January calendar (claimed, from walking peers through his ad account): Dec 25 dead; Dec 26-28 start testing at ~$5-10k/day to find "the best 10" creatives; Dec 28-30 conversion quality jumps again, boost; ads perform ~2x on Jan 1-2. Best case = Jan 1 on a Sunday; a Thursday/Friday New Year's (like 2026) mutes the jump — then peak push is the first Sunday (Jan 3-4), "10x the spend" Saturday-night-into-Sunday if metrics signal.
- Weekly cycle (claimed): Sunday/Monday/Tuesday are peak for health/fitness/productivity ads; Wed-Fri weak. Also first/second/third of every month = better subscription conversion in this category.
- Facebook era (2018-2020, claimed): micro-influencer UGC scripts run as Facebook ads converted "like crazy" because incumbents ran polished graphic ads; raw videos (crying babies, bad lighting) won. Reflectly peak ~$3-4M ARR [garbled — uncertain] while spending "the exact same" on ads — net break-even.
- Single-ad spend levels (claimed): a top ad might take ~$100k; a weaker-but-viral format capped at ~$25k in a January period "because we couldn't push it any higher."
- Outlier virality is unexplained even to them (first-hand anecdote): one creator's video hit 66.9M views (host later says "66 billion" — misspeak; on-screen number was 66.9M); replicating the same video across 20/30/40 creators failed; she hit again back-to-back, three videos total. Worked two years running.
- Results claim: Done was the #1 most-downloaded (top free) app in the US App Store on Jan 2-3, two years in a row, ahead of TikTok/Google — driven by concentrated ad spend on those days.

### UGC & creator deals

- Funnel (claimed): posted all videos organically on own brand channels (do.habits, growthbundle); boost with ads once a video breaks ~10k organic views (out of the 0-300-view "jail"); if it takes off, Spark-Ads it from the creator's own account — "four or five different ways to push the same video."
- Creator army: ~2,000 creators "on the books," cycled through ~500-700, ~250 regulars; ~10% of contacts become regulars; 1-2% are repeat "viral sensations."
- Selection (claimed): follower count NOT a priority — optimal 2,000-15/20k followers; "content creators, not influencers" — paying for the ad, not their reach. Screen: (1) test/probation task (day-in-the-life video to guidelines), (2) can they follow the brief, (3) do they deliver on time.
- In-house "university": trained creators on hooks (curiosity/empathetic/reflective), trending sounds (paid someone to compile trending-song lists weekly), and video specs — 10-15 seconds long, 25-27 frame cuts, 2+ shots/second, POV-down phone shots, N app shots per video. Full concept decks per angle (morning/evening routine, wellness, mindset, vlog).
- Team size (claimed): "basically me, a media buyer, and a head of content" ran the whole ad machine.
- Celebrity-voice hack (first-hand, legally gray): a creator used an Emma Chamberlain motivational speech as background audio → 50-60M views → cease-and-desist; they then deliberately repeated with AI voices of Ariana Grande, Beyoncé, Adele — "some took off," throttled to avoid law firms.
- Comments as early virality signal: 10-100 comments early, even at low views, predicts breakout.

### Content strategy (organic)

- Organic posting was primarily the free testing layer for paid, not an end in itself; in quiet/low-budget periods, test organically first, boost only what hits.
- Common winning format: existing TikTok trend/editing style + app interface shown in-hand for a couple of seconds (crossing off habits).
- Speaker's caveat: "everything now is just way more dialed in… hard to come up with unique ideas that haven't already been ran" — the arbitrage windows (FB 2018-20, TikTok 2020-22) closed as creators/brands got savvy.

## Deal structures

- Flat fee per video, NOT performance-only: baseline $30-$40/video low tier; $80-$100/video for proven creators. Rate rises with track record of viral output.
- View bonuses: flat bonus if a video hits ~50k-100k+ views.
- Ad-performance bonuses: a few hundred extra dollars, $500, up to ~$1,000 if a video really scales — discretionary, to keep creators loyal.
- Explicitly contrasts with the now-common CPM/performance-only model; argues upfront flat fee + bonuses buys loyalty and long tenure (some creators stayed years, some became full-time employees).
- Portfolio M&A as a deal structure: bought Done + 5-6 app bundle "undermonetized," recouped purchase price in 12-18 months via paywall/subscription improvements; thesis = buying apps usually beat ad spend for capital allocation, EXCEPT January.
- B2B (Mindfulness App, aspirational/in-progress): corporate contracts "a couple hundred thousand" over 3-5 years; 6-12 month sales cycle; 12-18 month wind-up; one contract can be a quarter of annual revenue; ROI sold via before/after stress-test data (10-30% stress reduction claims).

## Contrarian positions

- Concentrate spend, don't smooth it: 75% of annual budget in one month, 10x-ing spend on a single Sunday — the opposite of platform guidance and typical media-buyer pacing ("Facebook would never do that for you," host notes).
- Pay creators flat + bonus, not performance/CPM-only, against the current market trend.
- Low-follower creators over influencers; follower count is anti-signal above a range.
- Buying existing apps usually beats ad spend as capital allocation.
- Virality is not fully systematizable — even with thousands of videos and frame-count-level analysis, their biggest winners remain unexplained; the factory manages the odds, it doesn't remove the lottery.
- Consumer subscription > mobile gaming now; and new apps face a near-closed distribution game ("you're not going to knock calm… off the top spot") — needs a "new app store" moment for fair entry.

## Crave transfer

Most of this playbook assumes the thing Crave doesn't have: a paid-ads budget and a global lifestyle category with a January demand spike. The seasonal arbitrage, three-tier campaign math, and $20-test economics are paid-media mechanics, largely irrelevant at organic-only launch — though the underlying test-small/kill-fast/concentrate-on-winners logic transfers to which UGC formats Crave doubles down on. The most transferable concrete asset is the creator-sourcing spec: 2k-15k-follower local creators paid $30-100/video flat with view bonuses, tight briefs (10-15s, fast cuts, app-in-hand POV, trending sound), organic-first with comments as the early signal — this maps well to Austin food creators and is cheap enough for a bootstrapper. Discount the scale claims heavily: survivor story, garbled ARR, exited operator now selling growth tooling, and he himself says both arbitrage windows (FB UGC, early TikTok) are closed. His own admission that Reflectly at peak was break-even on ad spend is a quiet warning against equating download charts with a business — Crave's hard-paywall, retention-first model is closer to his Mindfulness App observation (high ARPU, loyal base) than to the download-factory apps.
