## Arc

Nicole built four consumer AI apps in ~2 years starting from her college bedroom with no engineering background: Glam Up (UMAX-style glow-up scanner for women, co-founded with Aaron whose HF0-cohort connection to the UMAX founder seeded the idea — accelerator name garbled as "H 0"/"H0" in captions), Prep AI/Sprout ("Tinder for jobs"), and two newer stealth apps. The Superwall podcast interviews her to extract the repeated playbook: faceless/UGC TikTok virality at industrial scale, referral-code paywall bypass that doubles as branded-keyword SEO on TikTok, and aggressive onboarding/paywall mechanics. She published the playbook free (it was her senior thesis material) and is now advising Sprout while chasing a bigger stealth app.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- Glam Up pricing started at $3/week, later raised to $9/week — the 3x price increase raised conversion rate ("it somehow increased your conversion rate" — host summarizing her playbook doc; she confirms, admits "I have no idea" why; speculates inelastic demand + higher perceived value). Claimed.
- Glam Up shipped weekly and monthly only, no annual plan. Paywall tiers on screen: $8.99 / $10.99 / $12.99 (weekly; tiers = scan allowances, e.g. 2 vs 4 scans/week). Claimed with on-screen paywall.
- Decline flow: hard paywall → user clicks out → immediate 50%-discount paywall at $4.49 / $5.49 / $6.49. Plus a usage-triggered upgrade paywall when weekly scans run out (multiple scan types — color analysis, glow-up guide with 6 makeup guides — drive the upgrade). Claimed.
- Everything blurred behind the paywall; the "scan" shown pre-pay is FAKE — "The scan is completely fake... We only run the API after they pay" to avoid wasting API cost. First-party admission.
- Stealth app: monthly + annual only (no weekly). Raised both prices 20% → conversion went UP and revenue-per-download up; now testing another +20%. Claimed.
- Sprout: weekly + monthly, no annual ("that means you need a year to find a job"); top plan up to $80/month; uses Stripe checkout in-app instead of native App Store IAP. Claimed.
- Sprout paywall trigger: user browses jobs freely; paywall fires on the first right-swipe (the moment of value). Glam Up similarly let users reach the home page and start a scan before blurring results.
- KEY TEST: on the stealth app she A/B tested "let them poke around the app first" vs complete hard paywall immediately after onboarding — hard-paywall-immediately won by a 50% conversion increase. She now recommends founders "completely hard paywall right after the onboarding screens." Claimed, no absolute rates given.
- No absolute conversion rates ever disclosed; Glam Up conversion "during that time is low."

### Trial & onboarding

- Onboarding's real purpose is "priming... to increase conversion," not analytics: beauty-goals/skin-concern questions make users feel they need the app. Sprout improvement: interleave "fun fact" pitch pages between questions (ask "Have you tried other job search apps?" → next page compares Sprout favorably; "What do you want to accomplish?" → "You have great potential to crush your goal"). She calls it "pitching the app in the onboarding."
- Rating hack: onboarding "leave a rating" page triggers the native pop-up; to advance you must tap a button labeled "I rated it" — "we're kind of forcing people to lie if they didn't rate." Her current co-founder was uncomfortable; newest app drops the forced button but keeps the rating ask, "it still works." Also a parallel notification-permission page.
- Referral bypass: results blurred → "view results" (paywall) OR "invite 3 friends" for free unlock. Referrers get it free only after 3 friends install AND enter the code; the invited friend gets no benefit. She judges conversion was NOT materially hurt because getting 3 real installs is hard — "a lot of the people that ended up sharing the code actually also ended up converting."
- The copied referral text is a template containing the app name ("download Glam Up and use my referral code"), so mass code-sharing in TikTok comments ranks the branded keyword — searching "Glam Up" on TikTok surfaces their content ecosystem. ~1.6M total requests on the OneLink attribution/referral endpoint (screenshot; captioned "one link," likely AppsFlyer OneLink — uncertain).

### UGC & creator deals

- Scale: Glam Up ~20 creators at first, grew to 30–40, plus faceless content; she and co-founder each warmed up ~3 accounts personally and found the viral format themselves before scaling ("across all four of my apps, I'm the first one to go viral on all of them"). Sprout: 200+ active creators at peak, >1,000 cycled through total.
- Views: Glam Up "millions of views every single day" at peak; very first faceless slideshow post ~10M views. Sprout: 100M monthly views while she ran UGC; per the Sprout team, 400M views in the last 7 days recently (~400–500M/month average). Secondhand for the recent figure.
- Creator course: Typeform application → CRM; 15-min "vibe check" interview (seriousness filter); modules with quizzes after each (getting started, warm-up playbook, creating content, content bank, content policies, account management). Claim: >1,000 creators onboarded with zero prior experience; >50% finish; course graduates "go viral within the first 2 weeks of starting a brand new account."
- Sourcing: inbound via application forms posted in big influencers' Discords (communities of semi-trained aspiring creators) and on Handshake targeting students who think it's a "social media internship"; plus outbound. Prior experience "a bonus" but unnecessary.
- Content bank run like film production (her brother is a director): per format — idea, details, example videos, approved audios, line-by-line script with per-line emotion directions and shot lists (long/medium/close-up); she films example videos herself.
- Example creator results: one Sprout-dedicated account >100k followers, "millions and millions" of views per video from her first video; another 60–70k followers (recruiter-call skits + talking heads).
- "War is over" faceless format: 6.7-second video (tested 5s vs 6s vs 7s; 6.7s won), spreadsheet-dropdown flips to "accepted"; tilted (never 90°) camera angle, prescribed zoom-in timing/pace; "brought in hundreds of millions of views" and "a lot of conversions" — though attribution from format to installs is admittedly untracked ("We are not sure... it's always hard to track attribution").
- Glam Up format science: identical caption/hashtags/hook across hundreds-to-thousands of posts. On-screen hook exactly "never saw myself with the / right makeup routine 😭😭" (2–3 lines, small text, off-center, "Gen Z Insta girly" styling); below-video caption "I will be unstoppable" + exhausted emoji + fixed hashtags; ~10 approved audios — other audios "just don't go viral." Failed formats before finding it: Latina makeup slideshows, color theory, celebrities, makeup tutorials.
- Source material hack: pulled pretty-girl photos from Pinterest, used ANOTHER app to remove makeup ~10x for contrast, then enhanced — posted "demos," not real users.
- A copycat app cloned Glam Up during its rise; because the copycat wasn't ranking, its viral videos routed searchers to Glam Up: "they just made us 100K in a week."

## Deal structures

- Creator comp = flat rate per post + view-threshold bonuses ("we pay based on every other UGC program out there"). Flat rate $10–$30 per post.
- Bonus option A, flat CPM: 10k views → $10; 100k → $100; 1M → $1,000 (i.e., ~$1 CPM).
- Bonus option B, decreasing CPM (what they use, "works the best"): 10k → $40; 50k → $50; 100k → $80; 1M → $800 (i.e., ~$4 CPM at 10k tapering to ~$0.80 at 1M).
- Positioned to students as an internship (Handshake, influencer Discords) — cheap semi-trained labor supply.
- Big-influencer agency talks (Charli D'Amelio name-dropped by host) and a Sephora-style B2B vision were explored for Glam Up but abandoned; no terms given.

## Contrarian positions

- Raising prices raises conversion: 3x on Glam Up and +20% twice on the stealth app, all with conversion up — against reflexive underpricing advice.
- Hard paywall immediately after onboarding beats "let them experience the product first" by +50% conversion — she explicitly reverses the host's (and her own prior) user-psychology intuition, on the strength of one stealth-app A/B.
- Onboarding is a sales pitch, not data collection — "priming... to increase conversion," directly disputing the analytics framing.
- Fake pre-paywall scan (no inference until payment) — most advice says demonstrate real value pre-paywall; she treats the blur/anticipation as the value.
- Referral unlock doesn't cannibalize revenue because completing 3 referrals is harder than paying.
- Virality is a deterministic production system (fixed captions/audios/angles/durations like 6.7s, trained interchangeable creators), not creator talent or luck.
- Ignore Twitter tactics; the best operators don't post.

## Crave transfer

Category mismatch is the dominant caveat: all three apps are single-session AI-wow or outcome apps monetizing the first-session dopamine spike (blurred scan, job swipe), while Crave is a repeat-use local utility — the fake-scan blur, referral-gated results, and forced-rating mechanics would poison the trust a data-quality product depends on, and her total silence on retention/churn means the playbook says nothing about Crave's core loop. What plausibly transfers: (1) the hard-paywall-immediately-after-onboarding result (+50%, her incentive-aligned host sells exactly this, but it converges with Crave's existing pay-now design and multiple independent testimonies); (2) price-insensitivity — her repeated raise-price-conversion-up experience suggests $7.99/mo is testable upward, not sacred; (3) the creator system as the real asset: founder-first grinds a format on personal accounts until ONE combo goes viral, then scales it with production-grade scripts and $10–$30/post + decreasing-CPM bonuses through students recruited as "interns" — that machinery is category-agnostic and matches Crave's zero-ad-budget constraint, though food-discovery lacks a built-in before/after visual and Austin-local content shrinks the viral ceiling versus her global demographic formats. The branded-keyword-in-referral-template trick (shared text ranks your app name on TikTok search) is cheap and clean enough to lift directly. Discount all magnitudes: every figure is self-reported by a survivor building an audience, on a channel whose sponsor sells paywalls.
