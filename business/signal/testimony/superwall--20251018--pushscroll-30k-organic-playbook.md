## Arc

Alejandro posted a TikTok pitching a not-yet-built app ("do 20 push-ups to unlock 20 minutes of scrolling," MediaPipe skeleton overlay); it went viral, he found Mario on YC co-founder matching (Mario already had a screen-time app to cannibalize), and they shipped an Android MVP in ~2 weeks. After losing ~$2K on influencer deals, they pivoted to a repeatable self-made organic format + ManyChat comment automation and now claim $30K/month pure profit. The video exists to showcase their organic playbook and their Superwall-managed onboarding/paywall stack.

## Claims

### Pricing & paywall

- $30,000 profit last month, all organic (claimed; framed as profit precisely because there's no ad spend).
- 7-day free trial; A/B tested 3-day vs 7-day trial — 7-day "really marked the difference" in how many people got into the app (claimed, dashboard shown).
- Monthly vs weekly price tested — weekly won, but "only a little bit of extra ARPU" (claimed).
- Paywall reach rate ("paywall rate") ~70% of onboarding starters; they consider this suboptimal and want it higher (first-party-ish, dashboard on screen).
- Onboarding-framework rework moved conversion "from like 7% to like 16%" (claimed; metric context loose — appears to be paywall conversion).
- Winning paywall variant = a VIDEO paywall (UGC-style clip starting with a face, showing push-up detection); improvements "not huge" but it was the consistent winner. Principle: "think of your onboarding as a short-form video" — same hook/watch-time laws.
- Abandoned-transaction paywall: if user bails at the paywall, show a second screen at ~"80% off or something" — one of their two biggest A/B tests; their #1 cancellation/non-subscribe cause is price sensitivity (audience ~70% age ≤18).
- ARPU is the north star metric, not paywall rate or conversion in isolation (longer onboarding can cut paywall rate while raising conversion).
- Geo/age cohorts: US cohort ARPU ~$0.70 vs non-US under-18 ~$0.30 — "almost double" US vs EU conversion. They built 3 A/B cohorts (US / under-18 / 18+) because the European skew polluted tests.
- Android vs iOS: Android made them MORE money ("contrary to so much advice") because Europe is Android-heavy — ~3x more Android users at lower per-user conversion, netting equal-or-more revenue; also users "willing to pay more on Android" for reasons unknown (claimed). Their rule: mainly-US audience → iOS-only is fine; European audience → skipping Android forfeits ~50% of revenue.
- Untested idea from Jonathan (ex-Superwall): soft paywall — let users X out and try the app, then hard-paywall exactly when the 15-minute unlocked scroll budget runs out ("highest dopamine" moment). Speculation, not yet run.

### Trial & onboarding

- Onboarding designed on Alex Hormozi's value equation (four levers: perceived likelihood of achievement ↑, time delay ↓, effort/sacrifice ↓ → perceived value ↑); modeled on Clear30's onboarding from the Superwall podcast ("we watched all of them").
- Conversation-style quiz: name, goals (dream outcome), hours wasted (pain), most-addictive apps → screens customized to the chosen app and the opposite of the reported feeling.
- "What have you already tried?" screen names alternative solutions (screen-time limiters etc.) then explains why each fails, with tappable research citations — positions the app as the five-star expert option. Host flags this as unique.
- Micro-commitments: pick workout times; v1 even had "sign your commitment" with the user's actual name.
- Social-proof screens interleaved (advice paid for from Chris, ex-Quittr).
- Let users TRY the core push-up detection before paying (trust: "is this a scam? can it really detect push-ups?") — but blocking stays locked.
- Big drop-off: screen-time + notification permission prompts feel "sketchy" — they front-load research/social proof to buy trust before the permission ask; the drop can be "good for conversion but bad for paywall rate."
- Currently testing a shorter onboarding (current one "a little too long").
- Benchmarks warning: "20% paywall conversion" numbers from App Mafia et al. don't transfer — audience age (theirs ~70% ≤18) and geography dominate.

### UGC & creator deals

- Influencer phase FAILED: ~2,000 cold DMs sent, landed 5–6 influencers, lost ~$2K total. One 38,000-view collab video produced only ~200–500 downloads (they say 300–500). Calisthenics-niche collabs flopped because demoing the app removes the influencers' crazy-skills appeal.
- Deal template they used: flat retainer, no view bonus — price at ~$2–3 CPM on the creator's AVERAGE views (100K avg views → ~$200/video × 4 videos = $800), 50% upfront / 50% after last video.
- US-market entry (they're European): VPN/SIM-card/remote-controlled-US-Android tricks are "sketchy," ~90% shadowban likelihood — even their US-phone remote-control attempt got shadowbanned. Recommended path: pay a US-based student ~$1,000/month for UGC with US-based incentives.
- UGC account warm-up: 3–4 days engaging the algorithm + 3–4 non-promotional niche posts before promoting the app, else shadowban risk.
- Don't go cheap on the first 4–5 creators: $400/mo shows in quality; ~$1,000/mo gets viral-capable videos. They tried AI/mass-creation; quality beat volume for them.
- UGC = scaling tool for a format ALREADY proven organic, not a discovery tool; don't give UGC creators full creative freedom (that's influencer territory).

### Content strategy (organic)

- Pre-product validation: ONE TikTok about a nonexistent app went viral → then build. "Before you build anything... pretend you already have a product." (It did NOT go viral on Instagram — cold account; TikTok needs no warm-up, per them.)
- Launch video (Android): first-week installers get lifetime free premium — ~175,000 views → ~20,000 downloads, a claimed ~10% view-to-download rate vs "normal benchmarks maybe 1% or less." Android got 8,000 downloads in first 3 days (stated in the iOS launch video). iOS launch repeated the video with only a 48-hour lifetime offer (iOS "will pay us more per user"); took 16 days and 7 Apple rejections. Post-offer viewers hit the paywall → first revenue ~$5K claimed, then self-corrected to "maybe 1K–2K."
- Winning format (copied from a competitor, per the Rice-app/Life Reset "find a format, rinse and repeat" doctrine): over-the-shoulder night-time skit ("my bro is doing push-ups at 2 a.m. to scroll reels", female AI voice + skull-emoji caption, uncanny/curiosity hook) → straight into 2–3-sec product demo. First try got only 8K views but lifted baseline; the repeat "blew up."
- Skip rate is THE virality predictor: winning video ~19% skipped in first 3 seconds; target ~18%; ≥30% → change format. 80% of energy goes into the hook. Combine low skip rate with good watch time.
- Full-ad-mode shameless selling out-converted a cinematic non-ad version — problem-aware audiences "don't care if it's an ad."
- Rotate 3–4 formats to beat fatigue; formats then feed influencer/UGC/paid-ads creative.
- Every consumer app needs a visual signature (their skeleton overlay; Cal AI's calorie scan; Quittr's streak/relapse visual).
- ManyChat automation: "comment APP for the link" → auto-DM with direct store link. ~90,000 automated DMs sent; DM link CTR only ~33% but store-listing conversion ~60%; real value = engagement-hacking the algorithm (one video: 6,900 comments; their most viral: 62,000 comments — comment rates ~10% force distribution). Many users skip the link and search the store name — attribution understates.

### Retention & product

- MVP: 2 screens (app-picker + push-up counter), built in ~2 weeks by importing Mario's existing screen-time app + MediaPipe (Google, on-device, no LLM — latency) pose detection; front-facing detection chosen over side view specifically because faces make content/influencer clips work.
- 4,000-person Discord ("3K... or 4K almost") as free UXR: A/B votes on designs/logo, feature suggestions ("repay-the-debt minutes" suggested 50+ times → signal), bug reports, testimonials (user did 300 push-ups; another 8,000), and free UGC. Caveat they state: keep your own vision; vocal users have unrepresentative needs; best for binary A/B questions.
- Working on retention now so the 7-day trial demonstrates value; App Store metrics show retention improving (claimed, vague).

## Deal structures

- Influencer retainer template: flat fee = creator's average views × ~$2–3 CPM (using $2), × number of videos; example $800 for 4 videos on a 100K-avg-view account; 50% upfront, 50% on delivery of last video; no view bonuses. Result: net loss (~$2K across 5–6 creators).
- US UGC creator: ~$1,000/month flat to a US-based student (vs $400/mo = visibly cheap output); no rev-share or per-view terms mentioned.

## Contrarian positions

- Validate with content BEFORE building: make videos about a product that doesn't exist; only build after one goes viral.
- Give the product away free-for-life at launch (week on Android, 48h on iOS) to buy virality + community instead of harvesting launch revenue.
- Android-first / Android-matters: Android out-earned iOS for them (European audience) — against standard "iOS-only" consumer advice.
- Consumers CAN tell you what to build: heavy Discord-driven product development vs the "consumers don't know what they want" school (with the vision caveat).
- Shameless full-ad-mode organic converts better than disguised cinematic content when the audience is problem-aware.
- Fake-ish engagement (ManyChat comment loops) is primarily an algorithm hack, not a conversion channel — DM CTR mediocrity is fine.
- Weekly pricing beat monthly (marginal), on a Gen-Z price-sensitive audience.

## Crave transfer

The transferable core is the method, not the numbers: pre-validate formats with content before spending, find ONE repeatable hook format (skip rate <~20% as the gate), go shameless-ad-mode once the audience is problem-aware, and use comment-automation (ManyChat "comment CRAVE") as an algorithm hack where the DM deep-link doubles as rare attribution — all cheap and compatible with Crave's zero-ad-budget launch. Their influencer deal math ($2–3 CPM flat retainer, 50/50 split, ~$1k/mo for quality UGC over $400 cheapies) is a useful pricing anchor for Austin creator deals, though their own influencer run LOST money — treat it as a floor for negotiation, not a proven channel. Do NOT transfer the conversion economics: PushScroll is a single-visual-gimmick tool with a ~70%-under-18, European, price-sensitive audience, weekly pricing, and a 7-day trial — Crave is a hard-paywall, $7.99/mo, adult-US-local utility, so their $0.30–0.70 ARPU, 10% view-to-download launch spike, and weekly-beats-monthly finding are near-meaningless for Crave; their own point that US adult audiences convert ~2x actually favors Crave's setup. Their inherent-virality thesis cuts against Crave: PushScroll IS a visual spectacle (skeleton overlay, push-ups in the rain); a map of ranked Austin dishes has no equivalent motion hook, so Crave needs a different visual signature (they insist every consumer app needs one) rather than this exact playbook. Finally, discount everything for survivorship and stage: this is one team, one viral seed video, Superwall-hosted, plugging paid communities they belong to — and their "first revenue" figure shrank from $5K to $1–2K when checked live, a good reminder that the headline $30K profit is claimed, not audited.
